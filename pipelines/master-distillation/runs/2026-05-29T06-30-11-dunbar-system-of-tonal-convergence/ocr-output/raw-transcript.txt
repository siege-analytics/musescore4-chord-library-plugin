Ted Dunbar is one of the truly great Guitarist of today but more
than that he is a rare and exceptional human being, consumed with
the desire to share his gifts and talents with others. It is a

joy watching him teach and make music come to life for young people.
His ability to connect music to the rest of what life is about makes
this book much more than just another theory book. It is a book

which is guaranteed to stimulate creativity on many levels.

I believe that it is a must for any musician who is serious about
his craft. This book is one of the first, to my knowledge to really
deal with the basic problems of chord and scale convergence, at

least to such a high degree of specificity.

The book is lucidly written, replete with well chosen examples, scale
charts, and a great deal of verbal explication. Each chapter is

packed with new information as well as different and exciting ways

of using extant information.

The improvisor will find himself confronted with new and challenging
ways of dealing with melody and harmony, and the arranger/composer
will find new and exciting chord voicings, fresh substitutions and
a wealth of raw material with which to infuse his creative work. In
short there is something here for the serious musician of any per-
suasion. However a word to the wise - if you have an aversion to
hard work and an unwillingness to go all out in the pursuit of

excellence this is definitely not the book for you.

Foreword

A single note removed from the chromatic scale has a relation-
ship to every remaining tone. If the tone represents a tonality
or key center, all remaining intervals chords and scales have a
certain type of converginal relationship in a cadential motion
sense to this key center. Each remaining tone is a constituent of
convergent scales and their chords that are embodied in the chromatic
scale. In my convergence system I use 24 scales on various tonali-
ties and of various colors (Blues, diminish, augmented, pentatonic,
angular, descending, spanish, modal & dorian Jazz minor), to
‘illustrate the many possibilities of use in composing, improvising
solos & arranging.

Each scale may be used as raw material for convergent ideas
within a piece of music as well as many chordal sounds extracted
from them,

These scales & their chords may be combined (several of them
together) in several rotas varying their order and making more
interesting musical usuage of color (A,—»; +, 0, 7th). The
important factor is the expanding of hearing tolerance increasing
the musicians ear awareness to the entire tonal resources of the
chromatic scale.

The usuage of these scales in turnbacks, cadences and cycles
(which comprise all music) in Jazz yields a wider source of ideas

for the improvisor-composer-arranger.
Setting the Mood

A tonal gravity law is created once you start to play music
(in a key). The key of the music exerts a gravity pull on all
other chords no matter what they may be. The energy amassed in
a jazz improvisors solo has to be climatically released by
arrival "Home" to the overall key center. The weight of this
released energy causes a last moment of furious groping for
approachment to finality through various formulas created under
pressure.

This process is equivalent to the twelfth house in astrology
where the ( 1 yr. ) cycle of human experience comes to a close, a
new seed hopefully is consolidated into a new cycle of growth.

Mr. Dane Rudhyar in his poetic book on The Astrological Houses

states "the art of bringing every experience to a creative end is
the greatest of all arts.

Everything that came before may be largely forgotten but such
a significant end will be unforgettable."

It is this "significant end" to which I have compiled this
convergence system. To end musically a major cadence or lesser
tonal cadence with variety of scale color & their constituent chords,
variety of plane for an unforgettable conscious end, wherever that
end may lie.

Many air planes approach New York from many convergent cities
in the world. A king walks down the aisle and converges on his
throne. Wine is a convergent pre-dinner beverage. Calisthenics
are pre-game convergent exercises to football players. Bb7 is a

standard V convergent chord to EP minor & major tonalities as well

as many other chords and their parent scales in this convergent

system.

The Effect the Lydian Concept Had On Me

I was exposed to the Lydian Concept around 1963 by David
Baker. This viewpoint of the chromatic scale opened my hearing
to the point where every interval in music became useful. All
chords intervals or scales became useful to me in any tune I
played. My hearing education grew consciously where the entire
Creator given chromatic fabric could be woven into musical fashions
of any design (with taste). I immediately experimented with the
scales of the concept noticing the sound "color" that David pulled
my coat to. Major, minor, augmented, dominant & diminish became
like the green, blue, black, red & yellow of the color spectrum
showing a manifestation and parallelism of two art forms. This,
from listening to John Coltrane, Yusef Lateef and others caused
me to try using many different scale "color" types on one chord
(7th). The 7th chord having the all important tritone host the
largest and most flexible of all the chord types. (7th bs, 7b 9,
7th #5, 7th #9, 7th 9s, ren bg bs ect). This in itself showed
many interval colors within one chord.

I noticed the closing cadence of a tune where all the prior
music that came before subjugated itself for the arrival "home" to
the Great Tonality, the door closing on that octave. It was at
this final juncture (II-V) where my convergence system was conceived,
noticing all the chord possibilities on the final 5th chord in the
cadence.

I remember the recordings where Miles Davis used tags (double
II-V's) and listened to Herbie Hancock create new harmonic pallettes

for these turnbacks. This created the emotional stimulus for me

also showing a molecule of an event to look into.

The Lydian Concept opened an organized world of substitution
based on the connector tritone and permitted color type substitu-
tion along with increased use of "chance taking" accompanied with
its supportive rhythmic energy.

I learned how to prolong tension by being an improvisor,
composer, conductor at the same time remembering the polar law of
contrast, (Balance of length of tension & release).

The Lydian Concept made me aware of planes- Vertical &

(chord)
Horizontal showing geometry & music. I saw & see the chromatic
(melody)
universe as being as manifest as our atmospheres and all atmospheres
waiting to be shaped into all types of musical forms. The horizontal
and the vertical made me think of other geometric musical planes
to experiment with.

The Lydian Concept exposed me to greater & lesser tonalities
in which all became humble under the great tonality of the ''TONEL
ABSOLU". This convergence system shows a fixed bombardment of
any type chord that we want to give "tonal significance" to then
bombard it with 24 scales, a mixture of them, their ideas and
constituent chords.

This bombardment process (or convergence) has more usuage on
the "tritone containing" 7th chord and its big family of chords.
All music has this dominant quality for built up energy tension for
release. The major and minor qualities represent relaxedness.

This convergence system is one of increasing your technique
to meet the emergency of fleeting tempo to meet tonal deadlines
on time.

Thank you God, Gurdjieff, George Russell, David Baker, Joyce,

Mother, Dad and many many more.

Conceiving this Convergence System (Scales & their Chords)

Conceiving this Convergence system

The 2-5 cadence is one of the most regulary occuring devices
in music. It has participated in music in assuming main tonalities
and lesser tonalities & to propel musical motion. The central in-
terval of harmonic signficance of the 2-5 cadence has been the
"mysterious" tritone interval.

The approach to a major or minor tonality has always caused
the improvising (Jazz) musician to think about different ways of
approaching the final major or minor chord in order to make more

music. Paul Hindeminth states in his book the Craft of Musical

Composition ''The extent to which every element in the cadence sub-
ordinates itself to the structural drive towards finality is shown
by the fact that in the chords making up a cadence even the highest
laws of clean writing are often disobeyed, and consecutive parallels
both open & covered, ugly melodic leaps, chromatic slides, and other
devices which would ordinarily be used only with greatest reserve
and only for particular expressive effects are employed without
hesitation."

The master players in Jazz have always given special signifi-
cance to turnbacks (which is basically a double 2-5 sequence)
because this gave them the opportunity to approach finality in
different ways. I have had Wes Montgomery mention to me about
the fact that most times the meat of the improvisation is in the
turnbacks in tunes.

This system I conceived while playing Stella Bye Starlight

and using a one chord turnback on the final V chord found that
many chords on various roots not necessary in the overall key of
the music could act as a cadence or convergent chord into the last
chord (Key of the tune).

Using the 5th as the pedal root of most of these chords and
placing different triads ect on top ( Absa, In the process of
finding these chords I found the parent scales of them and this
convergence system was born.

Each chord represents a member chord sound of one of the modes
of the scale it comes from.

The connection of these 24 scales in many permutative ways
permits a varying amount of approaches to finality of tonality.

On the following pages are examples of the actual method of
conceiving my method of convergence.

At every moment in life we are converging or keying on other
moments of lesser or greater signficance (as with tonalities of

greater & lesser signficance in music).

I conceived this convergence system by using a one chord tag
at the very end of "Stella Bye Starlite." The first chord (numbered)
of each of the following group of 43 chords & scales yield a different
type V chord (function) and its parent scale, yielding several con-
vergent scales & their chords for tonal convergence.

This process worked out around the keys gives a complete
system of "closing end," "Bombarding" or "Converging" on a tonality
chord that has significance (in other words a chord change that
last a few measures or has some lesser importance than the overall

key of the music) as well as the overall key of the music.
