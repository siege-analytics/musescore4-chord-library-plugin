## Moving tenths swing piano device

> Moving tenths in the left hand was one of the primary accompanying devices of pianists in the
> swing era. Fats Waller, Willie “The Lion” Smith, Art Tatum, Teddy Wilson, and many others made
> this the style until bebop came along. Within reason, you can emulate it on the guitar. The pianists
> mentioned often kept almost constant quarter-note moving tenths going with the melody. You
> should be able to manage at least half notes.

**Significance (p. 127).** Defines moving-tenths device with historical source and adaptation target.

## Closer voicing fourths and tritones

> This variation uses the same chords except the accompaniment is voiced so that the harmony
> moves in alternating pairs of fourths and tritones. The bottom note in each pair alternates between
> major or minor seventh to major third. There is no explicitly stated root or fifth of the chord. If you play
> in a group, the bassist will cover roots and fifths.

**Significance (p. 129).** Rootless close-voicing system using fourths and tritones.
