# Chapter 2 — Section One/Part 2: Learning the Blues: Fretted Notes, Rests

_Distillation; backed by `chapters/ch02.md`._

Chapter 2 teaches the rhythmic and notational foundations of jazz guitar, introducing dotted notes (which extend a note's duration by half its value) and the distinction between dots and ties based on readability, while establishing the two-part fingerstyle technique combining rest and free strokes across melody and bass. The chapter's characteristic moves include defining jazz's structural architecture as a quarter-note pulse supporting eighth-note melodies, and crucially establishing the swing/shuffle feel—the long-short interpretation of eighth notes that defines idiomatic jazz and blues—applied strategically at slower to medium tempos. Load-bearing terminology includes the dotted note extension rule, the 1-and-2-and counting system for eighth notes, the swing or shuffle feel, and the notational principle of organizing 4/4 measures as 2/4+2/4 for clarity; the chapter also teaches self-monitoring through counting aloud to catch rhythmic errors and specifies that ties (not dots) must be used when extending notes across measure lines.
