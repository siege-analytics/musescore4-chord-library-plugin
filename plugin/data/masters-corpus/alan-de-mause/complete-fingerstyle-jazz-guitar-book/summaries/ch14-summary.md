# Chapter 14 — Section Three/Part 6: Intros, Endings, Turnarounds, Tags & Modulations

_Distillation; backed by `chapters/ch14.md`._

Chapter 14 teaches the harmonic architecture of jazz song structures: intros must end on V7 or a V7 substitute to lead into the tune, while endings employ V7-I, IV-I, or II-V7-I progressions, with the practical shortcut of reusing the song's final measures as an intro. The master reveals characteristic techniques like chromatic motion, cycle-of-fifths movement, whole-tone color, and low pedal tones in intro construction, and introduces the vamp as a repeated setup device requiring active listening to the soloist's cue. Turnarounds are functionally equivalent to two-measure intros, using the same V7-gateway principle to set up each successive chorus, while tags (codas) are triggered by the cue Tag! and initiated by replacing the final I chord with V7. For modulations, the chapter establishes three methods with increasing sophistication: the two-beat V7-I substitution for instant key changes, back-cycling through the cycle of fifths, and the II-V7-I progression as the most idiomatic jazz bridge between keys. Load-bearing terms include vamp, turnaround, tag, modulation, and the II-V7 unit as the core harmonic vocabulary for connecting song sections.
