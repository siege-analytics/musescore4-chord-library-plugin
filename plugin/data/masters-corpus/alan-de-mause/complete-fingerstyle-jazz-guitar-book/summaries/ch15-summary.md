# Chapter 15 — Section Three/Part 7: Fun Jazz

_Distillation; backed by `chapters/ch15.md`._

Chapter 15 teaches fingerstyle jazz arrangement through nine pieces, grounded in the aesthetic principle of implying harmonic richness with sparse voicings while prioritizing melody. The author introduces key harmonic devices—descending chromatic key changes, half-step approach chords, diatonic cycle-of-fifths movement, deceptive cadences, and the tritone accompaniment technique—alongside specific rhythmic concepts like triplet feel, double-time feel within ballads, and polymetric 3-on-4 phrasing. A signature technique throughout is the walkdown, defined as a descending bass line with a common tone over a chord stack, which serves as the structural hook in several pieces. The chapter also teaches a transferable method for converting single-line melodies into self-accompanied fingerstyle arrangements, and demonstrates how sustained eighth-note melodic flow (attributed to Joe Pass) maintains rhythmic momentum despite sparse bass notes. Throughout, the author's characteristic move is stylistic fusion—layering bebop vocabulary over stride piano imitation, for instance—balanced by an overarching principle that whatever sounds good to the ear will work, tempered only by community musical standards.
