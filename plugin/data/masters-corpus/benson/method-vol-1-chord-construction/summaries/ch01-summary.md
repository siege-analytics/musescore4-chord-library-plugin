# Chapter 1 — Staff, Clefs & Musical Notes

_Distillation; backed by `chapters/ch01.md`._

Chapter 1 is foundational and introductory rather than guitar-technique instruction, laying out the notation vocabulary the rest of the book depends on. It defines the musical interval and its half-step/whole-step units (Interval definition), then establishes that Western music uses 12 pitches built from 7 natural notes mapped from solfege to letters (Do=C through Ti=B) with accidentals supplying the remaining five (Natural notes and letters, Accidentals derive remaining notes). It introduces the staff as five lines and four spaces and explains the clef as the symbol that anchors note names to those positions (Staff definition, Clef purpose), then walks through the Treble/G clef on line 2 used for guitar, the Bass/F clef on line 4, and the C clef on line 3, with a reference table of line/space notes for each (Treble (G) clef, Bass (F) clef, C clef, G clef line/space notes). It enumerates the ten clef-position variants while noting that popular-music guitarists work mainly with treble and bass (Clef subdivisions and positions, Popular-music clef usage), and closes by defining the grand staff and the term system used in piano/harp scores (Grand Staff). No master's stylistic moves appear here; the chapter is pure notation literacy.
