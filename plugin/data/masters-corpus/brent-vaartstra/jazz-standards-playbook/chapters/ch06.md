## VI7 as song opener

> Sweet Georgia Brown starts on the VI7. |t wouldn’t seem like such a
> surprising way to start if it was a minor 7 chord (meaning it would be
> the relative minor). However, the vi minor is converted into a VI domi-
> nant.

**Significance (p. 95).** Explains the defining harmonic feature of the tune — the non-diatonic conversion of vi to VI7 — which shapes improvisation and reharmonization choices throughout.

## diatonic minor to dominant conversion

> just
> know that it is entirely normal and acceptable to turn minor chords in
> the diatonic series into dominant 7“ chords.

**Significance (p. 95).** States the permissive rule that underpins the entire harmonic analysis of this song and recurs across multiple jazz standards in the method.

## cycle of dominant 7ths

> bars 1-13 are just a long cycle of dominant 7* chords resolving to the |
> chord.

**Significance (p. 95).** Gives the macro structural summary of the A-section that a player must internalize before attempting to improvise over it.

## descending chromatic approach

> Don't let this section throw you either. Understand we are just dealing
> with a series of chromatically descending dominant 7“ chords that are
> targeting the VI7 chord. From there the VI7 logically concludes to a ii-
> V-| to the parent tonic.

**Significance (p. 97).** Names and demystifies the chromatic turnaround device so students can recognize and navigate it in other standards.

## backdoor I7-VI7-ii-V-I

> You can simplify your understanding of this section by noting that it
> starts on the I7 and is targeting the VI7. You can think of bars 29-32
> as a “backdoor” |7-VI7-ii-V-I.

**Significance (p. 97).** Offers a reductive label ('backdoor') that makes the chromatic section memorable and transferable to other tunes.

## Mixolydian scale definition

> The Mixolydian scale is the 5th mode of the major scale.

**Significance (p. 98).** Provides the foundational modal definition students need before applying the scale as an improvisational tool over dominant 7 chords.

## Mixolydian as major with b7

> The easiest way to think of a Mixolydian scale is a major scale with a
> b7. Or you can think of it as starting and ending a major scale on its
> 5* tone.
>
> Formula: 1-2-3-4-5-6-b7

**Significance (p. 98).** Gives two practical shortcuts plus the formula for memorizing and constructing the Mixolydian scale on any root.

## scales as pitch collections

> The
> best way to think about the use of scales is “pitch collections.” They
> aren’t notes that are meant to be played in order, nor are they to be
> used solely to improvise. They are simply pitch collections which map
> out the note choices you can use to make melodies over a particular
> chord.

**Significance (p. 99).** Reframes scales from linear finger exercises to harmonic resource pools — a conceptual shift central to the method's improvisational philosophy.

## chord-tone pattern exercise

> Note that each bar starts on a different chord tone of the given chord.
> The first and last lines begin with quarter note chord tones moving in
> ascending order: R-3'*-5*-b7. The second line descends in this order:
> R-b7-5°%-3°¢.

**Significance (p. 99).** Specifies the exact structural logic of the Mixolydian exercise so students can replicate and extend it to other chord changes.

## practicing licks in 12 keys

> | always suggest taking things through all 12 keys. It’s a powerful
> practice that not only helps you internalize musical information, but it
> also enables you to become stronger in keys you may be weaker at.

**Significance (p. 101).** Articulates the method's core practice rule for lick internalization, applicable beyond this chapter to every improvisation lesson in the book.

## relative minor key area

> Starting in the second half of bar 24 we begin to transition into the
> relative minor key. What's the relative minor to Ab major? F minor.

**Significance (p. 96).** Marks the structural pivot to the relative minor, giving students the roadmap they need to switch scale and chord-tone choices mid-chorus.
