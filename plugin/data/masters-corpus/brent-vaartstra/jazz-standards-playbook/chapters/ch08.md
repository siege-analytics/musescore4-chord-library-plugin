## bIImaj7 as tritone/chromatic sub

> The easiest way to think of this chord is a substitute for the V chord. In
> other words, G7 is being replaced by a Domaj7. So really, bars 5-7 are
> a ii-V-I with a replacement for the V chord.
>
> Sometimes this is called a tritone substitution, meaning, a chord is
> being substituted by another chord with a root a tritone interval away.
> In most cases, a tritone substitution replaces one dominant 7 chord for
> another dominant 7. This is clearly not happening here, as the bllmaj7
> has a major quality.
>
> Another way to think of this chord is as a simple chromatic passing
> chord between the ii and | chord.

**Significance (p. 122).** Defines tritone substitution, distinguishes it from the chromatic-passing-chord interpretation, and gives the player two analytical frameworks for the same chord.
