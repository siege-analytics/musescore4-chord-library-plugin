# Chapter 1 — Before We Start: The Pro Guide to Learning Jazz Standards

_Distillation; backed by `chapters/ch01.md`._

Chapter 1 establishes ear-first learning as the foundational pedagogical method for jazz standards, introducing the LIST process (Listen, Internalize, Sing, Transfer) as the book's central framework for aural acquisition. The chapter teaches that jazz musicians must prioritize listening to multiple recordings, practicing intentional undivided attention, singing melodies and bass lines as proof of internalization, and then transferring that knowledge to the instrument through melody first, followed by chord learning via root motion and quality identification. Key techniques include learning vocal versions and lyrics to deepen melodic memory, using Frank Sinatra as a reference for straight melody renditions, and isolating bass notes to train harmonic ear training. The master's characteristic move is reframing sheet music not as a primary learning tool but as a checking mechanism, positioning effortful retrieval through repetition—rather than notation—as the engine of lasting musical retention and real-time improvisational responsiveness.
