# Chapter 2 — Jazz Standard Study #1: Autumn Leaves

_Distillation; backed by `chapters/ch02.md`._

Chapter 2 introduces the harmonic and formal foundations for analyzing and improvising over Autumn Leaves, a jazz standard in AABA form. The chapter establishes the Major and Minor Diatonic Series of 7th Chords as the primary framework for reading scale degrees and chord qualities, clarifies the convention of "concert keys" as the standard performance pitches for jazz standards, and defines the relative key relationship that governs Autumn Leaves' harmonic structure. The master's characteristic move throughout is the emphasis on practical jazz conventions—such as the modal mixture of minor IV to dominant seventh and the pervasive ii-V-I progression—over strict classical theory. The chapter introduces three load-bearing terms: guide tones (the 3rds and 7ths of chords, which define quality and serve as the melodic spine of improvisation), voice leading (the smooth stepwise motion of these tones from chord to chord), and the ii-V-I progression, declared the "cornerstone" of jazz harmony that students must recognize and improvise over immediately. The chapter concludes with a concrete practice method: playing guide tones isolated in half or quarter notes through the entire song form.
