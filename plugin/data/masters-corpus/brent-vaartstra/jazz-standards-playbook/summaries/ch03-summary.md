# Chapter 3 — Jazz Standard Study #2: Blue Bossa

_Distillation; backed by `chapters/ch03.md`._

Chapter 3 teaches the foundational elements of interpreting and improvising over the jazz standard Blue Bossa, beginning with its signature rhythmic characteristic: straight eighth notes rather than swung feel. The chapter establishes Blue Bossa's 16-bar form as structurally essential knowledge and introduces dual harmonic frameworks—the iv-VII relationship to C minor and the ii-V of relative major—for understanding bars 3-4, alongside the explicit mechanism of key center change to Db major via ii-V-I progression. A key generative principle emerges: strong melody licenses harmonic freedom, even "outside" playing, provided the improvised line resolves with intention. The chapter's characteristic pedagogical move is the three-chorus melody exercise, which scaffolds improvisation development from playing the melody straight, to embellishing it as a guideline, to freely improvising while periodically referencing melodic fragments—a concrete practice protocol that makes the tune's identity audible even during extended departures from the written melody.
