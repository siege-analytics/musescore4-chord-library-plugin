# Chapter 4 — Jazz Standard Study #3: All of Me

_Distillation; backed by `chapters/ch04.md`._

Chapter 4 teaches harmonic analysis of the jazz standard "All of Me" through the lens of chord function and voice leading. The central principle is that a chord's function depends on its harmonic context—both the chords before and after it—establishing that analysis is relational rather than absolute. Key concepts introduced include secondary dominants and tonicization (when a dominant 7 chord acts as V of a non-tonic diatonic chord), explicit formulas for major 7, dominant 7, minor 7, half-diminished, and diminished 7 chords, and the chromatic approach as a named technique for non-structural melodic movement. The chapter reinforces that chord tones (particularly guide tones: 3rds and 7ths) rather than scales are the primary material for improvisation, and establishes a voice-leading rule for chord tone arpeggios: they always move in order (Root-3-5-7 or any rotation), with smooth connection across chord changes determined by proximity to the next chord. Characteristic moves include analyzing the IV-to-iv minor pivot as tonicization into C minor and introducing the cycle-of-fourths as an analytical tool for substitution chords, while cautioning against major intervals when improvising over iv minor chords.
