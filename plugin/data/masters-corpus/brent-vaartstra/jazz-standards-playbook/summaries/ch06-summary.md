# Chapter 6 — Jazz Standard Study #5: Sweet Georgia Brown

_Distillation; backed by `chapters/ch06.md`._

Chapter 6 applies modal and chromatic theory to the jazz standard Sweet Georgia Brown, using its unusual VI7 opener and extended dominant cycles to teach harmonic conversion and improvisational navigation. The chapter establishes that diatonic minor chords can permissibly convert to dominant 7ths, then applies this rule to analyze bars 1–13 as a cycle of resolving dominants, the chromatic turnaround section as descending dominants targeting VI7, and the "backdoor" I7-VI7-ii-V-I device as a memorable reductive label for complex harmonic motion. The master introduces the Mixolydian scale—the fifth mode, constructed as a major scale with a flatted 7th (formula: 1-2-3-4-5-6-b7)—and reframes scales not as linear drills but as "pitch collections," harmonic resource pools that guide melodic choice over chord changes. Chord-tone pattern exercises and the practice mandate to take all licks through 12 keys embed the concrete internalization method central to the book's improvisational pedagogy.
