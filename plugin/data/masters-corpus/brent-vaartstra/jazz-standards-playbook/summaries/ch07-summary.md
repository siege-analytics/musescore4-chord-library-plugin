# Chapter 7 — Jazz Standard Study #6: So What

_Distillation; backed by `chapters/ch07.md`._

Chapter 7 introduces modal harmony through the jazz standard "So What," a foundational departure from the functional harmony that dominates earlier standards in the book. The chapter establishes that modal harmony operates on modes derived from a parent major scale—specifically, D Dorian (built from C major) with a brief modulation to Eb Dorian (from Db major)—and teaches students that guide tones are unnecessary in modal contexts. The core techniques presented include the Dorian mode formula (W-H-W-W-W-H-W), voice-leading principles for modal arpeggios that connect via the nearest chord tone rather than always starting on the root, and modal pattern exercises in thirds that link adjacent modes through half- and whole-step intervals. Rather than mechanically running scales, students learn to use modes as note choices within improvisation. The chapter distills Miles Davis's characteristic approach to this tune: sparse, space-conscious playing that stays primarily within the Dorian mode while occasionally employing chromatic approach notes, modeling how to apply modal knowledge idiomatically rather than literally.
