# Chapter 9 — Jazz Standard Study #8: Have You Met Miss Jones

_Distillation; backed by `chapters/ch09.md`._

Chapter 9 applies harmonic substitution and voice-leading techniques to the jazz standard "Have You Met Miss Jones," focusing on how diminished 7 chords function both as rootless dominant b9 substitutes and as chromatic passing chords. The chapter establishes that F#dim7 shares all pitch classes with D7(b9) except the root, making this substitution relationship analytically transparent and aurally convincing. A central architectural feature is the bridge's key centers, which move in a cycle of major thirds (Bb–Gb–D–Ab), a structure the chapter reframes as stacked ii–V–I progressions in different momentary key centers to make the rapid modulation navigable. For improvisation over diminished chords, the chapter teaches students to treat the passing dim7 as a dominant b9 and target its chord tones, and introduces the Whole-Half diminished scale (W–H–W–H–W–H–W) as the theoretical basis for note choice. The melodic techniques include stepwise connection at bar boundaries when moving between key centers, though the chapter explicitly validates phrase separation as an equally valid alternative, reflecting the author's characteristic balance between principle and musical flexibility.
