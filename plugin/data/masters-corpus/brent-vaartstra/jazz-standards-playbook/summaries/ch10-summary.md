# Chapter 10 — Jazz Standard Study #9: All the Things You Are

_Distillation; backed by `chapters/ch10.md`._

Chapter 10 analyzes "All the Things You Are," a 36-bar AABA jazz standard in Ab major that serves as an intensive study of cycling 4ths—a foundational harmonic movement in jazz. The chapter teaches how the tune opens with a vi chord that initiates a diatonic cycle of 4ths, how key transpositions repeat the first eight bars in new centers (with the V chord acting as the "secret weapon" for resolving to new keys), and how passing diminished chords function chromatically to connect chord changes. Jerome Kern's harmonic choices in bars 29-32 deliberately depart from pure cycle-of-4ths motion, offering players an alternative conceptual framework for non-diatonic passages. Beyond harmonic analysis, the chapter consolidates six core improvisation tools—guide tones, melody, chord tones, contrafacts, motifs, and transcribed licks—and emphasizes composed solos as a deliberate practice method to slow improvisation down and develop jazz language, whether notated or memorized directly on the instrument.
