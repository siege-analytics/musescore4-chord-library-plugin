# Chapter 12 — Wrapping Things Up

_Distillation; backed by `chapters/ch12.md`._

Chapter 12 frames jazz standards as the essential vehicles for learning improvisation, establishing that deep study of their harmonic and rhythmic structures builds transferable skills across the entire jazz repertoire. The chapter's core pedagogical move is to position the standards selected throughout the book not as isolated pieces but as exemplars of harmonic principles that apply to hundreds of others, making each standard a gateway to broader understanding. The master's characteristic perspective emerges in the insistence that music is a lifetime study with no final arrival point—a rule-of-practice that reorients the student's relationship to the material beyond mastery toward perpetual exploration. The load-bearing terminology centers on "vehicles" (standards as platforms for improvisation exploration) and the implicit principle that harmonic and rhythmic transferability is the measure of pedagogical value.
