## Roman Numeral chord labeling

> When talking about chord function in jazz we use Roman Numerals to label
> each chord in a given key. We do this to differentiate chord numbers from scale
> numbers (which are written with Arabic numerals such as 1, 2 and 3).

**Significance (p. 192).** Defines the foundational purpose of the Roman numeral system and the distinction from Arabic scale-degree numbering.

## Capital vs. small Roman Numerals

> * Capital Roman Numerals (Imaj7, \Vmaj7, V7): are used to indicate Major and
> Dominant chords.
>
> ¢ Small Roman Numerals (iim7, iiim7, vimm7, viimm7b5): are used to indicate
> Minor and Diminished/Half-Diminished chords.

**Significance (p. 192).** Core notational rule: capitalization encodes chord quality, required to interpret any Roman-numeral chord chart in the book.

## Chromatic alterations outside key

> If the chord appears outside of the basic key center (a tritone substitution for
> example) b’s and #'s are used to indicate these chords.

**Significance (p. 193).** Extends the Roman numeral system beyond diatonic harmony to cover chromatic substitutions like tritone subs.
