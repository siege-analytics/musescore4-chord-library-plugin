# Chapter 1 — Jazz Guitar Chords

_Distillation; backed by `chapters/ch01.md`._

This chapter teaches the five essential jazz chord qualities (maj7, dominant 7, minor 7, half diminished 7, diminished 7) using a moveable root-position system where the red dot indicates the chord's root and determines its name, combined with specific right-hand fingerpicking technique (thumb on bass, fingers 1-3 on upper voices) and the foundational ii–V–I–vi progression drawn from the major scale. The master's characteristic pedagogical moves appear in the transposition rule ("root = chord name") and the systematic progression from steady comping rhythms through the Freddie Green rhythm (quarter notes with 2-and-4 accents) to more sophisticated figures like anticipation and the Charleston pattern, each with explicit practice rules (metronome for syncopation, phrase-by-phrase learning for repertoire). Load-bearing terminology introduced includes red dot notation for root, chord tone numbers for quality designation, anticipation (placing next-bar's chord on the & of 4), the Freddie Green rhythm, and the Charleston—all of which establish the harmonic and rhythmic vocabulary for beginning jazz guitarists.
