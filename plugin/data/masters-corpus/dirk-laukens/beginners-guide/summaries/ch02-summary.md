# Chapter 2 — Playing Jazz Standards

_Distillation; backed by `chapters/ch02.md`._

This chapter teaches five core jazz standards—Birks' Works, Summertime, Maiden Voyage, Blue Bossa, and Autumn Leaves—establishing depth-over-breadth as the method's priority and recordings as the ear-first principle that precedes fretboard work. The master's characteristic move is bridging rock/blues players into jazz vocabulary: minor pentatonic soloing over Summertime, syncopation discipline in bossa grooves, and key transposition as a transferable discipline across tunes that share harmonic families. The load-bearing terminology introduced includes turnaround substitutions (bVI-V-I, ii-V-I, biii-bVI/ii-V-I in minor blues), modal harmony and Dorian modes, ii-V-I progressions in both major and minor forms, syncopation in comping and soloing, and chord melody as the three-element technique of playing bass, chords, and melody simultaneously with melody confined to the upper strings.
