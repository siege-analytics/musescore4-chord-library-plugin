# Chapter 3 — Jazz Guitar Arpeggios

_Distillation; backed by `chapters/ch03.md`._

Chapter 3 teaches the foundational technique of jazz guitar arpeggios—broken chords played with plectrum and muted to prevent ringing, independent from chord shapes and used for soloing rather than accompaniment—by anchoring all arpeggio shapes to the ii-V-I progression and introducing five movable grips for major, minor, and dominant chords that correspond to the CAGED system. The chapter establishes three functional purposes for arpeggios in improvisation: outlining harmonic direction, enabling smoother voice leading, and adding color through substitutions. Master technique surfaces in the voice-leading rule (connecting arpeggios by proximity rather than restarting from roots) and the creative liberation principle that arpeggios can start on any note and be reordered at will. Key terminology introduced includes arpeggio patterns (5ths/4ths skipping, groups of three), movable shapes, approach notes (chromatic tension created by approaching from one fret below, resolving to chord tones), and enclosures (the bebop decoration of surrounding each chord tone with notes one fret above and below).
