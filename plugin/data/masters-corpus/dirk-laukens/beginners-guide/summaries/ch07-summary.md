# Chapter 7 — Appendix - The Roman Numeral System

_Distillation; backed by `chapters/ch07.md`._

This appendix introduces the Roman numeral system for labeling chord function in jazz, establishing capital Roman numerals (I, V, V7) for major and dominant chords and small Roman numerals (ii, iii, vi, vii°) for minor and diminished/half-diminished chords—a notational convention that distinguishes chord labeling from Arabic scale-degree numbering. The system extends beyond diatonic harmony to accommodate chromatic alterations and substitutions like tritone substitutions, marked with flats and sharps when chords fall outside the basic key center. The key load-bearing terminology introduced are Roman numeral chord labels themselves, along with the distinction between capital and small numeral notation as an encoding of chord quality that readers must internalize to interpret chord charts throughout the book. This is a reference appendix rather than an instructional chapter.
