## Drop 2 and drop 3 chord types

> These shapes contain both drop 2 and drop 3 chords in root position, which is the best
> place to get started playing jazz guitar chords.

**Significance (p. 8).** Defines the voicing system (drop 2 / drop 3 in root position) that underpins all the chord shapes introduced in this chapter.

## Four-note shapes vs. smaller shapes

> You will learn smaller shapes in the next section, which are easier to play, but these
> four-note shapes drill the sound of jazz into your ears better than the smaller shapes.

**Significance (p. 8).** Gives a pedagogical rule about why full four-note voicings are prioritized first — ear training rationale over ease of execution.

## Learning sequence rule

> Start by learning one shape for each chord type then take those changes to a jazz tune
> you know or are working on.

**Significance (p. 8).** States the method's prescribed practice sequence: isolate one shape per chord quality, then immediately apply it in a musical context.
