## Shell chord definition

> These three-note chords contain the root, 3rd, 7th (or the 6th) of each chord.

**Significance (p. 14).** This is the foundational definition of a shell chord's intervallic content, establishing which chord tones are included and why they are harmonically sufficient.

## Shell chord tonal register

> Because of this, you hit the essential chord tones for each shape, and mostly on the
> lower String sets.

**Significance (p. 14).** This explains the practical voicing characteristic of shell chords — their placement on lower strings — which directly informs how the player positions and selects these shapes.

## Tone awareness / bass register

> Since these shapes are lower on the neck than other chord shapes, you need to keep
> your tone in mind so you don't clash with the bass player in your playing.

**Significance (p. 14).** This is an explicit rule about ensemble awareness: the low-register voicing requires conscious tone management to avoid muddying the bass player's frequency space.

## Shell chords in ensemble comping

> With a soft tone, these chords are highly effective when comping in a duo, trio, or
> larger ensembles.

**Significance (p. 14).** This gives the player a concrete prescription — soft tone — and the specific contexts (duo, trio, larger ensemble) where shell chords are the preferred comping tool.
