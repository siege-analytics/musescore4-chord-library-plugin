# Chapter 1 — How to Read Chord Diagrams

_Distillation; backed by `chapters/ch01.md`._

Chapter 1 teaches how to decode chord diagrams through visual symbols: a red circle marks the root (tonic), black circles with numbers inside indicate chord tones by scale degree, and finger numbers 1–4 on the right side show left-hand fingering. String-status indicators (X for muted, O for open) determine right-hand technique—most chords are played fingerstyle with thumb and three fingers rather than strummed. The chapter's load-bearing principle is the movable chord shape: non-open voicings are transposable templates that slide up or down the neck to any root, with a two-fret shift equaling a whole-step transposition (e.g., Cmaj7 becomes Dmaj7 two frets higher). This architecture multiplies the dictionary's utility across all twelve keys without requiring separate fingerings for each root.
