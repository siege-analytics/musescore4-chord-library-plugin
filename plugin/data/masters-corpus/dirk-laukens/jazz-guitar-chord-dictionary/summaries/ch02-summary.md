# Chapter 2 — Basic Chords

_Distillation; backed by `chapters/ch02.md`._

Chapter 2 introduces the foundational voicing system for jazz guitar through drop 2 and drop 3 chord types in root position, establishing the core shapes that underpin all subsequent chord work. The method prioritizes learning four-note voicings first despite their technical difficulty, as they develop the ear's jazz sound more effectively than smaller alternatives. Students follow a structured practice sequence: master one shape per chord type in isolation, then immediately apply it to a real jazz tune to integrate theory with musicality. Key terminology introduced includes the dual naming convention for Minor 7b5 (Half-Diminished) chords, essential for reading lead sheets and chord charts. The chapter's pedagogical emphasis reflects a characteristic balance between sound-driven ear training and systematic chord vocabulary building.
