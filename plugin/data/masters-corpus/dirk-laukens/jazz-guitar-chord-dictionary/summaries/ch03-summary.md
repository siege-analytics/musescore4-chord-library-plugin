# Chapter 3 — Shell Chords (aka Guide Tone Chords)

_Distillation; backed by `chapters/ch03.md`._

Chapter 3 introduces shell chords (guide tone chords), three-note voicings built from the root, 3rd, and 7th (or 6th) of a chord, which capture the essential harmonic identity of any chord while keeping the shapes positioned on the lower string sets. The chapter emphasizes the practical advantages of this voicing strategy: shell chords are harmonically sufficient despite their minimal note count, and their low-register placement makes them highly effective in ensemble comping contexts like duos, trios, and larger ensembles. A key load-bearing rule emerges around tone awareness and ensemble sensitivity—because shell chords occupy the bass register, the player must consciously manage tone control (using a soft tone) to avoid frequency clashing with the bass player. This reveals the master's characteristic move: economizing the voicing (three notes instead of four or more) while maintaining harmonic clarity, and coupling that economy with explicit awareness of the player's role within a rhythm section.
