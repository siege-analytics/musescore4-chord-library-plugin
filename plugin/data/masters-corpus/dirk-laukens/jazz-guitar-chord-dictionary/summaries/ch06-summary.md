# Chapter 6 — Chord Inversions

_Distillation; backed by `chapters/ch06.md`._

Chapter 6 teaches chord inversions—the technique of placing chord tones other than the root in the bass voice—and presents them in systematic order (root, 3rd, 5th, 7th) for each chord type. The foundational comping principle is that any inversion of a chord symbol can legitimately substitute for that symbol in performance, giving the student flexibility in voicing choices. The chapter emphasizes that while inversions use identical pitch content, the bass note's position creates a different harmonic flavor and sound quality, so ear-based selection determines which inversion works best in context. Key terminology includes inversion order, bass voice hierarchy, and the concept of voicing as a timbral decision rather than a fixed rule.
