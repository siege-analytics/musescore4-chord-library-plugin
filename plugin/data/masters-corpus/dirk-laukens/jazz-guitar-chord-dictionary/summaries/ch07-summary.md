# Chapter 7 — Open Chords

_Distillation; backed by `chapters/ch07.md`._

Chapter 7 teaches open chords, which are constructed by incorporating open strings into their voicings, fundamentally distinguishing them from closed barre chord shapes. While limited to a few keys, open chords provide essential comping vocabulary in guitar-friendly key centers and deliver a signature sound unique to the instrument through their open-string resonance. The chapter introduces load-bearing enharmonic notation, such as the equivalence Bm11(b6) = Gmaj7(add 6)/B, enabling students to read and name complex open voicings from multiple harmonic perspectives.
