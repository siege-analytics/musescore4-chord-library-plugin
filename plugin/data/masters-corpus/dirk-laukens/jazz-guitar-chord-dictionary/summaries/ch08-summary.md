# Chapter 8 — Quartal Chords

_Distillation; backed by `chapters/ch08.md`._

Chapter 8 introduces quartal chords (also called 4th chords), which are built by stacking 4th intervals above the lowest note, distinguishing them structurally from the tertian harmony of traditional chord types. The chapter establishes that quartal chords sound open and modern, making them universally applicable over jazz standards in both comping and soloing contexts rather than being limited to specific harmonic situations. The practical focus centers on two fretboard implementations: voicings based on the C Dorian scale using string set 2-3-4-5, and the same harmonic material transposed to string set 1-2-3-4, allowing players to access these sonorities across different regions of the guitar. This chapter is primarily instructional, teaching a specific chord-building technique and its application rather than biographical or introductory material.
