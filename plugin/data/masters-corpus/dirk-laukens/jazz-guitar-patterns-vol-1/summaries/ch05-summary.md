# Chapter 5 — Study 5 - All The Things You Are 2

_Distillation; backed by `chapters/ch05.md`._

Study 5 expands the harmonic vocabulary of "All The Things You Are" by introducing techniques for targeting scale degrees with arpeggios from below (the foundational Honeysuckle Rose motif) and establishing chord substitution patterns that work across multiple harmonic contexts. The chapter systematizes pattern-based approaches—including the minor 1235 pattern, major/dominant patterns distinguished by their 7th content, and extensions restricted to specific chord qualities—while introducing two major harmonic substitution rules: playing harmonic minor (or Phrygian dominant) over dominant chords to create a 7b9/b13 sound, and using diminished arpeggios (from the b9, 3, 5, or b7) to produce b9 coloration. A key technical insight threads through the chapter: the symmetrical interval structure of diminished chords (stacked minor thirds) makes their inversions identical on the fretboard, a practical advantage for application. The chapter also highlights deliberate voice leading across phrase spans, exemplified by chromatic motion descending from the high E string, demonstrating how long-range melodic coherence shapes the solo's architecture.
