# Chapter 6 — Study 6 - Solar

_Distillation; backed by `chapters/ch06.md`._

Study 6 introduces three foundational concepts for soloing over dominant chords: half-diminished and diminished arpeggios as chromatic substitutions (moving from the 9 to the b9), the altered scale as the 7th mode of melodic minor containing all four common altered tones (b9, #9, b5, b13), and the bebop scale as an 8-note framework built by adding a chromatic passing tone to modal scales. The chapter's characteristic moves involve superimposing arpeggios—Gaug7, Bmaj7#5, and Abm/maj7 extracted from the altered scale, or G6 shapes applied to both major and dominant qualities—to generate harmonic tension over the underlying chord. The load-bearing terminology centers on the altered scale's definition and three bebop variants (dominant, major, minor), with the most powerful rule being the bebop scale's metric guarantee: starting on a downbeat and chord tone (1, 3, 5, or b7) ensures all following chord tones land on downbeats, making the scale a precise rhythmic framework for jazz phrasing.
