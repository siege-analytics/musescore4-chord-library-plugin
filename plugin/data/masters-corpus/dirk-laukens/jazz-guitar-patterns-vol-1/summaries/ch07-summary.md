# Chapter 7 — Study 7 - Autumn Leaves

_Distillation; backed by `chapters/ch07.md`._

Chapter 7 teaches four variants of the bebop scale—dominant (Mixolydian + b7), minor (Dorian + b7), major (major scale + b6), and melodic minor (melodic minor + b6)—each with specific harmonic applications ranging from dominant sevenths to minor II-V-I progressions. The master's characteristic approach throughout involves substitution techniques and voice-leading principles, demonstrated in patterns that layer arpeggios (Fmaj7 over Dm7, diminished over G7, augmented over minor chords) before resolving through modal passages, creating idiomatic bebop movement. Key technical instruction emphasizes guitar-specific execution: using slides to navigate the chromatic passing tones and fingering chord shapes as single-note lines rather than full voicings. The load-bearing terminology introduced includes the four bebop scale formulas, the specific voice-leading motion '5th to b9 to 5th' over II-V-I, and the concept of substitution arpeggios built from interval relationships (relative major, diminished, augmented on the b3) that yield characteristic minor/major 7 and melodic sonorities.
