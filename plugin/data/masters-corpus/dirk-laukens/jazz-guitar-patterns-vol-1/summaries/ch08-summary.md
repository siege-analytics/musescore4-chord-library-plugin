# Chapter 8 — Study 8 - Groovin' High

_Distillation; backed by `chapters/ch08.md`._

Study 8—Groovin' High introduces a systematic toolkit of bebop patterns built on arpeggio substitutions, tritone substitution, and chromatic voice-leading techniques that extend across major, minor, and dominant chord contexts. The chapter demonstrates Wes Montgomery's characteristic approach of layering major seventh arpeggios over minor seventh chords (such as Fmaj7 over Dm7), combined with altered-scale runs and the foundational 1235 motif with encircling, which recurs across multiple harmonic situations. Key load-bearing concepts include the bebop scale's chromatic passing-tone construction, the systematic minor-triad substitution formulas (deployed on the 3rd of major chords, the 5th of minor chords, and the 5th or b9 of dominant chords), tritone substitution (mapping a Bb7 arpeggio over E7, for example), and idiomatic finger-friendly patterns that facilitate higher-tempo execution—all unified by the principle that rhythmic placement and voice-leading targets (such as chromatic descent from the 7 to the 5) are as definitive as pitch content in defining bebop voice.
