## Tritone substitution definition

> The basic application of a
> tritone chord substitution is to take any 7th chord and play another 7th chord that has its root a tritone away
> from the original.

**Significance (p. 1).** This is the foundational definition of the technique the entire chapter is built around — without it, none of the subsequent applications make sense.

## Tritone sub over II V I

> This guitar lesson demonstrates how you can play scales and arpeggios starting from the b5
> (a tritone away) of the V7 chord in a II V I chord progression. This way you will highlight altered tones as the
> b9 and the #11.

**Significance (p. 1).** This passage establishes the core method — using the substitution to target specific altered tones — which governs every lick and scale choice that follows.
