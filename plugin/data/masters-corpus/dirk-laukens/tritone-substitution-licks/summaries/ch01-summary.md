# Chapter 1 — Tritone Substitution Guitar Licks

_Distillation; backed by `chapters/ch01.md`._

Chapter 1 teaches tritone substitution as a technique for soloing over jazz progressions, specifically demonstrating how to replace a V7 chord with another 7th chord a tritone away to target altered tones like b9, #11, and b13. The chapter establishes four primary options for soloing over the substituted dominant—dom7 arpeggio, dom9 arpeggio, major pentatonic scale, and Mixolydian mode—each with detailed tone-mapping analysis showing which intervals of the substitute chord correspond to which altered extensions of the original dominant. The master's characteristic approach is methodical and analytical: each lick is deconstructed measure-by-measure, and every scale degree is mapped to its harmonic function, building a reusable framework that students can transpose to any key. Load-bearing terminology introduced includes tritone substitution itself, the four soloing options, altered tones (b9, #11, b13), approach notes, and the Dorian bebop scale (Dorian mode with a chromatic passing tone), all anchored to the canonical II V I progression and its Dm7 | Db7 | CM7 realization.
