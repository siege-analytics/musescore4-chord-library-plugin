“3-Note Chords for the I Chord in G Blues”
[Lesson/Letter to Bruce]
Ted Greene 1995-12-16, early Saturday
As you requested: For you, my friend…in your favorite key. Sorry for the delay –
life stepped in with other plans.
Dear Bruce,
I’ve never given this material out before – it’s just you and me with it. Let’s
keep it for ours for a while. You see, once of the downsides to teaching is
everything gets “cheap” or at least cheapened thru overuse – we desensitize.
An example: the harp-harmonics. At one time when people heard it, it took
their breath away. Now it’s like it barely has the power to move – it’s just an
unpleasant part of our species, I guess. I hate it.
So let’s not throw this onto others just yet – let it breathe for a while with
newness – yeah – let them be hungry to hear you play it. If they say, “show me,”
do an “end-run” (you’re good at this), or blame me: “Ted said to keep it under
wraps for a while,” or whatever.
I’d like to publish all my stuff, and God willing, I will. Sharing is cool (you
know). For now, I’m sharing this material with you and only you. I’ve never quite
codified it before so it’s kinda new for me too.
Hope you enjoy,
Ted
Have fantastic holidays, Bruce
P.S. I’ll be featured in Vintage Guitar, February ’96 issue (“Spotlight”).
I worked a long time on the “influences” section. Hope they don’t butcher it.
