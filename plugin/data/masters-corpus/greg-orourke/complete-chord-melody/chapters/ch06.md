## Learn the arrangements hands-on

> Observe the devices used in the arrangements as marked, and it would be very beneficial to actually
> learn these arrangements – getting your ‘hands dirty’ with some examples is one of the best ways to
> internalize the techniques that are being used, which you can then apply to your own arrangements.

**Significance (p. 200).** States O'Rourke's core pedagogical instruction for this module: internalize technique through hands-on study of complete arrangements, not abstract reading.

## Arpeggiation fills ballad space

> This rendition is a good example of using arpeggiation to fill in the long spaces between each melody
> note in this slow ballad.

**Significance (p. 201).** Names the load-bearing arranging device for slow ballads in O'Rourke's method: arpeggiation as a melody-space filler.

## Diads support the melody

> Diads are also used frequently to provide support to the melody line.

**Significance (p. 201).** Identifies diads as a primary melody-support device in O'Rourke's chord-melody toolkit.

## Up-tempo devices stack

> You can see the diads, octaves, and passing chords being a feature in this rendition.

**Significance (p. 206).** Catalogues the three load-bearing devices O'Rourke uses to drive an up-tempo arrangement: diads, octaves, and passing chords.

## Manipulate melody rhythm for fingering

> I needed to manipulate the rhythm of the melody in places to facilitate easier jumps across the
> fretboard.

**Significance (p. 206).** Gives the explicit rule that rhythmic adjustment of the melody is acceptable when fretboard jumps would otherwise be impractical.
