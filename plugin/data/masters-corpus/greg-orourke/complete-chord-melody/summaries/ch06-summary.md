# Chapter 6 — Module 4 - Solo Guitar Arrangement Examples

_Distillation; backed by `chapters/ch06.md`._

Chapter 6 opens Module 4 by framing complete solo-guitar arrangements as study material, with O'Rourke's core pedagogical instruction that students should physically learn the arrangements rather than just read them, because hands-on internalization is how the underlying techniques transfer to a student's own work (Learn the arrangements hands-on). The chapter then walks through the load-bearing arranging devices visible across the example renditions: arpeggiation as a melody-space filler in slow ballads (Arpeggiation fills ballad space), diads as a primary melody-support layer (Diads support the melody), and the up-tempo stack of diads, octaves, and passing chords used together to drive faster arrangements (Up-tempo devices stack). O'Rourke's characteristic move surfaces in the explicit permission to rewrite melodic rhythm when fingering demands it: rhythmic adjustment of the melody is acceptable to facilitate fretboard jumps (Manipulate melody rhythm for fingering), framing the arranger's pragmatics as a sanctioned rule rather than a compromise.
