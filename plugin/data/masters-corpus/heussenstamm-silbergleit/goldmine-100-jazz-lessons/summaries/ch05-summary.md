# Chapter 5 — 3/4 Time, Picking, and Chordal Shells (Lessons 18-25)

_Distillation; backed by `chapters/ch05.md`._

Chapter 5 teaches rhythmic and harmonic foundations for jazz guitar in 3/4 time and straight-4 swing, establishing that in 3/4 measures the "and" of beat 2 receives emphasis and (by default) a second chord entry, while solo accompaniment uses a bass-and-chords figure combining distinct rhythmic layers. The chapter introduces core left-hand techniques—finger rolls (both ascending on the tip and descending with flat pre-positioning) and finger substitution following a directional rule—alongside right-hand picking discipline: rhythm-governed alternate picking maintains continuous motion to catch downbeats and upbeats, while straight-4 quarter notes require precise release timing (evoking Freddie Green's swing phrasing), minimal voicing size, and low dynamic placement ("felt more than heard"). Shell voicings, the chapter's load-bearing harmonic concept, distill chords to their defining tones (root, 3rd, 7th), then eliminate the root to create compact two-string shapes on the middle strings that outline progressions alongside a walking bass—a technique reinforced by observing that tritone substitutes share shell shapes with inverted 3rds and 7ths. Scalar practice patterns (Pattern 1's descending groups of four, Pattern 2's directional change within groupings) and the scale-degree formula for "the Lick" (1–2–3–4–2–7–1) round out the chapter's melodic toolkit, emphasizing interval-driven versatility across scales and chords.
