# Chapter 9 — Concept, Shifting, String-Centric Lines, Arpeggios (Lessons 51-58)

_Distillation; backed by `chapters/ch09.md`._

Chapter 9 teaches creative approaches to scalar and linear motion through numeric pattern sequencing, position shifting, and string-specific techniques. The governing principle is breaking away from straight scalar motion—achieved through exercises like the seven-eighth-note shifting drill (which pairs quick thinking under time pressure with chord-change navigation) and the one-string thing's three-note permutation system. Key techniques include alternate picking for single-string speed, sliding with a light grip for fluency, and the foundational sweep-picking approach to arpeggios. The chapter introduces the three-octave string-group system (dividing low, middle, and high strings to structure long ascending lines with repeating fingering patterns) and mini-barre-mini-sweep technique for pick-based playing. Diatonic arpeggio construction—breaking scale tones into 1-3-5 triads and add9/slash-9 extensions—escapes linear scalar sound, while legato triplets demand pinky strength and anticipatory thinking. Throughout, the master emphasizes listening for sound over fingering convenience and developing a light-grip shifting technique that enables fluid hand movement across the neck.
