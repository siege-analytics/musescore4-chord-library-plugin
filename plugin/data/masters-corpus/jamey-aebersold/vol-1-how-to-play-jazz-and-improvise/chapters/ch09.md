## Pentatonic scale definition

> Pentatonic generally means a scale
> built of five tones. In jazz, the two scales which have become popular are the major pentatonic and
> the minor pentatonic.

**Significance (p. 30).** Foundational pentatonic scale definition.

## Pentatonic scale usage rule

> The pentatonic scale sound should
> be thought of as a small part of the overall musical spectrum.
>
> The pentatonic scale is a sound which can add variety to the overall musical framework. Rather
> than “running it in the ground," sprinkle it in amongst other scale sounds.

**Significance (p. 30).** Stylistic restraint rule for pentatonic use.

## Blues scale vs pentatonic comparison

> The blues scale and the
> minor pentatonic scale are very similar. The blues scale has six tones and the pentatonic scale has
> five.

**Significance (p. 30).** Distinguishes blues from minor pentatonic by tone count.

## Pentatonic over chord types

> The pentatonic scale can be used over major, minor, dom.7th, half-diminished, dim., whole tone
> and almost any other scale.

**Significance (p. 30).** Broad harmonic applicability of pentatonic.

## Pentatonic inside major scale

> There are usually several pentatonic scales inside every regular scale.
> Below, I list the pentatonic possibilities found inside the C major scale and the F dorian minor scale.
> We usually avoid using the 4th note of the major scale as part of a pentatonic scale. All of the notes
> of the minor (dorian) scale are useable.

**Significance (p. 30).** Nested-pentatonic rule and 4th-avoidance rule.

## Melody personalization rule

> Don't be afraid to personalize the melody to a song. Play the melody like you would sing it.
> Let it breath. Let it flow. Let it sing. Let it tell a story. Make the melody YOURS.

**Significance (p. 30).** Stylistic directive linking voice-singing to melodic interpretation.
