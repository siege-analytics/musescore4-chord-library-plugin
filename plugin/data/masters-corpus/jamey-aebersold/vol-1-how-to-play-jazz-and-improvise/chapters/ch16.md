## 3rd and 7th scale degrees

> The two most important notes in any scale are the 3rd and 7th. They tell the listener what the quality is and
> indicate the harmonic motion. The 3rd tells us if it's major or minor. The 7th tells whether the sound is stable
> (doesn't want to move to another chord) or if it wants to move on to a chord of resolution.

**Significance (p. 54).** Defines foundational harmonic function of 3rd and 7th.

## Dominant 7th resolution rule

> Dominants typically
> want to resolve to a chord up a perfect 4th (C7 wants to resolve to F, F-, F7 etc.).

**Significance (p. 54).** Core voice-leading rule for dominant scales.

## Altered tones and half-step resolution

> The altered tones are in bold type. Those tones usually resolve by half-step to a scale or chord tone. This
> amounts to tension then release. It's a natural occurence in music.

**Significance (p. 54).** Rule for treating altered tones — resolve by half-step.

## DOM.7th scale — 4th tone caution

> DOM.7th = C7 = CDEFGABbC This is the basic dominant 7th sound. Be careful how you treat
> the 4th tone. Use it as a passing tone.

**Significance (p. 54).** Foundational dominant scale with 4th-degree avoidance rule.

## Bebop scale — B natural placement

> BEBOP = C7 = CDEFGABbBC Play B natural as a passing tone. It should always appear on
> an upbeat, never on the downbeat.

**Significance (p. 54).** Bebop scale with upbeat placement rule.

## Lydian Dominant scale

> LYDIAN DOM. = C7#4 = CDEF#GABbC The #4 was/is a favorite note. It used to be called a b5.

**Significance (p. 54).** Defines Lydian Dominant and notes #4/b5 terminology.

## Whole-tone scale — 6-tone symmetrical

> WHOLE-TONE = C7+ = CDE F# G#BbC This scale only has 6 tones. It is a symetrical scale used
> often in cartoon music and by DeBussy and Ravel.

**Significance (p. 54).** Whole-tone scale with symmetry property and stylistic reference.

## Diminished scale — 8-tone symmetrical

> DIMINISHED =C7b9 = CDbEbE F#GABbC This scale has 8 different tones.It is symetrical
> and is also used in cartoon music.
> Michael Brecker is a master of this scale sound.

**Significance (p. 54).** Defines diminished scale with canonical practitioner reference.

## Diminished Whole-tone scale — altered tension

> DIM. WHOLE-TONE = C7+9 = CDbEbEF#G#BbC This scale has four altered tones which
> help create tension.

**Significance (p. 54).** Altered scale tied to tension concept.

## Spanish/Jewish scale — minor key use

> SPANISH or JEWISH SCALE = C7(b9) = CDbEFGAbBbC This scale is used often when
> playing in a minor key. It's the same as F harmonic minor.

**Significance (p. 54).** Spanish/Jewish scale = F harmonic minor in minor keys.
