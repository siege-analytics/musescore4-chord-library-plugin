## Transposing through 12 keys

> You should memorize one example and then
> play it through all 12 keys. You may have to write several transposed ones on paper in order to see
> how they look in the new keys.

**Significance (p. 68).** Memorize-then-transpose-12-keys is the book's fundamental practice discipline.

## II/V7 practice starting point

> You should transpose them through the other

> 11 keys. You may want to begin by playing the exercises on page 26 or the TEN BASIC
> PATTERNS.

**Significance (p. 68).** Anchors II/V7 transposition in previously learned patterns.

## Blues progression as three phrases

> Think of the blues progression as being three 4-bar phrases; A beginning, a middle, an end.

**Significance (p. 70).** Conceptual structural model for 12-bar blues.

## Playing what the mind hears

> Always play what your mind hears. Don't be mislead by your ego. Listen to YOURSELF.
> Your REAL self. The self that's deep within your mind.

**Significance (p. 103).** Foundational rule: inner hearing over ego-driven technique.

## Scales and chords as foundation

> Learn your scales and chords. There are not that many and they are the highway to musical
> expression. Make lifelong friends with them.

**Significance (p. 103).** Core technical premise: scales/chords are finite and learnable.

## Sound support (wind instruments)

> SOUND is most important. If you play a wind instrument be sure to support your sound. If you
> don't understand this principal seek out a teacher that can demonstrate it to you. It's your yoice.

**Significance (p. 103).** Tone production as highest-priority physical technique.

## Jazz articulation rule

> Articulation: Don't play a phrase, exercise, scale, chord, pattern without using proper jazz
> articulation. Articulation is the grassroots level of jazz. We often say "they ain't sayin' nothin’ "
> about a player because they fail to utilize familiar jazz articulation.

**Significance (p. 103).** Articulation non-negotiable at every level of practice.

## Memorize melodies and progressions

> Memorize everything. Especially the melodies and chord progressions to tunes. Surprise
> yourself.

**Significance (p. 103).** Memorization bridges knowing to spontaneous improvisation.

## Ear training as lifelong practice

> Ear Training is a lifetime experience. Sharpen your ears throughout the day even though you're
> not near your instrument or a keyboard. Music is all around us. We may as well learn from it.

**Significance (p. 103).** Ear training as continuous perceptual discipline.

## Don't wait to be good enough

> Don't be timid about seeking. And don't wait until
> you are good enough. Good enough is NOW.

**Significance (p. 103).** Counters perfectionism barrier.

## Imagination over knowledge

> Albert Einstein said "IMAGINATION IS MORE IMPORTANT THAN KNOWLEDGE"

**Significance (p. 103).** Reframes technical content as means to creative imagination.
