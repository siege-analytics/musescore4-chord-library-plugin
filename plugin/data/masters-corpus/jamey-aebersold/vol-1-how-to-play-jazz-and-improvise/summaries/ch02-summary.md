# Chapter 2 — How To Use & Practice Guide

_Distillation; backed by `chapters/ch02.md`._

Chapter 2 establishes the pedagogical foundation for improvisation-first practice: the book prioritizes playing over exercises, requiring learners to internalize four-bar phrasing, maintain form awareness through beat counting, and memorize key signatures to focus on musicality rather than notation. The master's characteristic moves include concrete recovery techniques—hearing changes by ear using drum accents, moving wrong notes a half-step to find correct ones, and building facility through a seven-step scale practice method that progresses from root-to-ninth motion through intervals to triads and seventh chords. Load-bearing terminology introduced here includes chord tones (the 1st, 3rd, 5th, 7th, 9th of any scale), the horizontal/vertical metaphor for scales and chords, Dorian mode as the universal minor scale, and the notation conventions (dash for minor, triangle for major, 7 for dominant seventh) that decode the entire volume.
