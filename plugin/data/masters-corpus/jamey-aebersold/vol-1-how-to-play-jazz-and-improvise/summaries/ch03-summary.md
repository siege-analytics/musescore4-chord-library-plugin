# Chapter 3 — How To Begin Playing With The Recording

_Distillation; backed by `chapters/ch03.md`._

Chapter 3 teaches a systematic approach to blues improvisation built on progressive mastery: starting with scale practice organized by chord symbol, advancing through pattern isolation and chromatic transposition, and culminating in fluent phrase playing across chord changes. The master's characteristic moves emphasize fingering fluency as the direct pathway to connected improvisation, with specific techniques like legato articulation (particularly for brass), scale memorization by sound, and recognition of common tones between scales to ensure smooth transitions. Key load-bearing terminology introduced includes chorus (one complete pass through the chord/scale progression), chord tones (root, 3rd, 5th, 7th, 9th, 11th, 13th), triad (the three-note 1-3-5 subset), and the foundational distinction between chords appearing vertically and scales horizontally—all organized through concrete exercises: whole-note scales, quarter-note scales, five-note subsets, scales in thirds extending to the 9th, and arpeggio drills using triad tones.
