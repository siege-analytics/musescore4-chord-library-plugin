# Chapter 4 — Eighth-Note Exercises And Swing

_Distillation; backed by `chapters/ch04.md`._

Chapter 4 introduces extended chords (seventh and ninth chords) as expansions of the triad using the scale's 7th and 9th tones, establishing a tonal hierarchy where roots and thirds form the "family" while 7ths and 9ths add excitement. The chapter's core technical contribution is the swing eighth-note rule—the fundamental distinction between swung eighths for jazz and even eighths for bossa nova and rock styles—each requiring a different rhythmic feel to authentically represent its genre. Key terminology includes seventh chords, ninth chords, the 9th/2nd enharmonic equivalence, and the performance concepts of swing feel and even eighths. The chapter grounds students in structural awareness by emphasizing that jazz phrasing typically organizes in 2, 4, and 8 measure phrases, and reinforces execution details like keeping wind-player fingers close to keys for smooth legato, ultimately teaching that authentic swing demands a loose, unburdened feel without dragging.
