# Chapter 6 — Range, Creativity, and Phrase Construction

_Distillation; backed by `chapters/ch06.md`._

This chapter teaches the harmonic and technical foundations for expanding improvisational range and expression, centering on the rule that chord tones (1, 3, 5, 7) must anchor strong beats to ensure melodies flow naturally. The master's characteristic pedagogical moves are evident in the prescribed four-step pre-improvisation checklist (listening, scale-chord review, memorization by sharps/flats/fingerings, and learning the form), the methodological progression from one-octave to two-octave scales adapted to student ability, and the mind–voice–instrument hierarchy that justifies technical practice as a means of closing the gap between creative intention and instrumental execution. The load-bearing terminology introduced includes "the form" (the memorized order of scales in a progression), "chord tones" as harmonic anchors, and "range extension," with the unifying principle that technical exercises exist solely to free the instrumentalist to improvise spontaneously rather than to constrain imagination to what the hands already know.
