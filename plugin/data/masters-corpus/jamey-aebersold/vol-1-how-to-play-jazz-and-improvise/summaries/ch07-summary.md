# Chapter 7 — Music Fundamentals For Improvising

_Distillation; backed by `chapters/ch07.md`._

Chord tones and strong beats form the foundation for beginning improvised phrases, while rhythm must be mentally heard before execution—a prerequisite for coherence. The chapter maps rhythmic density to emotional effect: fast notes build tension while rests and longer durations create relaxation, with specific "pretty notes" (color tones like the 6, 7, 9, and #4) defined for major, dominant, and minor harmonies to shape this tension-release arc. The centerpiece is a systematic 12-step memorization procedure progressing from single root notes through scales, triads, seventh and ninth chords, and intervallic patterns like broken thirds—designed to culminate in auditory anticipation where the mind hears the progression in advance and fingers find notes automatically. The chapter establishes the method's core philosophy: master the rules thoroughly before breaking them, and develop ear through specific exercises like "drop the needle" listening on play-along recordings.
