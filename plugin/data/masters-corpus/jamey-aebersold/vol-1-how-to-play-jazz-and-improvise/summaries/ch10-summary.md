# Chapter 10 — Chromaticism

_Distillation; backed by `chapters/ch10.md`._

Chapter 10 teaches chromaticism—the use of half-step intervals to approach and embellish chord tones—as a foundational technique for jazz improvisation across all chord types. The chapter introduces specific chromatic approaches: targeting each chord tone (root, 3rd, 5th, 7th) from a half step below, employing enclosure by combining scale tones above with chromatic approach notes below, and descending through half steps from a whole step above. The master's characteristic move is treating the chromatic scale as a complete musical vocabulary—the "musical alphabet"—that can animate any chord or scale context, reinforcing the earlier lesson that multiple pentatonic subsets exist within parent scales and extending that principle to continuous chromatic movement. Load-bearing terminology includes chromaticism itself, enclosure, approach tones, and the reframing of chromatic resources as tools applicable to major, minor, dominant 7th, and any other chord type.
