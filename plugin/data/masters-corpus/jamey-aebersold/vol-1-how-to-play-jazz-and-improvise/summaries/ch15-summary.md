# Chapter 15 — Nomenclature & Scale Syllabus

_Distillation; backed by `chapters/ch15.md`._

Chapter 15 introduces the foundational nomenclature and organizational principles for the Scale Syllabus that follows. The chapter establishes a reduced chord-symbol notation system (C, C7, C-, C∅, C-Δ, C7+9, C7b9) designed to minimize written clutter and free the improviser's mind from the page, along with a symbol legend defining half-steps, whole steps, and quality alterations. The master's characteristic pedagogical move is organizing all scale choices by dissonance level within each chord category, ranging from consonant sounds at the top to increasingly tense options below, grounded in the principle that music is made of tension and release—and the improviser's control of this balance determines communicative success. Load-bearing concepts include the chord/scale equivalence (each symbol represents a series of usable tones), the ear-voice-instrument learning method (sing the scale, play what you sang), and critical clarifications such as the complete tone content of abbreviated symbols like C7+9 (Diminished Whole Tone: Root, b9, +9, 3rd, +4, +5, b7) and C7b9 (Diminished: Root, b9, +9, 3rd, +4, 5th, 6th, b7), along with a practical preference order for minor scale choices favoring Dorian over Pure Minor.
