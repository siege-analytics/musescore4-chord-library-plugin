# Chapter 16 — The Music Student's Plea & Tune Learning

_Distillation; backed by `chapters/ch16.md`._

The 3rd and 7th scale degrees form the harmonic foundation of any scale, determining its quality and harmonic motion, while dominant 7th chords resolve predictably up a perfect 4th with altered tones resolving by half-step. This chapter catalogs seven dominant 7th-based scales—DOM.7th, Bebop, Lydian Dominant, Whole-tone, Diminished, Dim. Whole-tone, and Spanish/Jewish—each with distinct characteristics: the Bebop scale places B natural only on upbeats, the Lydian Dominant features a raised 4th, the symmetrical Whole-tone and Diminished scales appear in cartoon music and are mastered by practitioners like Michael Brecker, and the Spanish/Jewish scale aligns with harmonic minor in minor keys. The chapter introduces key terminology around altered tones, symmetrical scales, and scale-degree functions, then prescribes experimentation with these scales over a cycle-of-fifths track to develop the proper scale usage that defines jazz appeal.
