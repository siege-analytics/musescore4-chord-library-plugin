# Chapter 18 — Scales, Patterns, and Chord Reference

_Distillation; backed by `chapters/ch18.md`._

Chapter 18 teaches systematic scale construction and practice methodology across whole tone, diminished, major, dominant, and minor scales, with emphasis on identifying chord tones (root, 3rd, 5th, 7th, 9th) within each scale up to the ninth. The chapter's characteristic moves include strict taxonomy—only three diminished scales exist, only two whole tone scales—and the prescription of ten canonical warm-up exercises to be practiced across all keys using six distinct root sequences for comprehensive coverage. Load-bearing terminology includes whole tone scale (WHWHWHWH pattern), diminished scale (WHWHWHWH with three instances), chord tone identification, and Dorian minor as a specific modal variant. The chapter emphasizes ear-first internalization through singing before instrumental transfer, positioning voice as the foundational tool for music learning.
