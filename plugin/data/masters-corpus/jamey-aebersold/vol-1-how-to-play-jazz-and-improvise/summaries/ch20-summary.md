# Chapter 20 — Summary

_Distillation; backed by `chapters/ch20.md`._

Chapter 20 establishes memorization and transposition through all 12 keys as the foundational practice discipline, beginning with the II/V7 patterns and blues progression structured as three 4-bar phrases. The chapter articulates the master's characteristic moves: prioritizing what the inner ear hears over ego-driven technique, treating scales and chords as finite and learnable foundations for musical expression, and insisting on proper jazz articulation as a grassroots requirement in every phrase. The load-bearing terminology includes the conceptual model of blues as "three phrases" (beginning, middle, end), and the governing rule "play what your mind hears"—undergirded by lifelong ear training, memorization of melodies and progressions, and the permission to begin now rather than wait for perfection. The chapter culminates in reframing technical knowledge as a means to imagination, positioning sound support, articulation, and listening as non-negotiable practices that enable spontaneous expression.
