# Chapter 1 — Chapter One — No Alterations or Passing Notes

_Distillation; backed by `chapters/ch01.md`._

Chapter One teaches diatonic seventh chord substitution and upper-structure arpeggio selection by shared tones rather than root movement, establishing the book’s central principle through progressively complex rules: the arpeggio-on-the-3rd substitution (m7b5 on dominant sevenths) emerges as the most versatile technique, while 5th- and 7th-rooted options require more caution. The master’s characteristic moves visible here are the ascending-arpeggio-plus-descending-scale melodic formula and the Coltrane 1-2-3-5 pattern (applied forward, reverse, and in the minor variant 1-b3-4-5), both used to break up scalic lines and create interest over fast-tempo chord changes. The chapter introduces load-bearing terminology including shell voicings (root, 3rd, 7th only), quartal harmony (4th-based stacking), and the reframing of common scales—like D Minor Pentatonic—as extended arpeggios (Dm7add11), grounding every technique in harmonic rationale rather than mere fingering habit. A counterpoint principle (large intervals resolve stepwise in opposite direction) and a specific avoidance rule (C note over G7 creates unwanted sus4 tension) round out the chapter’s ruleset, which culminates in a consolidated II-V-I practice assignment that chains all techniques together.
