# Chapter 3 — Chapter Three — Harmonic Minor on the V

_Distillation; backed by `chapters/ch03.md`._

This chapter teaches how to construct melodic lines over harmonic minor changes, particularly on the V chord, using diatonic triads, arpeggios, and bebop counterpoint rules as primary tools. The author demonstrates characteristic moves including the #9 as a chromatic passing note over dominant chords, enclosure formulas (diatonic above, chromatic below), and the technique of targeting the V chord’s third by arpeggiating from the II chord’s fifth. Key terminology introduced includes the bebop melodic counterpoint rule (resolving large intervals stepwise in the opposite direction), motif-based versus target-note approaches to chord navigation, triad pairs with no common notes, and the construction of non-diatonic arpeggios like Bbm7b5 and C7#5 by reordering scale tones from alternate roots. The chapter emphasizes varying melodic ideas through different triad patterns (1-3-5 sequences, quartal arpeggios) and contextual chord-tone omission, showing that ambiguous voicings remain effective when heard within the harmonic framework of the key.
