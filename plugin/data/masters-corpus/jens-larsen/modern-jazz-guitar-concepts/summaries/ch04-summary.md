# Chapter 4 — Chapter Four — Altered Scale

_Distillation; backed by `chapters/ch04.md`._

The altered scale, the seventh mode of the melodic minor scale, creates an "outside" sound over dominant seventh chords and is typically understood through its parent melodic minor relationship—for example, Bb Altered as the seventh mode of B Melodic Minor. The master technique centers on guide tones: the 3rd and 7th of the altered dominant (F and Ab in Bb7alt) are preserved by selecting arpeggios like G#m7b5, which highlights the b9, b13, and 3rd extensions while functioning as a tritone substitute relationship. Shell voicings reduce chords to root, 3rd, and 7th, and triplet rhythms on seventh arpeggios deliver bebop phrasing; chromatic passing notes and rhythmic variation—eighth-note triplets, sixteenth-note subdivisions—embellish arpeggios and suggest unwritten passing chords for forward momentum. Key moves include resolving augmented triads (a bebop trademark), using minor pentatonic from the 3rd over tonic major chords, and deploying four-note chromatic enclosures to suspend and delay arrival on target chord tones. Load-bearing terms include altered dominant, guide tones, tritone substitute, shell voicing, chromatic passing notes, and chromatic enclosure.
