# Chapter 5 — Chapter Five — Diminished Scale on the Dom7th Chord

_Distillation; backed by `chapters/ch05.md`._

Chapter Five teaches the half-whole diminished scale—an eight-note symmetrical construction built from two interlocking diminished 7th chords a semitone apart—and its primary application as upper-structure triads over dominant 7th chords, particularly the idiomatic 13b9 voicing. The master’s characteristic pedagogical move is isolating the four major triads contained within the scale (F, Ab, B, D) and teaching them through positional cycling and neck-crossing exercises rather than scalar runs, leveraging the scale’s inherent minor 3rd symmetry to reduce learning load. Load-bearing terms introduced include the half-whole diminished scale formula (1 b9 #9 3 b5 5 6 b7), upper-structure triad, the 13b9 dominant extension, stacking triads for melodic range, and the connection to the altered scale through the B major triad’s shared intervals. The chapter emphasizes voice-leading transitions between triads—particularly how Ab and D major triads together convey F7 tensions of #9, b9, and 13—and frames triad exercises as tests of melodic logic and neck navigation rather than mechanistic patterns.
