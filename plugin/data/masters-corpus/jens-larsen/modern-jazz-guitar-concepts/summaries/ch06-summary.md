# Chapter 6 — Chapter Six — Putting It All Together

_Distillation; backed by `chapters/ch06.md`._

Chapter Six synthesizes the melodic techniques developed throughout the book by demonstrating how to construct sophisticated solo lines using triad pairs, arpeggios, and scale embellishments over jazz chord changes. The master’s characteristic moves visible here include voice-leading triads by common notes and encirclement, combining non-diatonic triad pairs (like B and Ab major over F7) to generate specific harmonic extensions, and deploying signature inversion-skip patterns attributed to canonical guitarists like George Benson and Grant Green. Load-bearing terminology introduced includes quintal arpeggios (stacks of fifths), encirclement as a voice-leading device, the harmonic minor as a dominant pull toward the next chord, and the principle of repeated melodic statements with slight alteration to fit new harmonies. The chapter anchors learning through detailed bar-by-bar analysis of a complete solo, explicitly inviting students to develop multiple valid interpretations of the same phrase rather than memorizing a single reading. Practice assignments emphasize triad pair improvisation and experimentation with open voicings to internalize fretboard connections across the instrument.
