# Chapter 1 — Notes Per Bar

_Distillation; backed by `chapters/ch01.md`._

Chapter 1 introduces the foundational principle that rhythmic sophistication and effectiveness are distinct goals, with the master's characteristic move toward polyrhythmic thinking through note groupings independent of bar-line structure. The chapter teaches that practitioners should develop awareness of note density and bar position by experimenting with different rhythms per bar, then advance to playing specific note groupings across bar lines to generate new rhythmic ideas. A core pedagogical insight that internal mental grouping of notes affects articulation and musical phrasing even when invisible to the listener connects thought process directly to physical execution. The chapter establishes five-note groupings as the entry point into hearing polyrhythms, with the rule that identical note groupings can occupy flexible durations (1 beat to 4+ beats). The 22 one-bar rhythm exercise provides embodied practice scaffolding deliberately limited to quarter and eighth notes, omitting sixteenth notes and triplets to sequence learning deliberately.
