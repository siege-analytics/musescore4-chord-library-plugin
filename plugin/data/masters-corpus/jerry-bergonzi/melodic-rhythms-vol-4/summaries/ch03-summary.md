# Chapter 3 — Eighth-note rhythms

_Distillation; backed by `chapters/ch03.md`._

Chapter 3 teaches the eighth-note displacement method: students play four consecutive eighth notes and systematically displace their starting point across eight positions in the bar. The chapter introduces the harmony anticipation rule, where later displacements (groupings #6, #7, #8) sound the next chord by approaching its notes or stating it directly, with beat-5 groupings automatically anticipating harmony on their final eighth note. Load-bearing terms include displacement, anticipation, and superimposition, the last extending the method into metric complexity through 3/4 patterns played across 4/4 bars. The progression moves from isolated one-bar patterns through combination into multi-bar phrases, then to random beat placement. A deliberate notational choice keeps eighth-note downbeats even where quarter notes would suffice, preserving transparency of the underlying logic. Displacement functions as a generative principle producing harmonic motion without explicit chord changes.
