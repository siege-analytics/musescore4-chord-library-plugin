# Chapter 4 — Playing five consecutive eighth notes

_Distillation; backed by `chapters/ch04.md`._

Chapter 4 teaches the systematic practice of playing five consecutive eighth notes beginning on different beat positions using Tunes 1, 2, and 3, with attention to harmonic choices when the pattern crosses bar lines: entries #5, #6, and #7 can anticipate the next bar, function as approach notes, or sound the chord of the moment. The pedagogical progression moves from structured drilling across all eight entry points to mixing them up randomly, developing what the master calls a window in the mind that hears bigger spaces of time and form awareness through polyrhythmic perception. Load-bearing terminology includes entry points (the eight positions where five notes can begin) and the harmonic anticipation principle at bar-line crossings. The chapter's characteristic move embeds technical discipline of position-by-position practice within an explicit goal of perceptual freedom.
