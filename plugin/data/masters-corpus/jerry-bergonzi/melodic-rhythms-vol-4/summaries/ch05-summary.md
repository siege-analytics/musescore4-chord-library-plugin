# Chapter 5 — Playing six consecutive eighth notes

_Distillation; backed by `chapters/ch05.md`._

Chapter 5 teaches polyrhythmic superimposition: layering odd meters (3/4, 5/4, 7/4, 9/4) over a 4/4 foundation, including starting patterns on the upbeat to create off-beat phrasing. The load-bearing concept is that practicing these cross-rhythmic devices trains the ear to internalize larger structural form, opening awareness of how musicians navigate complex time relationships. The characteristic move is systematic introduction of increasingly complex odd-meter overlays that expand polyrhythmic vocabulary. The chapter is purely instructional technique work aimed at developing form awareness through rhythmic displacement.
