# Chapter 6 — Playing seven consecutive eighth notes

_Distillation; backed by `chapters/ch06.md`._

Chapter 6 teaches seven consecutive eighth notes and their three harmonic functions depending on bar position: anticipating the next chord, serving as approach notes, or sounding the chord of the moment. Techniques include mastering multiple starting points through randomization drills, then layering 7/4-over-4/4 and 5/4-over-4/4 polymetric patterns. The characteristic move integrates cross-rhythmic devices training the player to internalize rhythmic independence. The load-bearing terminology covers the functional harmonic roles of consecutive eighth notes and the polymetric concept of playing against the underlying time signature, which students practice by returning to foundational exercises while applying these time-signature devices.
