# Chapter 8 — Playing two consecutive eighth notes

_Distillation; backed by `chapters/ch08.md`._

Chapter 8 teaches systematic mastery of two consecutive eighth notes through progressive rhythmic displacement and polyrhythmic superimposition. Core drills establish a foundational grid for precise rhythmic placement across four starting beats and their and subdivisions. The pedagogical arc escalates through random beat starting practice, which synthesizes rote drills into internalized rhythmic flexibility, followed by increasingly complex metric superimpositions: 7/4, 6/4, and 11/4 patterns played over a 4/4 foundation. Load-bearing terminology includes polyrhythm, metric superimposition, and time-signature devices. The final synthesis step circles back to random starts now enriched by these polyrhythmic tools, completing a loop from mechanical precision to expressive rhythmic independence.
