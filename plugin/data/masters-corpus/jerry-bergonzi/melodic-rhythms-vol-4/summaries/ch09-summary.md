# Chapter 9 — Triplet groupings

_Distillation; backed by `chapters/ch09.md`._

Chapter 9 teaches triplet groupings as a foundational rhythmic concept, beginning with practicing three consecutive eighth-note triplets starting at different metrical positions within a bar. The chapter introduces superimposing odd-meter groupings over a steady 4/4 pulse, allowing players to develop polyrhythmic independence and displacement awareness. A key technical rule across exercises 10-13 is the selective silencing of triplet subdivisions, using only four of six available notes from two consecutive eighth-note triplets to develop rhythmic precision and selective articulation. Triplet groupings and superimposition serve as load-bearing rhythmic vocabulary for more advanced phrasing work.
