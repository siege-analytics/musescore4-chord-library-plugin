# Chapter 10 — Five consecutive eighth-note triplets

_Distillation; backed by `chapters/ch10.md`._

Chapter 10 teaches internalization of consecutive eighth-note triplets through two progressive exercises: melodic displacement, where a four-note melody is played starting on different beats within triplet groupings to build rhythmic fluency; and randomized triplet groupings, which escalates practice from structured patterns to unpredictable application. The instructional approach moves from controlled position-based practice to random variation, emphasizing transcending mechanical counting and developing intuitive feel for triplet subdivisions. Key terminology includes melodic displacement as a pedagogical device for embedding triplet internalization and randomized groupings as the advanced stage of mastery.
