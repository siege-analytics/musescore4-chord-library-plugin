# Chapter 11 — Six consecutive eighth-note triplets

_Distillation; backed by `chapters/ch11.md`._

Chapter 11 teaches six consecutive eighth-note triplets through structured practice anchored to Tune 5. It introduces superimposing 7/4 triplet phrases over a 4/4 framework, exploring polyrhythmic complexity. Core methods progress from fixed rhythmic patterns to random triplet improvisation, emphasizing internalization through repetition and free-form application. Load-bearing terminology includes eighth-note triplets, 7/4 over 4/4, and practicing rhythmic patterns both systematically and spontaneously.
