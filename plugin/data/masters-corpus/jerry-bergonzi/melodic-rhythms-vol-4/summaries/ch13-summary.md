# Chapter 13 — Three consecutive eighth-note triplets

_Distillation; backed by `chapters/ch13.md`._

Chapter 13 covers three consecutive eighth-note triplets and broader rhythmic expansion: converting eighth-note exercises to sixteenth-note patterns by doubling up (creating double-time vocabulary), the 2/4-to-4/4 repetition rule fitting them into jazz contexts, and mastering hemiola, playing 3/4 rhythmic phrases over 4/4 tunes. The characteristic move builds rhythmic awareness through deliberate practice: conscious counting and foot-tapping train the improviser to hear larger phrase structures (four, eight, sixteen bars) and begin lines from varied beats. Load-bearing terms include hemiola, the precise arithmetic that four bars of 3/4 equals three bars of 4/4, and the distinction between conscious practice and subconscious mastery it builds.
