# Chapter 16 — Hemiovals

_Distillation; backed by `chapters/ch16.md`._

Chapter 16 introduces hemiovals, a rhythmic mirroring technique that creates polymetric effects through displacement. Two constructive rules are taught: a 5/4 mirror rhythm is generated by starting the same rhythm on beat 2 and that was started on beat 1, while a 7/4 mirror rhythm comes from displacing to beat 3 and. The characteristic move: systematic offset of a single pattern produces complex metrical relationships. Load-bearing terminology is mirror rhythm and hemioval, denoting patterns that create the illusion of metric modulation through strategic repetition and timing shifts rather than changes to the underlying pulse.
