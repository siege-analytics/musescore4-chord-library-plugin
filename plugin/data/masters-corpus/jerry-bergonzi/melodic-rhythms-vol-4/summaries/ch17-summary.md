# Chapter 17 — 5/4 rhythms over 4/4

_Distillation; backed by `chapters/ch17.md`._

Chapter 17 teaches superimposing 4/4 rhythms over a 3/4 harmonic context, using the 22 one-bar rhythms from Chapter 2 as foundational material. The directive covers simple superposition (one 4/4 rhythm against the 3/4 tune) and compositional extension (combining two 4/4 rhythms into longer polyrhythmic phrases). This introduces cross-meter thinking: maintaining metric independence between the rhythmic figure and the underlying harmonic framework. Load-bearing concept is the one-bar rhythmic unit as a modular building block deployable across different metric contexts.
