# Chapter 18 — 7/4 rhythms over 4/4

_Distillation; backed by `chapters/ch18.md`._

Chapter 18 teaches the coordination between eighth notes and triplet-based feels, the foundation of playing in the pocket with a jazz rhythm section, alongside the 7/4 over 4/4 superimposition. Loaded terms include pocket (eighth-note placement relative to triplet pulse), laying back (delaying note attacks), and lob (another rhythmic articulation tool). The characteristic move is the coexistence principle: eighth notes should not become literal triplets but coexist with the triplet feel, distinguishing authentic pocket playing from mechanical rhythmic mimicry. The chapter builds this feel through alternating practice (triplet rhythms then eighth notes on the same tune) to internalize the groove rather than intellectualize it.
