# Chapter 19 — 4/4 rhythms over 3/4

_Distillation; backed by `chapters/ch19.md`._

Chapter 19 teaches 4/4 rhythms over 3/4 by presenting five interchangeable time zones or gears, expanding vocabulary through systematic gear-switching of melodic lines. The characteristic move is the practical truncation rule for fitting eight-note phrases into triplet subdivisions: leaving off either the first or last four notes to maintain phrasing logic. Load-bearing terminology includes time zones as distinct rhythmic subdivisions (quarter-note triplet, eighth-note, triplet, sixteenth-note, sextuplet gears). The chapter establishes a groove-internalization method: repeat lines at metronome 50-60 on the half note until triplets feel natural when phrased with eighth-note articulation. The rhythms here are twice as long as those in Chapter 20, establishing a structural relationship across the method.
