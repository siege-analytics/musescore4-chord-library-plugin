# Chapter 22 — Time zones (polyrhythms)

_Distillation; backed by `chapters/ch22.md`._

Chapter 22 teaches time zones / polyrhythms through temporal displacement: constructing and feeling multiple time signatures layered against a steady four-beat foundation. Three primary construction methods are introduced: additive (building 9 notes by adding to eighth-note patterns), subtractive (removing notes to create 10-note or 12-note groupings), and displacement (offsetting phrases by eighth-note triplet intervals). The characteristic move is feel-first learning: polyrhythmic awareness develops through repetition and articulation variation in the foundational 5-against-4 exercise. Load-bearing terminology centers on polyrhythmic ratios (5, 9, 10, 12 notes over 4 beats) and construction vocabulary (additive, subtractive, offset).
