# Chapter 23 — More time zones

_Distillation; backed by `chapters/ch23.md`._

Chapter 23 (More Time Zones) teaches polyrhythmic approaches to scale sequences by applying odd-grouping subdivisions (5, 7, 9) to scalar material, establishing frameworks like 5/2 four-note sequences and nine-note patterns whose beat span depends on tempo. Modes written in quintuplets extend polyrhythmic practice into fragmented forms: broken quintuplets, septuplets, nonuplets, each deployable across two, three, or four-beat metric contexts. Key terminology: quintuplets, septuplets, nonuplets, elastic time, and the concept of rhythmic ear training as distinct from melodic practice. The characteristic move is systematic exploration of multiple time zones, treating the same material across different metric containers. The chapter emphasizes that polyrhythmic mastery is a long-range pursuit, not a weekly drill.
