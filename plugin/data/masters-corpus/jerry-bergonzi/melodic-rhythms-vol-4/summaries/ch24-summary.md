# Chapter 24 — Broken quintuplets, septuplets, and nonuplets

_Distillation; backed by `chapters/ch24.md`._

Chapter 24 (Broken quintuplets, septuplets, nonuplets) teaches the practice and perception of irregular note groupings through a three-stage approach: any notes, then single chord, finally over tunes. Expansion and contraction are introduced as twin cognitive tools: taking a fixed group of notes and reinterpreting it across different beat spans, from one beat to four beats, or compressed into 1 1/2 beats, dissolves the strangeness of odd tuplets. The characteristic move is metric displacement, where exercises cross the 4/4 bar line by starting early or late, building polyrhythmic independence. Load-bearing terminology centers on expansion/contraction as a reframing strategy positioning irregular tuplets as flexible rescalings of familiar melodic shapes.
