# Chapter 28 — Rhythmizing Scale Sequences

_Distillation; backed by `chapters/ch28.md`._

Chapter 28 (Rhythmizing scale sequences) teaches systematically varying a core pattern through modal transposition across scale degrees, note omission, rests, directional changes, and varied entry points to expand aural vocabulary. Characteristic moves center on using sus4 chords to isolate pure scale relationships without avoid-note constraints, and generating unexpected polyrhythmic groupings (groups of seven from strategic note omission). Key terminology: modal transposition, note omission, polyrhythmic approaches applied to sequences. Practice methodology emphasizes internalization first (familiarizing with the sequence), then deliberate exploration through 12 composed modal sequences played over backing tracks.
