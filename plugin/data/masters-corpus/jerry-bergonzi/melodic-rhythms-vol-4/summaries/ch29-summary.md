# Chapter 29 — Conceptual Practice and Rhythmic Grammar

_Distillation; backed by `chapters/ch29.md`._

Chapter 29 (Conceptual Practice and Rhythmic Grammar) establishes six core constraints on beat placement: never starting or ending on beat one, varying between predominantly eighth notes and passages without them, and manipulating chord anticipation (from half-beat to three-beat offsets) or late resolution (one to four beats). The chapter teaches that silence and endings are active compositional choices: rests frame the listener's attention, and knowing when to end a solo (leaving periods at the ends of musical sentences and paragraphs) invites the next idea rather than forcing continuation. Together these principles form a rhythmic vocabulary for storytelling on the instrument.
