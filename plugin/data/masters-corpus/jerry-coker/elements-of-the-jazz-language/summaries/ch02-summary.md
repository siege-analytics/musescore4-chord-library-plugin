# Chapter 2 — DIGITAL PATTERNS AND SCALAR PATTERNS

_Distillation; backed by `chapters/ch02.md`._

Chapter 2 introduces **change-running** (chord-arpeggiating), a foundational improvisation device with five musical functions: tuning the improviser's ear to chord structure, learning chord progressions, providing pickup notes, clarifying harmony to the audience, and reinforcing form for the ensemble. The chapter establishes the core pedagogical method of **digital analysis**—thinking in scale degrees (1-3-5-1 rather than note names) to achieve key-universality, and mandates practicing all exercises in 12 keys with accompaniment tracks sequenced to mirror real-tune harmonic motion (circle-of-fifths and chromatic progressions). Change-running phrases follow three structural freedoms: they need not begin on the root, may omit chord members, and move in any direction (ascending, descending, or both). The chapter prescribes a three-stage practice progression (alone, with exercise track, with play-along tune) and demands complete arpeggiation regimens across four transposition sequences and five extension levels. Critically, students must internalize patterns through mental and aural skill rather than notation, and should supplement structured practice with transcription study—learning how master improvisers deploy these devices by ear alone.
