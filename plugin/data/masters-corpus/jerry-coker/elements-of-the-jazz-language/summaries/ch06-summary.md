# Chapter 6 — BEBOP LICK

_Distillation; backed by `chapters/ch06.md`._

Chapter 6 introduces the **bebop scale** (David Baker's term and discovery) — a major, dorian, or mixolydian scale with one specific chromatic note added, yielding an eight-note scale. The chapter explains the historical-rhythmic rationale: an eight-note scale fits 4/4 time exactly (four beats of eighth notes) and lands chord tones on downbeats, unlike the seven-note scale. Coker specifies the precise location of the added chromatic note for each variant — between 5 and 6 in major, between 3 and 4 in dorian, and between 7 and 8 in mixolydian — and notes two surprising empirical facts: the chromatic note is ~99% consistent across transcribed solos, and in the dorian and mixolydian forms it is a perfect anachronism (major 3rd against minor 7 chord, major 7 against dominant 7) that contradicts the underlying chord quality. The chapter also covers practice context (cycle of fifths, II-V chains) for the 3-b9 device introduced previously, plus a substitution rule (Track AA uses half-diminished for minor seventh, altered for unaltered dominants) and a worked register-change decision for Exercise 138.
