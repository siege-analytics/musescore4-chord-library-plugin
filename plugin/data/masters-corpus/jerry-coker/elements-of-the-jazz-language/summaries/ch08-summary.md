# Chapter 8 — ENCLOSURE

_Distillation; backed by `chapters/ch08.md`._

Chapter 8 introduces the **bebop lick** — a specific melodic phrase, closely related to the bebop scale and using a portion of that scale, that typically occurs on dominant seventh and minor seventh chords. Coker credits David Baker with the discovery, naming, and codification of this device through analysis of Bebop-Era and later solos, framing the lick as an emergent (not composed) idiom rather than a deliberately taught construct. The pedagogical sequence is explicit: learn the lick in isolated form first against tracks with a preponderance of minor seventh and dominant seventh chords, then learn to weave it into longer melodic lines preceded and followed by non-bebop-lick substance. Coker extends the lick's application beyond its basic harmonic context by showing it can be used to imply tri-tone substitutions or altered dominants, a forward-pointing concept the book treats in detail later. The chapter calibrates the student's expectations: the lick has low acquisition cost due to its brevity and structural simplicity, and high stylistic payoff — a strong implicit argument for prioritizing it in practice.
