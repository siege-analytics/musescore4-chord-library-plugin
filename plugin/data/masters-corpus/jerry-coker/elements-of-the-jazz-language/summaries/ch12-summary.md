# Chapter 12 — "CRY ME A RIVER" LICK

_Distillation; backed by `chapters/ch12.md`._

Chapter 12 is predominantly notation — CESH patterns to practice in all keys against play-along exercise tracks. The only load-bearing prose extends the CESH harmonic-context rule: the supposed setting is a minor chord of long duration, but improvisers commonly use CESH on II-7 chords, on V7 chords (with the minor CESH built on the fifth of the V7, as G-minor CESH over a C7), or across a combined II-V. The chapter is otherwise a reference set of CESH patterns that the student internalizes through transposition and play-along practice rather than through additional explanatory prose.
