# Chapter 17 — APPENDIX B - APPLYING ELEMENTS OF THE JAZZ LANGUAGE TO TUNE PROGRESSIONS

_Distillation; backed by `chapters/ch17.md`._

Chapter 17 (Appendix B prose) establishes the book's master practice methodology and the conventions for the annotated tune progressions. Coker's foundational rule is that elements must be ingrained into the mind, ears, and hands — reading the book is insufficient and the memory loses what the hands have not learned. He defines a four-stage assimilation process: (1) mental understanding through definitions and illustrations, (2) motivational reinforcement by hearing the element in master solos, (3) hands-on practice in all keys, tempos, and registers, (4) ear-to-harmony association through play-along exercises (Appendix A), culminating in (5) application to real tune progressions (Appendix B). Each of the three included progressions appears in two versions — a first version with only chord symbols and recommended scales for harmonic internalization, then an annotated second version showing element placement — so the student moves from contrivance to spontaneous recognition. The chapter codifies notational conventions: scales may not start on chord roots (the root is the weakest chord tone), enharmonic spellings minimize accidentals, a single scale covers a one-measure II-V (a form of harmonic generalization), and filled-in noteheads flag chords shorter than four beats. A master abbreviation legend (7-3, BBL, HG, CMAR, GBNF, TT/AD, BD, #II°) plus parenthetical-annotation parsing rules (with worked examples) decode the second-version charts. Coker explicitly lists the elements omitted from annotations because their use is location-independent (sequences, change-running, digital patterns, scalar patterns, bebop scale, enclosures, quotes, linear chromaticism, bar-line shifts, side-slipping), warning that absence from charts does not mean lesser importance. Footnotes provide a Back-Door substitution melodic rule (CMAR or GBNF starting on the 9 of the substitute, BBL on the 4) and explicitly authorize voice-leading-justified GBNF use beyond its canonical context.
