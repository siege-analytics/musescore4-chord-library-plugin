## Major vs. minor triad bottom rule

> any of the patterns which follow, having the numbers (like 1-2-3-5) could be projected
> over any tune’s entire length, when the minor forms of these patterns have been added to the fol-
> lowing major forms, since nearly all chords contained in the tune’s progression will have either a
> major triad on the bottom (i.e., 1-2-3-5) or a minor triad (i.e., 1-2-flat 3-5).

**Significance (p. 29).** This is the book's governing harmonic rule: virtually all chords reduce to major- or minor-triad bottom structures, so mastering these two pattern families covers nearly all real-world chord changes.
