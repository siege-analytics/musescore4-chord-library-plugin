## Minor chord numerical formulas

> The following four chords: Minor Triad, Minor Sixth Chord, Minor Seventh Chord and Minor Ninth
> Chord will be formed by applying a numerical formula to the B Dorian Mode. The corresponding
> alphabetical symbols will also be indicated.

**Significance (p. 62).** This passage establishes the method's core approach: all minor chord types are derived from a single Dorian Mode via numerical formulas, unifying the chapter's technique under one system.

## Minor Triad formula (1-3-5)

> By extracting the tones No. !-3-5 from the B Dorian Mode, we arrive at the notes forming the B
> Minor Triad.

**Significance (p. 62).** Defines the foundational minor triad construction rule from which all subsequent minor chord extensions are built.

## Minor Sixth Chord formula (1-3-5-6)

> Tones No. 1-3-5-6 of the B Dorian Mode form the B Minor Sixth Chord.

**Significance (p. 62).** Gives the precise numerical formula that distinguishes the minor sixth chord from the basic triad, teaching the extension pattern.

## Dorian Mode octave number system

> The B Dorian Mode is illustrated in two octaves. The numbers under each cone indicate the position
> of that tone in this scale. Note that beyond the first octave, tone No. 8, the tones have the same
> letter names and the numbers corresponding to these tones refer to the respective distances from the
> starting tone, No. 1.

**Significance (p. 62).** Explains how the book's numbering system extends across octaves, which is essential for correctly interpreting any formula that includes tones above 7.
