## Minor chord-scale relationship

> This chord tone chart organizes the tones of all Dorian Modes in the order needed for the construc-
> tion of the four types of Minor Chords. Reading up from the chord root C (also indicated as tone No.
> 1), we see the tones C, E-Flat, G, B-Flat, D, F, A, which are all the notes in the C Dorian Mode. Note
> that the ninth, eleventh, and thirteenth are also the second, fourth and sixth tones respectively, as
> they are the same letter names.

**Significance (p. 65).** This passage defines the foundational rule that minor seventh/ninth chord tones are derived from the Dorian mode, and clarifies the enharmonic equivalence between upper extensions and lower scale degrees.

## Minor chord practice routine

> In preparation for the subsequent patterns, be able to recite, write and play the following chords as
> they are extracted from every Dorian Mode: Minor Triads, Minor Sixth Chords, Minor Seventh
> Chords and Minor Ninth Chords. Use the illustrated routine form for playing the chord tones. It
> does not require any specific rhythm or tempo.

**Significance (p. 65).** This passage prescribes the method's characteristic practice approach — learning all four minor chord types across every Dorian mode root, and specifies that execution is rhythm-free (pure chord-tone drill).
