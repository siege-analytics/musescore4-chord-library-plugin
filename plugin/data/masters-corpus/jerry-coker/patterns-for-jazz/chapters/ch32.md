## fourths in improvisation

> Fourth intervals have become extremely popular among improvisers, in recent years, both because
> the interval interests them and because fourths tend to break away from the sound and rigid fun-
> ction of thirds heard in ordinary ruminations on chords built in thirds.

**Significance (p. 143).** This passage defines the musical rationale for using fourth intervals — breaking away from chord-tone thirds — which is the conceptual foundation for the exercises that follow.

## scale-based fourth intervals

> The following
> studies will help prepare the student to play general fourth intervals (sometimes perfect, sometimes
> augmented ) as they are determined by the tones of major and minor scales.

**Significance (p. 143).** This sentence establishes that the exercises use diatonic (not purely perfect) fourths derived from scale tones, defining the harmonic logic governing the entire drill set.
