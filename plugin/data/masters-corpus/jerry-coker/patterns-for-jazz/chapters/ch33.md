## Lydian Augmented Scale purpose

> The
> LYDIAN AUGMENTED SCALE is an extremely useful scale in this respect. It has two kinds of
> application to dominant seventh chords, one resulting in the addition of a flatted fifth (or aug-
> mented eleventh) and the other application supplying an augmented fifth, a flatted ninth, an aug-
> mented ninth, and an augmented eleventh.

**Significance (p. 145).** This defines the scale's two distinct harmonic functions over dominant seventh chords, which determines when and how a player chooses to deploy it.

## Root position: dominant 7th flat 5

> In the case of the dominant 7th, flat 5th (b 5), the root of the scale is the seventh of the chord.

**Significance (p. 146).** This rule specifies the exact scale-to-chord alignment for the first application, telling the player where to start the scale relative to the chord root.

## Root position: dominant 7th altered extensions

> In the case of the dominant 7th, raised 5th, raised 9th, lowered 9th, and augmented 11th, the root
> of the scale is the third of the chord.

**Significance (p. 146).** This rule specifies the second, distinct scale-to-chord alignment for the altered-extensions application, requiring a different starting pitch than the first use case.

## Learning all twelve keys

> There are 12 lydian augmented scales.
>
> Learn all twelve scales by playing them over and over throughout the range of your instrument.
> Then learn them as you did the major scale (by playing them in thirds, etc.). Be sure to practice
> the scales in all twelve keys.

**Significance (p. 146).** This is the method's explicit practice prescription, establishing both the scope (all twelve) and the method (range + intervallic patterns) for internalizing the scale.
