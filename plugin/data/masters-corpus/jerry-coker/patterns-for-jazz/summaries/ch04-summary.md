# Chapter 4 — Major Scales

_Distillation; backed by `chapters/ch04.md`._

Chapter 4 establishes that the major triad, M6, M7, and M9 chords all derive from the same parent scale—the major scale of the chord root—unifying these four chord qualities under a single organizational principle. The chapter introduces a notational convenience: triad symbols in patterns are used interchangeably since all related chords share the common major scale, preventing students from misinterpreting chord symbols as prescriptive quality markers. This chapter marks a structural pivot in the method, shifting focus from chord-tone extraction exercises to scale patterns themselves, moving students from practicing individual chord types to exploring the major scale as an independent pattern-learning domain.
