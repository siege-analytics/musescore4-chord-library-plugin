# Chapter 5 — Digital Patterns in Major

_Distillation; backed by `chapters/ch05.md`._

Chapter 5 teaches Digital Patterns in Major through three interconnected principles: the alternating ascending/descending application of chord scales across patterns 34–36, establishing a generative technique that students must apply independently rather than memorize mechanically; the CM chord family usage rule, which contextualizes each major digital pattern to specific harmonic qualities (C, CM6, CM7, CM9), grounding the patterns in real harmonic deployment; and the standing directive to practice in all keys, reinforcing the method's foundational demand for chromatic internalization. Together, these topics shift the student from pattern imitation to pattern mastery—alternation makes the patterns flexible, harmonic specification makes them purposeful, and transposition makes them universal.
