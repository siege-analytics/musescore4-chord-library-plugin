# Chapter 6 — Major Scale Intervals

_Distillation; backed by `chapters/ch06.md`._

Chapter 6 establishes scale-degree numbers as the method's core abstraction for transposition, teaching students to recognize chord-scale relationships through numeric patterns like 1-2-3-5 (major triad) and 1-2-flat 3-5 (minor triad) that can be applied to any chord root regardless of pitch. The chapter introduces two complementary fragments—the ascending 1-2-3-5 pattern and the descending 5-3-2-1 pattern—and their directional rules, which together form the vocabulary for nearly all chord progressions in real tunes. Key harmonic scope rules determine pattern application: the 1-2-3-5 pattern works on any chord with a major third, while specialized patterns govern minor chords descending in whole steps, allowing students to project these numeric templates across an entire tune's changes. This governing rule—that virtually all chords reduce to either major- or minor-triad bottom structures—makes mastery of these two pattern families sufficient for real-world chord accompaniment.
