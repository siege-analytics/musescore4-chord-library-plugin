# Chapter 7 — Patterns with Embellishing (non-harmonic) tones

_Distillation; backed by `chapters/ch07.md`._

Chapter 7 teaches how to build patterns using chord scale fragments that incorporate non-harmonic (embellishing) tones alongside the core triad notes. The chapter systematizes pattern construction through reorderable fragments like 1-3-5-3, 5-3-1-3, and 3-5-2-1, each named to clarify which scale degrees are used and how they expand beyond the triad's basic tones. A key load-bearing concept emerges in the two-fragment major chord coverage rule: when two fragments (5-8-6-4 and 2-5-3-1) are combined, a single pattern covers all major chord qualities—triad, M6, M7, and M9—demonstrating how systematic reordering and extension of scale-degree sequences generates harmonic completeness. This chapter makes embellishing tones a structural element rather than ornamental, organizing them through the method's characteristic approach of naming and reusing chord-scale fragments.
