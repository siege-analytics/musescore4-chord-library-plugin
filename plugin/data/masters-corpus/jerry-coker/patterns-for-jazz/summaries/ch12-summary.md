# Chapter 12 — Minor Sixth Chords

_Distillation; backed by `chapters/ch12.md`._

Chapter 12 teaches the formation of minor sixth chords by applying the Dorian Mode parent key rule, the method's foundational principle requiring students to relate each Dorian mode to its parent major key before applying chord formulas. The chapter introduces numerical formulas for building extended minor chords—minor seventh (tones 1-3-5-7) and minor ninth (tones 1-3-5-7-9)—showing how scale degrees extend the basic triad. A key insight unifies all four minor chord types: they share the same triad foundation and derive from the same Dorian mode scale, establishing both procedural consistency and sonic coherence. This chapter anchors the student's understanding that minor chord construction is inseparable from modal thinking and parent-key relationships.
