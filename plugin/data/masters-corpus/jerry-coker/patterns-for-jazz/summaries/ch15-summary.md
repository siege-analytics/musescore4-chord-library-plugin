# Chapter 15 — Digital Patterns on Minor Seventh Chords

_Distillation; backed by `chapters/ch15.md`._

Chapter 15 introduces digital patterns played across minor seventh chords by establishing the Dorian mode as the unified scale underlying all four minor chord qualities: minor triad, m6, m7, and m9. The pedagogical core of this chapter reveals that since Cm, Cm6, Cm7, and Cm9 all derive their tones from the same C Dorian mode, a single fingering pattern can serve multiple chord symbols—a practical insight the author uses to justify labeling all patterns with the minor seventh chord symbol for convenience. By shifting focus from individual chord tones to the scale (Dorian mode) itself, the chapter equips students to understand how modal thinking unifies seemingly different minor chord types under a common technical foundation. This represents a fundamental shift from chord-centric to scale-centric practice, allowing students to master one pattern and apply it across the minor seventh chord family.
