# Chapter 16 — Patterns for the IIm7—V7 Progression

_Distillation; backed by `chapters/ch16.md`._

Chapter 16 teaches the IIm7–V7–IM7 progression as the foundational harmonic unit for jazz improvisation, establishing how seventh chords function by scale degree in major keys and why a single parent scale (Dorian mode for the IIm7, Major scale for the IM7) covers all three chords with shared key signature. The chapter introduces the load-bearing concept of chord-group reading—thinking in related harmonic units rather than single chords—as the skill that enables idiomatic improvisation and spontaneity. Students are directed to memorize the all-keys IIm7–V7 chart (Figure 27) as a prerequisite, then apply this reference through a series of patterns (123, 124) that use scale-degree numbers and modal sources to develop transposition fluency. The practical heuristic that M7 typically functions as IM7 and m7 as IIm7 reduces decision burden in real-time playing. Throughout, the chapter's characteristic move is pedagogical scaffolding: from foundational rules (seventh chord qualities by scale degree) through explicit pattern work and memorization, training the ear and hand to recognize and improvise idiomatically over this ubiquitous progression in standard tunes.
