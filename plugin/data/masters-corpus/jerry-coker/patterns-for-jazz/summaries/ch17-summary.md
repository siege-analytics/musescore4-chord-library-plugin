# Chapter 17 — Patterns for the IIm7—V7—I Progression

_Distillation; backed by `chapters/ch17.md`._

Chapter 17 teaches systematic patterns for improvising over the IIm7–V7–I progression by extracting scalar and chord-tone fragments from the underlying chord scales. The foundational approach involves fragments like 1-2-3-5 extracted from each chord's scale, complemented by chord-tone derivation methods and two-fragment substitution rules (3-4-5-7 from the minor seventh, 3-4-5-1 from the dominant seventh) that are practiced across multiple pattern iterations. A key insight introduced is the shared parent-key principle: because IIm7 and V7 chords share the same parent tonality, a single scale can span both chords while preserving harmonic clarity. The chapter extends one-measure IIm7-V7 patterns to two-measure progressions by recycling previously mastered fragments and applying directional scale assignments (ascending for IIm7, descending for V7), teaching compositional economy through generative extension rather than introducing entirely new patterns.
