# Chapter 18 — Augmented Triads

_Distillation; backed by `chapters/ch18.md`._

Chapter 18 introduces the whole tone scale, a unique non-diatonic collection defined by whole steps between every scale degree, which has no relationship to key. The foundational construction rule states that a whole tone scale is produced by placing a whole step between each note until reaching the octave (scale degree 7). Students must understand the enharmonic spelling convention that resolves the notational paradox where the interval between scale degrees 1 and 7 appears as an augmented seventh but is theoretically an octave. The chapter establishes the critical principle that only two distinct whole tone pitch collections exist: all twelve possible starting pitches generate scales containing identical note sets, with only the starting tone differing. This economy of pitch collections—six scales sharing one set, six sharing another—defines how the whole tone scale functions across all chromatic starting points.
