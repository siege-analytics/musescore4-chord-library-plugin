# Chapter 19 — Whole-tone Scale

_Distillation; backed by `chapters/ch19.md`._

Chapter 19 introduces the whole-tone scale as a source for deriving two essential chromatic harmony chords: the Augmented Triad (extracted via tones 1–3–5) and the Augmented Seventh Chord (via tones 1–3–5–6). The chapter establishes enharmonic equivalence as a foundational rule, permitting substitution of enharmonically equivalent tones (e.g., B-sharp for C) to maintain proper chord spelling and access the whole-tone scale's harmonic material without orthographic confusion. A key conceptual corrective warns students not to miscount the augmented triad as having six tones when its chart representation appears to span six positions due to enharmonic respelling—the correct spelling of E Augmented Triad is E–G♯–B♯ (rendered as E–G♯–C in the chart). Both the Augmented Triad and Augmented Seventh Chord share the same parent triad and parent scale, establishing a unifying principle for voice-leading and harmonic substitution within whole-tone harmony.
