# Chapter 20 — Diminished Triads

_Distillation; backed by `chapters/ch20.md`._

Chapter 20 introduces diminished triads through the lens of the shared parent scale governing both minor seventh and dominant seventh chords—a theoretical principle that allows a single scalar pattern to voice both chords of a IIm7-V7 progression across two measures. The chapter defines specific fragment assignments (3-4-5-7 from m7, and 1-2-6-5 from V7) that students must learn, with particular attention to the unusual interval between scale degrees 2 and 6, which serves as a load-bearing detail in ear training. Rather than introducing entirely new patterns for four-measure progressions, the method extends the mastered two-measure IIm7-V7 vocabulary to accommodate the resolution to IM7, embodying a compositional economy where students apply and combine existing tools rather than accumulating disconnected material.
