# Chapter 21 — Diminished Scale

_Distillation; backed by `chapters/ch21.md`._

Chapter 21 introduces the diminished scale and its foundational structural property: the four-root symmetry, where each diminished scale contains the same tones when starting from any of four different roots (e.g., C, E-flat, G-flat, or A all produce the identical diminished scale). The chapter maps the complete diminished system across three families—establishing that these symmetrical groupings govern how the student applies diminished harmony across all keys. Two core numerical formulas are introduced: extracting scale degrees 1-3-5 yields the Diminished Triad, while 1-3-5-7 yields the Diminished Seventh Chord, making the diminished scale the direct source for both chord types. This systematic approach—deriving chords directly from scale tones by formulaic extraction—is characteristic of the method's architectural clarity and gives the student a unified mechanism for harmonic exploration.
