# Chapter 22 — Parallel Progressions

_Distillation; backed by `chapters/ch22.md`._

Chapter 22 introduces Parallel Progressions through the lens of diminished scale patterns, establishing that the Diminished Triad and Diminished Seventh Chord share a common diminished scale on the chord root, making chord symbol choice a notational convenience rather than a harmonic distinction. The chapter's core teaching method requires systematic transposition practice: students must execute diminished scale patterns starting from every tone illustrated in the reference chart (fig. 32), embedding the principle that scale-based pattern mastery demands exhaustive transposition. The load-bearing terminology includes the diminished scale, chord root, and the conceptual link between chord symbols and underlying scale material—a characteristic move that privileges scale literacy over chord-symbol parsing. This chapter shifts focus from isolated diminished-chord extraction to continuous scalar navigation, preparing students to navigate parallel harmonic territories where a single scale generates multiple chord identities.
