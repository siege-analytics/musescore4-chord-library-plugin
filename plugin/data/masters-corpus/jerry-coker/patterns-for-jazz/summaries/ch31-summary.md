# Chapter 31 — Harmonic Minor Scale

_Distillation; backed by `chapters/ch31.md`._

Chapter 31 introduces the harmonic minor scale and its application to fourth intervals in improvisation, emphasizing how fourths—whether perfect or augmented as determined by major and minor scales—offer improvisers an alternative to the rigid harmonic function of chord-thirds. The chapter establishes the foundational rationale for studying fourths as a technique to break from conventional chord-based sounds, while flagging that successive perfect fourths in free-form improvisation represent an advanced topic distinct from the mixed-interval patterns presented here. The core instructional load centers on the scale-determined rule for interval quality: students learn to identify whether each fourth interval is perfect or augmented based on the underlying major or minor scale. A standing directive to "play this in all keys" applies uniformly across the section's exercises, making transposition and full-key fluency non-negotiable competencies. Overall, this chapter bridges harmonic theory (scales as interval governors) with practical improvisation technique, preparing students to deploy fourth intervals fluidly across all tonal centers.
