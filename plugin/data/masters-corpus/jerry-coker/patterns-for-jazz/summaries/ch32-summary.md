# Chapter 32 — More on the Whole-tone Scale

_Distillation; backed by `chapters/ch32.md`._

Chapter 32 introduces the pedagogical use of fourth intervals in improvisation, presenting them as a means to escape the harmonic rigidity of chord-built thirds (the fourths in improvisation concept). The chapter establishes that students should practice scale-based fourth intervals—both perfect and augmented—derived from major and minor scale tones rather than arbitrary intervals. Through a series of exercises anchored in diatonic harmony, the instruction emphasizes the characteristic move of breaking away from traditional tertian chord function. The method enforces mastery through systematic transposition: the directive to transpose to all keys is the chapter's load-bearing practice rule, ensuring students generalize the fourth-interval patterns across the full chromatic spectrum rather than learning them in isolation.
