# Chapter 33 — Lydian Augmented Scale

_Distillation; backed by `chapters/ch33.md`._

The Lydian Augmented Scale serves two primary harmonic functions over dominant seventh chords, distinguished by where the scale's root is positioned relative to the chord: when applied to a dominant 7th flat 5, the scale root aligns with the chord's seventh, yielding a flatted fifth (augmented eleventh); when applied to dominant 7th chords with raised 5th, raised 9th, lowered 9th, and augmented 11th extensions, the scale root aligns with the chord's third. The chapter's load-bearing terminology includes the two distinct scale-to-chord alignments and the four altered extensions available from the second application. Mastery requires learning all twelve keys through both scalar practice across the instrument's range and intervallic patterns (thirds, etc.), establishing the systematic, repetitive method characteristic of this instructional approach.
