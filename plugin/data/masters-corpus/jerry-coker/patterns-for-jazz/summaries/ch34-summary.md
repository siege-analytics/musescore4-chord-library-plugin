# Chapter 34 — Interval Studies

_Distillation; backed by `chapters/ch34.md`._

Chapter 34 teaches Interval Studies through systematic paired-interval exercises that progress by smaller intervals, establishing a generative method for building scale and harmonic literacy. The chapter introduces the core technique of minor seconds interval pairs (both ascending and descending), which together produce a chromatic scale sonority, then extends this principle through cross-interval progressions such as minor seconds by major seconds and perfect fourths by minor seconds, where one interval type functions as the unit and another as the stepping distance. Key load-bearing terminology includes the interval-pair concept (the foundational structure), chromatic scale result (the harmonic outcome), and the transposition instruction (requiring students to apply patterns across different starting pitches rather than memorize fixed fingerings). This chapter represents a shift from mechanical drill toward systematic intervallic thinking, where each exercise connects technique to aggregate sonority and reinforces the bidirectional (ascending/descending) practice method essential to the method book's architecture.
