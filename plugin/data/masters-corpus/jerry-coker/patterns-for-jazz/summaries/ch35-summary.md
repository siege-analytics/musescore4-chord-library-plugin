# Chapter 35 — Chromatic Scale

_Distillation; backed by `chapters/ch35.md`._

Chapter 35 introduces the chromatic scale through a systematic extension of previous fourth-interval patterns into triplet groupings, exemplified by adding successive fourths (Bb-Eb-Ab; B-E-A; C-F-Bb, etc.) to create novel practice combinations. The core instructional moves involve transposition across multiple starting points—requiring students to apply patterns not only to Bb but also to B-natural and C, and crucially to recognize that symmetrical structures like augmented triads need only three roots (Bb, C, Db) while diminished sevenths require additional roots to complete their cycle. The chapter's load-bearing terminology centers on whole-tone scale variants and the explicit identification of transposition roots, reinforcing the method's fundamental pedagogical principle: mastery through systematic transposition of a single pattern across the chromatic spectrum.
