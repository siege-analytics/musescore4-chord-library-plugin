## Articulations as inflections

> I think of articulations as inflections. When someone is speaking not all the words have the same
>      accent, volume, inflection etc. When playing lines you need to alter the inflection of the
>      notes; not all the notes are long or short or accented.

**Significance (p. 38).** Defines the book's core conceptual framing of articulation as note-level inflection analogous to speech.

## Slurring mechanic

> These inflections are accomplished
>      by slurring notes. Pick one note and play two notes.

**Significance (p. 38).** States the operational rule that produces articulation: one pick stroke yields two sounded notes via slur.

## Same-string slur rule

> Try moving the slur over any two notes that are on the same string.

**Significance (p. 41).** States the physical constraint governing where slurs are playable on guitar: hammer/pull requires a shared string.
