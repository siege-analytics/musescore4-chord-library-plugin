# Chapter 4 — The Major Scales

_Distillation; backed by `chapters/ch04.md`._

Chapter 4 generalizes the directional picking rule from earlier chapters to scale playing by applying it to six basic major-scale fingerings (Picking rule generalizes to scales). The chapter is explicitly anchored to Bruno's broader six-fingering system from his companion book Six Essential Fingerings for the Jazz Guitarist, making this chapter the bridge between the picking method and that scale framework. A string-crossing notation is introduced - asterisks mark moves to higher versus lower strings - so the student can see exactly where the down-on-higher / up-on-lower rule applies in each exercise. The six fingerings are labeled with a V/H taxonomy keyed to the root string: 6V2 and 5V2 (V2 family), 6V4 and 5V4 (V4 family), and 6H2 and 5H2 (H2 family), introducing the V (vertical/two-notes-per-string) and H (horizontal/position-shift) shape conventions used throughout. The chapter is essentially a uniform application of the picking rule across all six core major-scale shapes rather than new technique.
