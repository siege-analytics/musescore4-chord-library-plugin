# Chapter 6 — String Skipping

_Distillation; backed by `chapters/ch06.md`._

Chapter 6 extends the book's core directional-stroke rule to non-adjacent strings, establishing that string skipping follows the same logic: down-strokes when moving to a higher string, up-strokes when moving to a lower one. Bruno systematizes the technique by drilling it across string pairs (4 and 2, 5 and 3, 6 and 4) and layering in the harder in-between stroke variant, while also naming deliberate exceptions like consecutive down-up and consecutive up-down strokes with a skip. The repeated directive to START THIS EXERCISE WITH BOTH STROKES, DOWN AND UP enforces symmetry across every drill. A musical application appears with sixths played using a string skip, and the chapter culminates in a difficult exercise combining accent placement on the up-stroke, the in-between stroke, and skipping. Notably, Bruno offers his own pragmatic preference here, finding alternate down-up picking easier for this hardest combination, a characteristic moment of the master overriding his own framework when experience dictates.
