# Chapter 7 — Typical Be-Bop Phrases

_Distillation; backed by `chapters/ch07.md`._

Chapter 7 applies the book's picking system to bebop vocabulary, opening with Bruno's core principle that fingerboard position dictates picking pattern: the same phrase relocated to the 7th position changes the picking considerably. The chapter then pivots to Slurred Triplets as its load-bearing technique category, linking bebop phrasing to slur articulation rather than pure alternate picking. Bruno states an explicit aesthetic preference for slurred over picked triplets because they sound more horn-like, evoking saxophone phrasing, a characteristic stylistic prescription. Pedagogically he requires the slurred triplet to be mastered starting with both a down-stroke and an up-stroke, and reinforces the two-direction discipline by presenting the same example beginning with an up-stroke. The chapter thus binds positional re-picking, slurred-triplet articulation, and bidirectional symmetry into a single bebop-phrase practice unit.
