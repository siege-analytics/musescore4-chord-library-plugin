# Chapter 8 — Articulations

_Distillation; backed by `chapters/ch08.md`._

Chapter 8 frames articulations as inflections, drawing an explicit analogy to how a speaker varies accent, volume, and emphasis across words rather than treating all notes equally. The operational mechanic is slurring: pick one note and play two notes, producing inflection through hammer-ons and pull-offs rather than additional pick strokes. Bruno anchors the study in typical bebop phrases and teaches that shifting slur placement to a different beat within an identical phrase changes its inflection, a deliberate rehearsal-design principle. Concrete articulation rules appear, such as the long-short ending gesture for phrase cadences, alongside the physical same-string slur rule that hammer and pull techniques require notes on a shared string. The chapter closes with a characteristic pedagogical move: Bruno provides a few slurs and then instructs the student to experiment with their own placement, codifying slur choice as a creative decision the player must internalize rather than a fixed prescription.
