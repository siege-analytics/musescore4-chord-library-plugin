# Chapter 9 — Breaking the Rules

_Distillation; backed by `chapters/ch09.md`._

Chapter 9 explicitly licenses departure from the book's three core picking rules under special circumstances, framing rule-breaking as a deliberate stylistic choice rather than a lapse. The primary exception is consecutive down strokes used to emphasize certain notes when emulating a big-band-type phrase, importing horn-section accent character into the picking hand. Bruno then offers slurs as an equivalent articulation option for the same big-band phrase, expanding the player's toolkit beyond consecutive downs. The second sanctioned exception is rhythmic: when there is a rhythmic break between notes, two down strokes may be preferable to strict alternation. The chapter is short and prescriptive, codifying when the alternation-and-skip-direction system can and should be overridden.
