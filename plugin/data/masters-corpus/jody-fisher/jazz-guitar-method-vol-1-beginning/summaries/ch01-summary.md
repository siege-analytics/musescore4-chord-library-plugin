# Chapter 1 — Getting Started

_Distillation; backed by `chapters/ch01.md`._

Chapter 1 establishes the foundational prerequisites and notation literacy required for the entire jazz guitar curriculum: mastery of open-position and movable barre chords (major and dominant 7th forms on both sixth and fifth string roots), fluency in reading standard notation's pitch and rhythm elements, and understanding of time signatures, note values, rests, and tablature. The chapter introduces load-bearing jazz terminology—specifically "changes" as the jazz term for chord progressions—and frames silence (rests) as rhythmically significant rather than absent. Its most characteristic move is the dual-track lesson architecture (A sections for harmony and chords, B sections for single-line improvisation), which governs how students navigate every chapter that follows. The chapter's tone is pragmatic: notation is presented not as ornament but as the gateway to "a whole world of instructional books and great music," and strumming coordination is framed as a coordination skill rather than a stylistic end, with technique details deferred to page 87.
