# Chapter 2 — Chapter 2

_Distillation; backed by `chapters/ch02.md`._

Chapter 2 teaches the harmonic and melodic foundations of jazz guitar improvisation, covering essential chord voicing rules, dominant chord variants (7b5, 7#5, 7b9), and the critical target-note technique for connecting improvised lines to underlying chords. The master's characteristic moves are visible in the emphasis on voice-leading priority—treating the 3rd and 7th as essential (and omittable notes as expendable)—and in the practical symmetry of rootless 7b9 voicings, which allow fingering patterns to move at minor-third intervals without changing chord quality. Load-bearing terminology introduced includes spelling out the changes (or targeting chord tones), neighbor tones (half- and whole-step approach devices), swing eighths (the rhythmic foundation of jazz phrasing), and slash-chord notation with its dual meanings (bass note vs. added extensions). The chapter also establishes the ii-V7-I progression as the core unit of jazz vocabulary and the principle that chord substitution must be judged by context and ear, not theory alone.
