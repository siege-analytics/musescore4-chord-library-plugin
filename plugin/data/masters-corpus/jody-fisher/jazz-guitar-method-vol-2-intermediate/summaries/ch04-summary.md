# Chapter 4 — Chapter 4

_Distillation; backed by `chapters/ch04.md`._

Chapter 4 teaches jazz improvisation licks organized by chord type—major, minor, dominant, and half-diminished (min7b5)—along with the foundational ii-V7-I chord progression framework that appears throughout the chapter. The core pedagogical move is consistent: learn sample licks for each chord type, transpose them to all twelve keys, and immediately integrate them into solos, with progression difficulty escalating from three-beat chord-per-beat patterns to compressed one-measure ii-V7-I challenges. The chapter introduces the ii-V7-I pattern as a load-bearing structure and extends it across string sets to expand fretboard range, while establishing the principle that licks written for specific voicings (e.g., major 7th, dominant 9th) transfer to related extensions through careful ear training. The master's characteristic move is the emphasis on transposition discipline and immediate application—students must transpose every progression to all twelve keys and insert licks into their own solos as soon as they're learned, building fluency through active use rather than isolated study.
