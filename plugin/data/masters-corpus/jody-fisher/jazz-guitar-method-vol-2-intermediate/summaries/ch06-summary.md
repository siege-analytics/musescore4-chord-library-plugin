# Chapter 6 — Chapter 6

_Distillation; backed by `chapters/ch06.md`._

Rhythm changes, a chord progression originating from George Gershwin's "I've Got Rhythm," form a cornerstone of jazz vocabulary and have underpinned canonical works like Oleo, Anthropology, and The Flintstones. This chapter teaches soloing over rhythm changes using familiar scales from earlier in the method (establishing a foundation before moving to the highly altered and extended variations found in professional jazz). The master's characteristic pedagogical move is deferred complexity: the author asks students to become familiar with the sound of this progression before tackling the advanced alterations and extended scales that arrive later. Key terminology introduced includes rhythm changes itself and the concept of scale-based improvisation over this standard progression. Practice is anchored to a play-along method—recording or using the book's CD to practice soloing in real time—reinforcing the internalization of the progression's sound.
