# Chapter 7 — Chapter 7

_Distillation; backed by `chapters/ch07.md`._

This chapter introduces turnarounds—two- or four-measure harmonic sections used at first endings or tune conclusions that smooth the transition back to the opening measure—and establishes them as a foundational vocabulary in jazz guitar performance. The chapter demonstrates the guitarist's leadership role in selecting turnarounds and emphasizes the performance rule that other players react to the guitarist's choice, making variety in turnaround vocabulary essential for creating unexpected arrangements and effective intros and endings. A key scaling rule states that four-measure turnarounds simply double the duration of the chords used in their two-measure equivalents. The load-bearing terminology introduced includes "turnaround" itself and the concept of "first ending," and the chapter directs players to memorize and transpose turnaround examples while listening actively to how other musicians deploy them—making ear-training and transposition practice the core skills for mastering this idiom.
