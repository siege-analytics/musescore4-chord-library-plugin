# Chapter 1 — Right-Hand Options

_Distillation; backed by `chapters/ch01.md`._

Chapter 1 introduces the three primary right-hand techniques for classical and jazz guitar: pure fingerstyle, hybrid pick-with-fingers, and single-note alternating patterns. In fingerstyle, the thumb (p) controls the sixth and fifth strings while the fingers (i, m, a, c) each govern one string respectively, enabling selective chord voicing and simultaneous multi-voice harmony impossible with a pick alone. The hybrid technique holds a pick between the thumb and index finger while the remaining fingers manage higher strings, offering easy switching between techniques but limiting chord voicing to four notes. For melodic playing, jazz guitarists typically alternate either the first two fingers (i, m) or the thumb and second finger (p, m). The chapter establishes flexibility as the governing principle: mastering all techniques rather than relying on one or two prepares the player for any musical situation.
