# Chapter 3 — Techniques for Harmonizing Non-Chord Tones

_Distillation; backed by `chapters/ch03.md`._

Chapter 3 teaches the core techniques for harmonizing both chord tones and non-chord tones through diads (two-note harmonizations using 3rds and 6ths), voice leading principles that emphasize stepwise motion between chord changes, and strategic chord extensions derived from the melody itself. The chapter introduces load-bearing concepts including passing chords—intermediate harmonies that smooth transitions between chords—and demonstrates how dominant seventh chords can be replaced with diminished chords positioned a half step below the target chord, a substitution that works because the two chords share three notes and resolve similarly. Advanced techniques presented include quartal harmony (stacking 4ths rather than 3rds), octave doubling for melodic emphasis (exemplified by Wes Montgomery and George Benson), and the practical insight that diminished chords repeat every three frets on the fingerboard. The chapter emphasizes that the 3rd and 7th degrees are the most important chord tones for defining chord quality, and offers a minimalist alternative for players who prefer single-note melodies with chords added only at chord changes. Throughout, the master's characteristic moves center on achieving smooth, idiomatic voice leading by understanding how non-diatonic and altered chords can enhance harmonic progressions when applied thoughtfully.
