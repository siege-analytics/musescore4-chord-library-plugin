# Chapter 4 — Passing-Chord Approaches

_Distillation; backed by `chapters/ch04.md`._

Chapter 4 introduces passing-chord approaches, a set of harmonic techniques for enriching skeletal chord progressions through embellishment and voice-leading. The core concepts include using same-quality chords a half-step away as passing chords, employing the IV chord to approach major I, and understanding secondary dominants as a generalization of the V7-I principle to any chord in a progression. The ii-V7-I progression emerges as a load-bearing harmonic formula that defines or establishes a key through the movement of roots, thirds, and sevenths, with backcycling serving as a systematic extension of this approach through repeated fourth-movements. Tritone substitution is presented as a reharmonization technique applicable to both dominant chords and secondary dominants, allowing a dominant chord to be replaced by another whose root is a diminished fifth (tritone) away while maintaining harmonic function. Throughout, the chapter emphasizes learning songs in skeletal form to enable improvised harmonization, making these techniques practical tools for real-time arrangement and soloing.
