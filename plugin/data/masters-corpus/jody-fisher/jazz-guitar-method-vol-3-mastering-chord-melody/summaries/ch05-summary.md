# Chapter 5 — Creating Arrangements and Bass Lines

_Distillation; backed by `chapters/ch05.md`._

Chapter 5 teaches the foundational techniques for creating convincing two-part arrangements where a bass line and chord voicings sound like separate instruments. The core pedagogical approach is learning voicings systematically in all twelve keys, using minimal fingers to preserve freedom for melody and improvisation, then applying bass-line approaches across all keys. Key technical moves include tritone substitution by root distance rather than dominant function, right-hand finger assignment where the thumb handles bass notes while index and middle fingers voice the triad, and using scale tones to connect chord voicings. The master's characteristic voice-leading techniques include half-step approaches to chords (fundamental to walking bass lines), sustaining overlapping triad notes for harmonic continuity, and creating rhythmic separation by delaying the chord slightly after the bass note hits the downbeat. The load-bearing principle throughout is maintaining realistic volume balance and dynamic separation—bass notes on every beat as the anchor, with chords appearing as accents—to achieve the illusion of a bass player and guitarist playing together.
