# Chapter 6 — Moving Voices

_Distillation; backed by `chapters/ch06.md`._

Chapter 6 explores how to discover new voicings and harmonic possibilities within chord shapes you already know by moving them to different octaves and using symmetrical transposition—a technique where certain chords retain their harmonic function (dominant chords stay dominant, etc.) when moved by consistent intervals, particularly whole steps for voicings with 9ths and altered 5ths. The chapter introduces practical ensemble techniques like trading fours and the method of reharmonization through bassline composition, where composing a new bass line guides and drives harmonic choices across a section. Throughout, the author emphasizes that artistic voice and expressive musicianship matter far more than accumulating theoretical knowledge; while mastery of many voicings and techniques brings flexibility, it is how you use what you know—not how much you know—that ultimately counts as a musician.
