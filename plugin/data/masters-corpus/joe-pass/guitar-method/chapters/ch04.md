## Chromatic Patterns practice

> The following Chromatic Patterns and Variations are some starting with open E, then moving up by half-steps to extend
> which I still practice. The student should make up his own the exercise.

**Significance (p. 18).** Self-directed extension of the chromatic pattern up the neck by half-steps is the core technique.

## Scale lines chord foundation

> The chords on which these scale lines are based should be
> played at the beginning and ending of each exercise.

**Significance (p. 18).** Anchors every scale line exercise in its parent chord voicing — harmonic context as the frame.

## Position shifting technique

> In
> moving from one position (Chord Form) to another, one can
> shift to any string by using either the first finger or fourth
> finger to play two notes. This will place the hand on the next
> Chord Form.

**Significance (p. 18).** 1st or 4th finger as pivot is the method's explicit rule for navigating between chord forms on the fretboard.
