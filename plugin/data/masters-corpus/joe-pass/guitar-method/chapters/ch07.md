## Jazz Licks purpose

> The Jazz Licks will acquaint the student with some of the
> basic phrasing and germinal ideas. Most are based on the
> Dominant 7th Chord (C7) since many people seem to need
> guidance in their exploration of its possibilities, especially
> in distinguishing it from the major chord.

**Significance (p. 22).** Dominant 7th as primary vehicle for jazz phrasing; distinguishing dom7 from major.

## Memorization and transposition rule

> Students are
> urged to memorize these licks, then try incorporating them
> into their own improvisations. All the phrases must be
> played within at least three Chord Forms and transposed
>
> C7
>
> into all keys. They should be practiced repeatedly at a
> medium tempo until the student can sing them and hear
> them and feel them.

**Significance (p. 22).** Three chord forms, all keys, medium tempo, internalization by singing.

## Dominant 7th chord form rule

> When playing a Dominant 7th Chord or Scale, one Chord
> Form must be kept in mind. By constantly using and think-
> ing in terms of the Basic Chord Forms and simply flatting
> the seventh tone, the number of mistakes made in playing
> on the Dominant will be greatly reduced.

**Significance (p. 23).** Anchor to chord form + flat the seventh = fewer mistakes on dominant.

## Blues Lines reminder

> Though most students know these Blues Lines, they will
> serve as a reminder to those who may have forgotten them.

**Significance (p. 22).** Blues lines positioned as prerequisite vocabulary reference.
