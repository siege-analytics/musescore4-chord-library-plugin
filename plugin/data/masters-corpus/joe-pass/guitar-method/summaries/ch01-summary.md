# Chapter 1 — SCALES

_Distillation; backed by `chapters/ch01.md`._

Chord Forms serve as navigational anchors for scales, freeing improvisers from note-hunting by providing vertical frameworks within which patterns become automatic; the chapter introduces notation (fingering numbers and string circles), establishes that fingerings are flexible suggestions rather than dogma, and defines the physical territory of each Form (spanning 5-6 frets). Pass prioritizes mastery of the A–E–C/D Forms for efficient fingerboard coverage, teaches the structural equivalence of C and D Forms, and instructs students to extrapolate abbreviated scale examples across all six Forms and practice in either chromatic or Form order across two octaves. The method links Augmented, Diminished, and Altered Dominant 7th Scales to their corresponding chord alterations, introduces the load-bearing rule that learned material must pass through the ear rather than be applied mechanically, and grounds all scale work in a single technique principle: play each note evenly and clearly at slow tempos to build automatic fingerboard knowledge.
