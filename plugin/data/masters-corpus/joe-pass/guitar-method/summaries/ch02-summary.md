# Chapter 2 — PRACTICE PATTERNS

_Distillation; backed by `chapters/ch02.md`._

Chapter 2 introduces practice patterns as a structured scaffold for developing a personal melodic voice, anchored by Pass's core picking rule: alternate picking generally, with down-strokes on every string shift. The chapter teaches that picking style itself is a personal choice to be discovered through practice, not a fixed prescription, and emphasizes the foundational aesthetic principle that music must come from the head, not from fingers mechanically running patterns on the fretboard—students should sing or hum their musical ideas while playing them. Pass prescribes a specific method: take a chord sequence like C–A7–Dmin–G7–C and construct eighth-note exercises over four or eight bars, then extend them, practicing all patterns (thirds, scales, trills, etc.) across all chord forms and keys. The load-bearing concept is that these patterns are developmental tools for building a guitarist's unique sense of melodic construction, ultimately serving improvisation rather than being ends in themselves.
