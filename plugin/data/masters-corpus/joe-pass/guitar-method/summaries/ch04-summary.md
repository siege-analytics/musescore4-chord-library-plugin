# Chapter 4 — SCALE LINES BASED ON CHORDS

_Distillation; backed by `chapters/ch04.md`._

Scale lines based on chords teaches the technique of building melodic phrases within harmonic contexts—practicing chromatic patterns that extend up the neck by half-steps, always framed by the parent chord voicing played at the exercise's beginning and end. The master's characteristic move is anchoring scalar improvisation to chord forms, ensuring melody serves harmony rather than operating independently. Key load-bearing terms are Chromatic Patterns (half-step extensions up the neck), Scale lines (melodic passages derived from chord tones), and Chord Form (the voicing that defines the harmonic foundation and position shifts). The method introduces position shifting as the explicit navigation technique: using the first or fourth finger as a pivot to transition between chord forms while maintaining hand placement on the fretboard. This chapter teaches that melodic fluency emerges not from abstract scale runs, but from intimate knowledge of how scalar motion relates to the underlying harmonic structure.
