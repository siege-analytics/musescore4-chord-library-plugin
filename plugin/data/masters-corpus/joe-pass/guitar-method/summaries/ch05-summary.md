# Chapter 5 — CHORD SEQUENCES

_Distillation; backed by `chapters/ch05.md`._

This chapter teaches that scale choice is determined by chord quality: three diminished scales are used exclusively with Dominant 7th chords, while two whole tone scales fit Dominant 7+5 chords—the foundational principle being that melodic lines grow organically from the underlying chords rather than being imposed upon them. The master's characteristic move here is the insistence on chord-derived composition: students learn melodic patterns by recognizing how lines emerge from chord tones. The chapter introduces essential terminology around scale-to-chord pairing (diminished scales, whole tone scales, Dominant 7th variants) and emphasizes two complementary practice methods—using backing tape or a partner to build chord-tone intuition, and practicing changes out of tempo to fully realize the harmonic richness of each chord without rhythmic pressure.
