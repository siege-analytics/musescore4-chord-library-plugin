# Chapter 7 — JAZZ LICKS

_Distillation; backed by `chapters/ch07.md`._

Chapter 7 introduces jazz licks as foundational phrasing patterns built primarily on the Dominant 7th Chord (C7), chosen to help students understand this chord's unique character distinct from the major chord. The core instruction emphasizes memorization and internalization: students must learn the licks, transpose them through at least three chord forms and into all keys, and practice at medium tempo until they can sing and hear the phrases. The master's characteristic move is anchoring to Basic Chord Forms while simply flatting the seventh tone—a rule that dramatically reduces mistakes when improvising on the dominant. The chapter treats blues lines as prerequisite vocabulary and positions the Dominant 7th as the load-bearing concept that unlocks jazz improvisation.
