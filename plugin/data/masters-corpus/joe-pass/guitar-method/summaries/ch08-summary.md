# Chapter 8 — JOE'S BLUES

_Distillation; backed by `chapters/ch08.md`._

Joe's Blues teaches a blues composition in G major built on the foundational chord progression G7–C7–D7, with extensive harmonic substitution options that develop the student's ear and harmonic palette. The chapter introduces dominant-chord color variants for the tonic (G13, G9, G9(6), G7+9, G7(b9)), harmonic pivots in the turnaround bar 8 (Bm7 replacements with F and B dominant families), and tritone substitution as an explicit rule (E7 interchangeable with Bb9 family chords). A characteristic move throughout is the emphasis on free choice: students must select from available alternatives in both chording and solo improvisation, verifying each phrase against their chosen harmonic interpretation rather than following a single fixed voicing. The chapter's load-bearing principle is ear-driven autonomy—the student checks each phrase against the chord being used, making harmonic decisions rather than reading them passively.
