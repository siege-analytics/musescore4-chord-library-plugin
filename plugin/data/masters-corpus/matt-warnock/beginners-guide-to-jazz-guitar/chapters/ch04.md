## Approach chord definition

> Approach chords are where you play a chromatic chord one fret above or below
> the target chord you’re aiming for.

**Significance (p. 11).** This is the foundational definition of the chapter's core technique, establishing the precise mechanical rule for what constitutes an approach chord.

## Approach chord tension/release function

> Approach chords bring a tension and release sound to your comping, jazzing up
> your chords along the way.

**Significance (p. 11).** This explains the harmonic purpose of the technique, teaching the player why approach chords work musically, not just how to execute them.

## Approach chord sequence order

> Start with the approach chords from above, then from below, then mix both
> together to take your comping to the next level today.

**Significance (p. 11).** This gives the pedagogical sequence — above, below, combined — which is the structural rule governing how the student should practice and internalize the technique.

## Lead sheet chord simplification rule

> In jazz, you normally see the simplest chord symbol
> written on a lead sheet, then you as the performer color that chord from there. So,
> it’s good to get used to seeing Cmaj7 but playing the Cmaj9 color as it imitates a
> real-life musical situation.

**Significance (p. 11).** This defines a core jazz performance convention — reading simplified symbols and adding color — that governs how players interpret lead sheets throughout the genre.

## Approach from above mechanics

> The first exercise features approach chords from above, where you play the same
> shape one fret higher, then resolve into your target chord from there.

**Significance (p. 12).** This specifies the exact mechanical procedure for above-approach chords: same voicing shape, one fret up, then resolve — the actionable rule the player executes on the fretboard.

## Approach from below direction

> You can also work approach chords from below and resolve into your target chord
> from there in your comping.

**Significance (p. 13).** This establishes the second approach direction, completing the two-sided chromatic approach system introduced in the chapter.

## Mixing approach directions

> When ready, mix this pattern with the approach chords from above to double
> your fun with this workout.

**Significance (p. 13).** This instructs the student to combine both approach directions, which is the synthesis step that makes the technique practically applicable in real comping.

## Rhythm/pattern variation rule

> feel free to change the rhythms and picking patterns when comfortable to
> expand this approach chord exercise even further.

**Significance (p. 13).** This is an explicit instructional rule that rhythmic and picking variation should be applied once the shapes are secure, teaching the student how to self-extend the exercise.
