## ii V I scale compatibility

> Because all 3 chords ina ii V | are in the parent key, so Dm7-G7-Cmaj7 are all in C
> major, you can use the tonic major scale to solo over all of those chords.

**Significance (p. 14).** This is the foundational harmonic rule of the chapter — it justifies using a single scale over all three chords of the ii V I progression, which is the core soloing concept being taught.

## scale learning sequence

> Start by learning these scale shapes one at a time, add them to your soloing
> workout, then combine them to cover the fretboard in your solos.

**Significance (p. 14).** This gives the explicit method for progressing through the material: individual shapes first, then integration across the fretboard — a structured practice prescription.

## Shape 1 practice method

> Start by memorizing the scale shape, then working it with a metronome.
>
> As soon as you’re ready, you know the scale but it’s not perfect, throw on the
> backing track and solo with this scale over the C major ii V I.

**Significance (p. 14).** This defines the two-phase practice protocol — metronome drilling followed by applied improvisation over the backing track — which is the method's characteristic move for internalizing each shape.
