# Chapter 5 — Chapter 4 — Major Scale Patterns

_Distillation; backed by `chapters/ch05.md`._

This chapter teaches two major scale shapes—one rooted on the 6th string and one on the 5th string—as the foundation for soloing over ii V I progressions, leveraging the harmonic principle that all three chords exist within the parent major key. The method’s characteristic move is the two-phase practice protocol: memorize each shape with a metronome, then immediately apply it over a backing track before moving to the next shape, emphasizing early applied improvisation over perfect technical mastery. The chapter introduces the key concept of scale shape (positional fingerings anchored to specific string roots) and fretboard integration, teaching students to individually master each shape and then combine them to cover the full neck in their soloing. Load-bearing terminology includes ii V I scale compatibility (the harmonic justification for using one scale across the progression), Shape 1 root and Shape 2 root (the string-based positional anchors), and the progression from isolated scale learning sequence through combining both shapes as the structural arc of acquisition.
