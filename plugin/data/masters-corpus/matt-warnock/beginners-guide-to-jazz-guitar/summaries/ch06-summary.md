# Chapter 6 — Chapter 5 — 134 Pattern

_Distillation; backed by `chapters/ch06.md`._

Chapter 6 teaches chromatic ornaments as the essential bridge from scale knowledge to authentic jazz phrasing, introducing them as structured tension-and-release devices through the 134 fingerpattern. The core mechanism is the 134 pattern—a four-note ornament triggered whenever that fingering appears on any string in any key—which must always resolve completely to the third finger to avoid sounding unintentional. The master’s characteristic move here is pedagogical: deliberate overplay of the new ornament to train the ear, then restraint for musical integration in solos, reflecting an earn-before-restraint philosophy. The chapter’s load-bearing terms are chromatic ornaments (organized chromaticism with resolution), the 134 pattern (the specific fingering trigger), and mandatory pattern resolution (the non-negotiable technical law). Integration is incremental—isolated position work with metronome, then combination of 6th and 5th string shapes, then free application over backing tracks—encoding the book’s progressive method for absorbing new chromatic vocabulary.
