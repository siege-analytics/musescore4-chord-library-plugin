# Chapter 7 — Chapter 6 — Chords and Single Notes

_Distillation; backed by `chapters/ch07.md`._

This chapter teaches the technique of mixing chords and single notes within a solo, a skill essential for ensemble playing in duos or pianoless trios where a guitarist must fill harmonic space without a dedicated chord instrument. The master’s approach is pedagogically progressive, employing a four-stage study sequence that moves from imitation to improvisation: first learn the solo as written with metronome and backing track, then replace single notes with your own lines, then keep the single notes fixed while creating new chord rhythms, and finally improvise both chords and single notes freely over the chord changes. The load-bearing terms here are mixed solo (the hybrid texture of chords and melody) and the study sequence itself, which codifies how to internalize this texture through deliberate practice stages rather than all-at-once improvisation.
