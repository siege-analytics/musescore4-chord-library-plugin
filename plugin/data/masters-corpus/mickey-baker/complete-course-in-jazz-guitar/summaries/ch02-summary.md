# Chapter 2 — Melody Chord Progressions, Introductions, Arpeggio and String Bass Styles (Lessons 6-8)

_Distillation; backed by `chapters/ch02.md`._

Chapter 2 pivots from rhythm comping into melody-chord playing, introductions, and the two characteristic accompaniment textures Baker elevates above plain strumming - arpeggio and walking string-bass (Melody chord progressions; Arpeggio and string bass styles). The transposition mandate from Chapter 1 is operationalised as a concrete manuscript-paper exercise: the student physically rewrites Lesson 4 into F, A flat, and B flat before going further (Transpose Lesson 4 by hand), and the four-key training scope (F, G, A flat, B flat) is preserved while C, D flat, and E flat are deferred. Baker's signature chromatic up-and-down-the-neck drill is reapplied specifically to intros, with physical familiarity treated as the path to fluency (Chromatic intro practice), and the Lesson 6 intro shapes are designated as the canonical melody-chord vocabulary for these four keys (Most important melody chords). The chapter's load-bearing creative claim is combinatorial: a small fixed chord set generates unlimited intros if the student varies rhythm - playing root-then-chord, arpeggiating - and recombines the shapes in as many ways as possible (Vary rhythm to create intros; Combine intros creatively), with the arpeggio and string-bass studies pinned to a specific template, Intro No. 2, so the texture variations have a concrete object to act on (Reworking Intro No. 2).
