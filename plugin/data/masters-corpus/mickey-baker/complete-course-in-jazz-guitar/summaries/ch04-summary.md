# Chapter 4 — Vamps and Fill-ins (Lessons 13-14)

_Distillation; backed by `chapters/ch04.md`._

Chapter 4 introduces vamps and fill-ins and states what is arguably Baker's central architectural claim about jazz-guitar voicings: with the new chords there are only two position sets per key - in C major one set starts at the third position and the other at the eighth - and mastering both sets is what enables fluent transposition between keys (Two position sets per key). The chapter then codifies Baker's signature reharmonisation engine: any dominant-7 chord can be replaced by the minor a fifth above it (G7 by Dmi7, D7 by Ami), with the student required to write out an explicit twelve-row dominant-to-relative-minor chart from C7 through B7 (Tritone-sub via relative minor; Build a dominant/minor chart). A second concrete reference artefact - a scrap book holding all this material - is prescribed alongside the existing manuscript notebook (Build a scrap book). Vamps themselves are defined as a genre-anchored category specific to jump and novelty tunes, taught only in their canonical progressions, and treated as universal targets for the string-bass and arpeggio textures introduced in Lesson 8 (Vamps for jump/novelty tunes; Apply bass/arpeggio to vamps). The chapter closes by specifying the exact seven-key transposition target list - D flat, E flat, F, G, A, B flat - that the C-major vamp study must cover (Transpose vamps to seven keys).
