# Chapter 9 — Scale Runs, Minor/Dominant/Diminished Runs and Blues Pattern (Lessons 27-31)

_Distillation; backed by `chapters/ch09.md`._

This chapter (Lessons 27-31) drills the major-scale, minor, dominant-7th/9th, and diminished runs and then turns them onto a blues pattern, hinging on the load-bearing position-system rule that a single fingering shape is transposed by simple position-shift up and down the neck (Chromatic fingering invariance; Same fingering all directions) — the mechanical key to converting one learned pattern into a 12-key vocabulary at tempo. Baker gates entry into the blues lessons on being able to locate any named run anywhere on the neck on demand (Mastery prerequisite for blues), then declares blues the entry vehicle for all solo work in the method (Blues centrality in jazz). The transposition target is narrowed from all keys to the five horn-friendly blues keys F, G, Ab, Bb, and C (Five blues keys), and the characteristic Baker move is made explicit: the blues solos are built from the exact runs of Lessons 27-30 with no modification, so the exercise vocabulary directly becomes the improvisational material (Reuse of exercise runs).
