## V to I resolution

> the cycle in which we are most interested is from dominant V to tonic I. Now
> the question is, what is the V and the I? The V of a chord demands resolution, the I is
> final. In other words, once you have played the | the ear is satisfied.

**Significance (p. 11).** This defines the fundamental harmonic tension-and-release relationship between dominant and tonic that underpins all subsequent cycle work in the method.

## Dominant as most active chord

> Any chord that you
> play during the course of a cycle demands some kind of temporary resolution until it
> eventually reaches the V (dominant the most active chord of the tonality) and the V re-
> solves into the I

**Significance (p. 11).** This establishes the hierarchical role of the dominant within a chord cycle, explaining why all harmonic motion gravitates toward V before resolving to I.
