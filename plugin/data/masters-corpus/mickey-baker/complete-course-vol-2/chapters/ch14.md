## Symmetric Cycles definition

> In this lesson we have 4 examples of Symetric Cycles. It isn't necessary to write
> them in full detail. Only the first chord will be diagrammed and those to follow, as you
> should know, are a minor third higher.

**Significance (p. 22).** Defines the core concept of the lesson — symmetric cycles built by repeating a chord shape in ascending minor thirds.

## Fingering for Ex. 32

> In exercise 32 the fourth finger moves from the 2nd to the 3rd string as diagrammed.

> Simply follow the same fingering as you progress up the finger board in minor thirds.

**Significance (p. 22).** Gives a specific left-hand fingering instruction — the fourth finger string transfer.

## Cutaway guitar extension

## Applying cycle to any chord

> Any chord can be played in a Symetric Cycle. Take any of your favorite chords, start
> them in a low position on your guitar and move them up in minor thirds or start them in
> a high position and move them down in minor thirds.

**Significance (p. 22).** Generalizes the technique beyond the four written exercises.

## Retrograde practice rule

> Play the exercises below backwards, starting with T1 then 4, 3, 2 and back to the 1.

**Significance (p. 23).** Practice directive — playing the cycle in reverse order to train descending motion.
