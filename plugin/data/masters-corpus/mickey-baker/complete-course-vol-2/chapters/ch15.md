## Symmetric Cycle endings

> Exercises 36 and 37 are identical to exercises 29C and 30A in their opening bars,
> but they end with a Symetric Cycle. These Symetric additions give the music a dynamic
> sound which could never be achieved in Diatonic Harmony.

**Significance (p. 22).** Defines the structural purpose of the Symmetric Cycle as a device that transcends diatonic harmony.

## Applying Symmetric Cycle to existing material

> Practice exercises 36 and 37 and commit themto memory. This done, take the remaining
> introductions and ending in lesson 12 put the last chord in each example into a Symetric
> Cycle. If the last chord ends high on the finger board, move it down in minor thirds, if it
> ends low, move it up.

**Significance (p. 22).** Concrete directional rule for applying the Symmetric Cycle.

## Chord fragments in Symmetric Cycle

> It is possible to use only fragments of the chord rather than its entirety. As anexample
> go back to lesson 11, exercise 25A. In the second bar the last two beats are diminished,
> notice this chord glissandos up in minor thirds. In the same bar of exercise 25B it gliss-
> andos down in minor thirds. In the 6th bar of both examples it happens again. The same
> thing occurs in the 3rd bar of exercise 24.

**Significance (p. 22).** Extends the Symmetric Cycle concept by showing partial chord voicings can execute the glissando-in-minor-thirds move.
