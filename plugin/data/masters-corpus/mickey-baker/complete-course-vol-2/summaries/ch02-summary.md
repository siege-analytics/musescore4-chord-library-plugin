# Chapter 2 — Chord Forms cont'd

_Distillation; backed by `chapters/ch02.md`._

Chapter 2 introduces Chord Forms, the method's foundational concept, which are structures that evolve by sustaining certain tones while moving others freely up or down the fretboard—a voice-leading mechanism demonstrated across four examples. The fourth finger plays a major role in forming these structures and must be practiced chromatically across frets 1 through 10 to develop the dexterity and precision required. A defining characteristic of these chord progressions is that the bottom tone of each chord moves down a whole tone, establishing the harmonic motion that drives the exercises. The chapter's pedagogical approach emphasizes systematic, chromatic practice to build muscle memory and internalize the relationship between finger position and chord voicing. This combination of sustained tones, mobile voices, and consistent bass motion creates the framework for understanding how chords can be transformed through position shifts rather than finger repositioning.
