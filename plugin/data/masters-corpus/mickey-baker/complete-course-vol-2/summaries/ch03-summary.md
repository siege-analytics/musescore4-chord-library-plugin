# Chapter 3 — Chord Exercises

_Distillation; backed by `chapters/ch03.md`._

Chapter 3 teaches chord exercises that bridge Modern and Standard guitar playing techniques through a series of structured drills. The core technical concept is the moving bass line—a single-note progression played underneath a static chord—which creates a counterpoint texture that may appear complex in notation but becomes straightforward once understood as independent melodic motion below harmonic anchors. By working through these exercises, players develop the ability to voice chords while maintaining a flowing bass line, a skill that distinguishes advanced playing from beginner technique. The chapter's characteristic move is the pedagogical patience of acknowledging notation strangeness upfront, explaining that the apparent complexity resolves once the moving line is heard as melodically separate from the chord. This lesson represents a deliberate closure of the technical gap between Modern and Standard approaches, making it foundational for balanced guitar facility. The load-bearing terms introduced are "moving bass line" and the implicit distinction between harmonic (chord) and melodic (bass) layers that coexist in the same passage.
