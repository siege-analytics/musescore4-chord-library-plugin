# Chapter 4 — Simple Chord Analysis

_Distillation; backed by `chapters/ch04.md`._

Chapter 4 teaches simple chord analysis through position-aware exercises that move from minor ninth to minor sixth chords and major ninth to major sixth chords, all via passing tones, establishing harmonic motion as the conceptual framework for both minor and major modes. The master's characteristic move is pragmatic scaffolding: while exercises begin in first position requiring a full five-fret stretch, students who find this difficult are permitted to start in fourth or fifth position, acknowledging that eventual facility comes through practice rather than forced struggle. The chapter introduces essential load-bearing terminology—the chord pair types (minor ninth, minor sixth, major ninth, major sixth), the mechanism of resolution (passing tones), and the notational legend ("R" for Root, "P" for Passing tone) that governs how every chord diagram on the page must be read. By placing major and minor forms side by side and noting that mode is their sole structural difference, the chapter reinforces harmonic parallelism as a core organizing principle.
