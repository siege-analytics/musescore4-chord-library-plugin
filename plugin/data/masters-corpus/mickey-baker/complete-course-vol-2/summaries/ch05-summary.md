# Chapter 5 — Chords and Passing Tones

_Distillation; backed by `chapters/ch05.md`._

Chapter 5 introduces the dual-line exercise format that becomes the method's signature teaching device, presenting standard chord progressions on the first line and modern progressions on the second to contrast traditional and contemporary approaches. The chapter teaches ninth-to-sixth voice leading as the conceptual advancement of modern harmony, replacing the simpler dominant-to-tonic resolutions found in standard progressions, with exercises 10 and 11 demonstrating the move from minor ninth to minor sixth and major ninth to major sixth. The master's characteristic move is the chromatic transposition drill—a standing practice rule requiring students to analyze examples thoroughly, then move them chromatically up and down the fingerboard in both minor and major modes to internalize the progressions across the full neck. The chapter establishes the notation convention of circled numbers above each chord, which reference the foundational chord voicing chart from Lesson One, creating a systematic link between new exercises and previously learned material. This architecture of parallel progressions, chromatic transposition, and cross-referenced voicing forms the pedagogical backbone for mastering voice-leading principles.
