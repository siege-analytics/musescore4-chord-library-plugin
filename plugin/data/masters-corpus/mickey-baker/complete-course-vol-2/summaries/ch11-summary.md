# Chapter 11 — Chords and Melody Playing

_Distillation; backed by `chapters/ch11.md`._

Chapter 11 teaches the forced chord cycles method, which substitutes alternative chord progressions against the original cycles in song fragments to expand harmonic vocabulary and creative options. Students are presented with original song excerpts in sheet music format alongside two variations for each example, drawn from lesson 7 and additional songs. The load-bearing concept is the forced chord cycle itself—a deliberate harmonic substitution technique that allows players to reharmonize melodies while maintaining their melodic integrity. The chapter's characteristic pedagogical move is the two-variation format, which systematically presents alternatives and trains ear recognition of harmonic color. The practice directive is explicit and direct: master all variations, commit them to memory, then select and use only those that align with personal musical taste, balancing technical mastery with artistic choice.
