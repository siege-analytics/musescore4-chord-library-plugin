# Chapter 12 — The Cadence

_Distillation; backed by `chapters/ch12.md`._

Chapter 12 teaches the Authentic Cadence, the harmonic gesture that ends a musical thought by moving from the dominant (V) chord to the tonic (I), establishing closure and tonality. The chapter anchors this concept in popular music's structural foundation of eight- or sixteen-bar phrases, where cadences mark formal boundaries. A characteristic distinction the master draws is between introductions and endings: introductions stop on the V chord before approaching the song's first chord, while endings complete the V-to-I motion as a final statement. The load-bearing terminology is "Authentic Cadence" itself, which names this V-I progression and its functional role in harmonic syntax. The master emphasizes that cadential thinking applies at any structural scale—not only in standard phrase lengths but in single- or two-bar sequences—making the Authentic Cadence a scalable principle rather than a fixed form.
