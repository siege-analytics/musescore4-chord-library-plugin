# Chapter 14 — Symetric Cycles

_Distillation; backed by `chapters/ch14.md`._

Chapter 14 introduces Symmetric Cycles, a technique for transposing chord shapes by moving them up or down the fretboard in ascending or descending minor thirds, with only the first chord diagrammed since the remaining positions follow the same shape at the higher intervals. The chapter presents four written examples and establishes a characteristic left-hand move where the fourth finger transfers from the 2nd to the 3rd string as the player progresses up the fretboard in these minor-third increments. The master emphasizes that any chord can be played as a Symmetric Cycle, whether ascending from a low position or descending from a high position, making this a universal transposition tool rather than a context-specific technique. A practical constraint acknowledges that cutaway guitars allow extension to higher positions while non-cutaway instruments are limited by their body design. The chapter includes a retrograde practice rule directing students to play the exercises backwards (from position 4 back to 1) to build facility with descending motion, cementing "Symmetric Cycle" and "minor third" as the load-bearing terminology for this transposition vocabulary.
