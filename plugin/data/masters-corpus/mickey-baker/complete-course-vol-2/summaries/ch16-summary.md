# Chapter 16 — Symetric Introductions and Endings — Part 2

_Distillation; backed by `chapters/ch16.md`._

Chapter 16 expands the symmetric-cycle technique beyond linear minor-third motion, demonstrating that these patterns can be applied in varied harmonic contexts to create introductions and endings. The master's characteristic move is retrograde application—taking previously learned broken-chord exercises and reversing them to generate new material, as shown in Example 34's inversion of Example 33. The load-bearing terminology centers on "Symmetric Cycles" and "broken chords played backwards," which enable guitarists to construct mirror-image phrases that feel cohesive and musical. The chapter's core directive is to internalize these variations through practice and apply them to songs the student already knows, embedding the technique into real musical contexts rather than treating it as abstract theory.
