# Chapter 17 — Augmented Cycles

_Distillation; backed by `chapters/ch17.md`._

Chapter 17 teaches augmented chord construction based on major thirds and their symmetric properties across the chromatic scale. An augmented chord (such as C–E–G#) maintains its pitch-class identity when transposed up a major third, enabling the formation of an "Augmented Cycle" by dividing the 12-tone scale into three equal parts to yield three distinct tonic chords, a principle demonstrated in Exercises 38–40. The chapter expands this framework to accommodate six tonics by inserting intermediate chords into the cycle (Exercise 41), and extends further to introduce "Symmetric Harmony," a technique in which parallel voice leading connects start and end harmonic points regardless of intervening motion, permitting up to 12 tonics within a single progression (Exercise 42). The master's characteristic approach treats harmonic motion as a matter of endpoints and direction rather than voice-leading detail, emphasizing the mathematical economy of equal division and transposition as organizing principles for harmonic exploration.
