# Chapter 18 — Augmented 7ths

_Distillation; backed by `chapters/ch18.md`._

Chapter 18 introduces the Augmented 7th chord, formed by adding a 7th interval to the Augmented triad, as demonstrated in Exercise 43. This chapter establishes a fundamental principle governing non-diatonic harmony: chords that do not appear in a song's original chord structure can be strategically introduced to add color and originality to the arrangement. The Augmented 7th represents one of several substitute chord options available to the guitarist seeking harmonic variety beyond the diatonic palette. The key terminology this chapter loads is "Augmented 7th" and the concept of non-diatonic color chords as deliberate harmonic choices rather than errors. By understanding this relationship between chord extension and tonal color, players learn to expand their harmonic vocabulary beyond the constraints of traditional songwriting structure.
