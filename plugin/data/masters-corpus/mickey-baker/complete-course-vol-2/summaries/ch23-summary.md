# Chapter 23 — Pedal Point

_Distillation; backed by `chapters/ch23.md`._

Chapter 23 introduces the pedal point technique, a foundational harmonic device that sustains a single note (typically the root or fifth of a key) while harmonies move above it, creating tension and harmonic interest. Students learn to identify and execute pedal point on D in G major, where D functions as the fifth degree and serves as the fixed anchor while other chord voices shift independently. The chapter uses drop-D tuning on the sixth string as the prerequisite setup, enabling the sustained D to resonate naturally throughout the exercises. By isolating pedal point as the single variable added to an existing jazz guitar exercise, the pedagogy makes explicit what students are learning to control—the deliberate contrast between harmonic movement and tonal stability. The load-bearing terminology centers on pedal point itself, harmonic function (the V, or fifth degree), and the relationship between sustained tones and changing harmonies above them.
