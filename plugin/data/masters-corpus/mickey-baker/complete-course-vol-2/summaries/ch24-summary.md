# Chapter 24 — Practical Use of Pedal Point

_Distillation; backed by `chapters/ch24.md`._

Chapter 24 teaches the practical orchestration of Pedal Point in ensemble settings, establishing that the bass player holds the sustained pedal tone while the piano or guitar provides harmonic variations above it. The master's characteristic move is the three-layer texture—bass playing the pedal point, guitar or piano executing chord variations, and a melody or solo instrument carrying the song's line—which defines the method's functional model for this technique. The chapter introduces the distinction between pedagogical exercises (Exercises 57 and 59, designed for ear-training only) and performance-ready arrangements (Exercises 60 and 61), clarifying which patterns transfer to live playing. Load-bearing terminology includes "Pedal Point distribution" to describe how the sustained note must be allocated based on ensemble size, and "Cadence Breaks," identifying structural moments where Pedal Point appropriately extends beyond its primary harmonic function. This chapter transitions Pedal Point from isolated technical study into the practical domain of ensemble arrangement and performance contexts.
