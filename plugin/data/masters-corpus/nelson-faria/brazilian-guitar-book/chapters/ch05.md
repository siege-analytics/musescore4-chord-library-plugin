## Baião origin and style

> Music from the north east of Brazil, more specifically from Ceara, Maranhao and
> Bahia, Baiao is derived from a folk dance (bumba-meu-boi) and emerged as a
> musical style in the 40's.

**Significance (p. 119).** Establishes geographic and historical origin of baião.

## Baião modal and harmonic characteristics

> The baiao melodies are usually in the mixolydian or lydian b7 modes. It’s written
> in 4/4 but you can find also the use of binary meters (2/4 or 2/2).
>
> The harmonies are quite simple with much use of pedal tones in the bass.

**Significance (p. 119).** Defines the two primary modal flavors and the signature bass-pedal harmonic approach for baião.

## Basic pattern practice advice

> You can practice these patterns and variations using a static seventh chord (mixolydian mode).
>
> It's also a good idea to practice away from the guitar, clapping the high voices and
> tapping the low voices with your foot.

**Significance (p. 121).** Cross-training technique for internalizing the two-layer baião texture.
