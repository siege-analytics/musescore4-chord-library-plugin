# Chapter 1 — PART I - Samba

_Distillation; backed by `chapters/ch01.md`._

Chapter 1 teaches the foundational techniques and rhythmic concepts of samba guitar, beginning with the samba syncopation cliché—the single most important rhythmic figure that must become intuitive to the student. The core technical rule assigns the right hand fingers 1, 2, and 3 to syncopated upper voices while the thumb plays the on-beat bass note, a division of labor that governs all variations and which the student should practice away from the guitar by clapping high voices and tapping the low with the foot. The bass line operates under a root/fifth rule with the fifth preferentially voiced below the root, and when syncopated independently, requires the thumb for the bass line but the index finger in block-style situations—distinctions that preserve the samba feel while maintaining rhythmic independence. The chapter introduces seventeen variations (numbered #1 through #17) that systematically explore techniques including mirror-image two-measure forms, agogô simulation through voice-register switching, the batucada style that mixes syncopated and non-syncopated rhythms over four measures, and named sub-styles like Partido Alto. Left-hand fingering is prescribed specifically for each variation (such as 3-1-4-2 for Variation #3 and 4-1-1-3-2 for Variation #17), with problem-solving strategies like thumb-over technique and split-finger barres for wide stretches. The chapter establishes that variations can be mixed within a song and deployed strategically at cadenzas and section endings, teaching the student to apply patterns contextually rather than mechanically.
