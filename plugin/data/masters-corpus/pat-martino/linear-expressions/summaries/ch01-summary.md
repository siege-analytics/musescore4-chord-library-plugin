# Chapter 1 — Phase I: Horizontal Movement: Inversions and Activities In One Key

_Distillation; backed by `chapters/ch01.md`._

Chapter 1 teaches the foundational concept of "activities"—melodic line forms improvised over harmonic structures—and establishes that five positional scale forms in G minor (derived from the Bb major scale via relative minor relationship) cover the guitar neck horizontally across one octave, with each form determined by a corresponding chord inversion according to the chord-to-activity mapping rule. The characteristic move of this method is horizontal coverage: like the four chord inversions, the five scale patterns move up the fingerboard until all twelve frets are covered, and the system is infinitely repeatable since activities 1 and 5 share the same chord inversion and bookend the octave. Key terminology introduced includes "activities" (the core unit), "relative minor," "inversions," "position" (where the form is played, which determines the key), and the transposition principle that activity forms are position-independent and must be mastered in all twelve keys before advancing to Phase II. The chapter's load-bearing rule is that the chord form dictates which activity is played, making chord-activity pairing the operative decision system for improvisation.
