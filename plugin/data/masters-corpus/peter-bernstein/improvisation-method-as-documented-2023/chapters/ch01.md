## Bernstein as living tradition

> I believe Bernstein embodies a living repository of jazz tradition while also pushing the
> boundaries of jazz guitar as a revolutionary exponent.

**Significance (p. 3).** Positions Bernstein's pedagogy as drawing on tradition rather than abstract theory, motivating the oral-transmission approach used throughout the book.

## No strict method

> Bernstein himself acknowledges that his teaching approach lacks a strict methodical structure.
> Instead, he emphasizes the importance of contextualizing ideas within specific musical pieces.
> Rarely does he explain a concept without relating it to a musical context.

**Significance (p. 3).** Defines the foundational pedagogical stance: concepts only carry meaning when tied to a specific tune, a constraint that shapes every later chapter.

## Learning by playing

> This informal approach
> reflects his own learning journey, which involved attending music school for a period but
> primarily gaining knowledge through playing and touring with revered figures in the jazz
> tradition, rather than in an academic setting.

**Significance (p. 3).** Grounds the anti-academic framing of the method in Bernstein's own apprenticeship lineage and justifies the oral, contextual style of instruction.
