## Use natural fingerings to internalize

> He suggests
> using fingerings that feel natural and comfortable for us, as trying to execute ideas with awkward
> fingerings can hinder the process of internalization.

**Significance (p. 24).** Practical rule: fingering choice should serve internalization, not exhibit difficulty.

## Flexibility in delivery

> Bernstein emphasizes the importance of being flexible in the delivery of ideas to
> ensure they feel organic within the flow of a solo. He likens it to a conversation, where you don't
> want to be preoccupied with when to interject something you've just learned.

**Significance (p. 24).** Rule for integrating new vocabulary: it must be flexible enough to appear unforced in real time.

## Narrative vs notes that work

> Bernstein also emphasizes the distinction between playing ideas that relate to each other and
> create a narrative with purpose versus merely playing notes that work over the chords.

**Significance (p. 24).** Core aesthetic axis of the method: connected narrative outranks correctness-of-notes.

## Melody target notes as basis

> Bernstein consistently emphasizes the concept of using the target notes of the melody as the basis
> for melodic improvisation whenever we play a tune.

**Significance (p. 25).** Defines the central improvisational starting point: the tune's own melodic target notes.

## Comping voicings mirror solo targets

> these landing points are closely connected to the notes he
> chooses for the top voicings while comping. This is because those notes often embody strong and
> melodically pleasing phrases.

**Significance (p. 25).** Connects comping voicing choices to solo target-note choices as a single melodic system.

## Lines leading to target notes

> Once you can identify these notes, he encourages exploring melodic lines that lead to these target
> notes, starting with simple ideas and later exploring more complex ones, imparting a narrative
> quality and providing a specific framework for storytelling during improvisation.

**Significance (p. 25).** Practice procedure: identify target notes from the melody, then build lines that lead to them.
