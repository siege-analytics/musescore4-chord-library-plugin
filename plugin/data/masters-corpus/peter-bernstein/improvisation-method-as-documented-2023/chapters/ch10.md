## Technique serves beauty and story

> He believes that technical
> prowess should always serve the purpose of creating beauty and conveying a story.

**Significance (p. 29).** Summary maxim of Bernstein's technique philosophy: virtuosity is subordinate to story.

## Beauty within limitations

> for him,
> guitar playing is not about showcasing difficulty but rather about discovering and expressing the
> inherent beauty within his limitations.

**Significance (p. 29).** Reframes limitations as the very material from which a personal style emerges.

## One shape, moving hand

> Bernstein also recounted an interesting encounter where someone praised his comping, noticing
> that he seemed to utilize the same chord shape for each chord, moving his hand around the neck
> instead of changing finger positions.

**Significance (p. 29).** Concrete characteristic move: maintain a single chord shape and shift position rather than reshape.

## Narrow aesthetic path

> Bernstein's approach to jazz improvisation is highly distinctive, developed over many years to
> cultivate a specific aesthetic understanding of the music, heavily influenced by traditional
> players.

**Significance (p. 29).** States the book's framing of Bernstein's style as a deliberate narrow path versus broad exploration.

## Improvising from a vast library

> like an exceptional speaker, he explores ideas on the bandstand,
> drawing from his vast library of musical concepts. He combines these ideas in a way that
> engages and surprises both the audience and himself

**Significance (p. 30).** Defines the model of improvisation as combinatorial exploration of internalized vocabulary.

## Learn classic lines, then abstract

> he has emphasized the significance of learning classic lines. He would
> demonstrate a well-known vocabulary piece and then proceed to create variations, gradually
> abstracting further from the original idea.

**Significance (p. 30).** Pedagogical procedure: start from memorized classic vocabulary, then progressively abstract.

## Impressionist analogy

> Bernstein, in a similar manner, can be considered a master of taking the
> jazz tradition and abstracting it at different levels, creating an impressionistic portrayal of the
> great masters.

**Significance (p. 30).** Frames Bernstein's style as impressionistic abstraction of tradition, not literal reproduction.

## Study the whole jazz tradition

> he consistently stresses the importance of studying the
> entire jazz tradition and familiarizing oneself with the contributions of the numerous great artists
> who have shaped the music. While he has placed particular emphasis on blues, swing, bebop,
> and hard bop, he possesses a comprehensive understanding of the entire evolution of jazz

**Significance (p. 30).** Prescribes comprehensive tradition study with blues/swing/bebop/hard-bop as primary emphasis.
