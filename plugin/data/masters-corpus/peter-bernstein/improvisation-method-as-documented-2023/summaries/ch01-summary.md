# Chapter 1 — Introduction and Recurrent Themes

_Distillation; backed by `chapters/ch01.md`._

This chapter is introductory and biographical rather than instructional: Moreno frames the document as a record of roughly a year of lessons with Peter Bernstein at NYU, grounding its authority in direct apprenticeship rather than secondhand interpretation (Source and method). It positions Bernstein as a living repository of jazz tradition who simultaneously pushes the music forward (Bernstein as living tradition), motivating an oral-transmission style of pedagogy throughout the book. The chapter is explicit that Bernstein has no strict methodical structure and almost never explains a concept without anchoring it to a specific tune (No strict method) — the foundational constraint that shapes every later chapter. Moreno ties this anti-academic stance to Bernstein's own learning lineage of touring and playing with jazz elders rather than schooling (Learning by playing), establishing the load-bearing terms 'living tradition,' 'contextualized in tunes,' and 'oral, informal pedagogy' that the rest of the document depends on.
