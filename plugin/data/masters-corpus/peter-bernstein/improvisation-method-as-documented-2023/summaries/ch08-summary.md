# Chapter 8 — Melodic Target Notes and the Narrative of the Solo

_Distillation; backed by `chapters/ch08.md`._

Chapter 8 articulates the central aesthetic of Bernstein's improvisational method: connected narrative outranks correctness-of-notes, drawing a sharp line between ideas that relate to each other with purpose and merely playing notes that work over the chords (Narrative vs notes that work). The load-bearing concept is that the melody's own target notes—the tune's landing points—are the basis for improvisation, and the practice procedure is to identify those targets and then build melodic lines that lead to them, starting simply and growing in complexity (Melody target notes as basis, Lines leading to target notes). The chapter also unifies the soloing and comping systems by observing that the top voicings Bernstein chooses while comping are drawn from the same melodic target notes he uses as solo landing points (Comping voicings mirror solo targets). Two practical rules support internalization: choose fingerings that feel natural rather than ones that exhibit difficulty (Use natural fingerings to internalize), and ensure new vocabulary is flexible enough to appear unforced in the real-time conversation of a solo (Flexibility in delivery).
