# Chapter 6 — Reharmonization Using Bass Lines

_Distillation; backed by `chapters/ch06.md`._

Chapter 6 teaches bass-line reharmonization as a method for creating harmonic variety by deriving chord progressions from stepwise bass movement toward target chords. The foundational principle is that consistent bass-line direction (either ascending or descending) drives cadential strength, with root motion treated as the primary voice in establishing harmonic flow. Practitioners must treat bass notes as chord tones (1, 3, 5, or 7) rather than extensions, maintain stepwise motion, limit consecutive dissonant intervals between melody and bass to three or fewer, and exercise caution with seventh-degree bass notes unless preceded by root-position context. The chapter introduces hybrid chords—ambiguous voicings that arise when bass notes lack harmonic grounding—as a distinctive but potentially destabilizing device that should be deployed carefully to preserve phrase cohesion. The prescribed methodology is iterative, beginning with a rough-draft stepwise bass line, progressively refined through chromatic variation and harmonic densification, with optional melodic alteration used sparingly to resolve conflicts. Load-bearing concepts include cadential strength, voice-leading prerequisites, harmonic rhythm density (which constrains chord complexity), and the distinction between tension tones and chord tones in determining chord symbol complexity.
