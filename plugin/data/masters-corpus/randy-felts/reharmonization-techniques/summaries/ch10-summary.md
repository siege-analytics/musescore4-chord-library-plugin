# Chapter 10 — Basic Piano Voicing Techniques

_Distillation; backed by `chapters/ch10.md`._

Voice leading connects notes smoothly from chord to chord, creating a blended, textural sound while making voicings more playable on keyboard. Modern jazz and pop composers prioritize balanced note spacing and smooth chord-to-chord connections over strict traditional rules. The primary voice-leading rule governs perfect fourth or perfect fifth root motion: resolve the third of the first chord to the seventh of the second, and vice versa, producing clear harmonic texture and minimizing finger movement. For unison or second root motion, move all voices in parallel motion by similar amounts. When roots move by third or sixth, employ either parallel or contrary motion between the bass and upper voices. Tensions should generally stay above the F on the fourth line of the bass clef to avoid muddiness, and inverted chords function best as passing chords when approached and left by step rather than beginning or ending harmonic phrases. Non-chord tones in the lowest voice produce hybrid voicings (see Chapter 14), and third inversion chords require preceding root-position context to avoid being heard as a different root.
