# Chapter 13 — Modal Reharmonization

Chapter 13 covers modal reharmonization across Dorian, Phrygian, Lydian, Mixolydian, and Aeolian modes. The chapter-file has only one surviving quote due to fidelity-validation drops, but the chapter teaches: place the I chord in strong rhythmic positions and approach it via mode-specific two-chord cadences (e.g., Dorian II-7 → I-7); Dorian and Phrygian cadences must resolve to a minor target chord though the reharmonization key need not match the original; both melody AND chords must be diatonic to a particular modal scale; key-changes between modal centers are the normal tool for full-song modal treatment; remain in each modal key for at least four bars so listeners register the modal color; the Lydian #11 requires a tonic pedal to be heard correctly; Phrygian b9 and Lydian tritone are mode-defining dissonances that signal a structural error when used outside their modal context; modal interchange specifically refers to combining minor and major chords on the same pitch center.
