# Chapter 15 — Constant Structure Patterns

_Distillation; backed by `chapters/ch15.md`._

Constant structure patterns organize sound through consistent bass motion (half steps, whole steps, or thirds) and maintain textural coherence via melody-harmony relationships, which form the primary organizational elements of the technique. While patterned root motion typically enhances the sound—such as roots outlining implied harmonies like diminished or major triads, or spelling tensions within larger chord structures—it is not strictly required. The roots may outline complete chords with their extensions (FMaj7 with tensions) or only loosely suggest tonal centers (I, IV, V) without establishing functional tonality, and tension tones commonly appear in the lead voice across most chords. This approach prioritizes consistent textural sound and harmonic outlining over traditional tonal function, creating non-tonal or loosely tonal progressions organized by systematic voice-leading patterns rather than chord-to-chord resolution.
