## Convergence system definition

> This convergence system shows a fixed bombardment of
> any type chord that we want to give "tonal significance" to then
> bombard it with 24 scales, a mixture of them, their ideas and
> constituent chords.

**Significance (p. 17).** Core definitional statement of the entire method.

## 2-5 cadence and tritone

> The 2-5 cadence is one of the most regulary occuring devices
> in music. It has participated in music in assuming main tonalities
> and lesser tonalities & to propel musical motion. The central in-
> terval of harmonic signficance of the 2-5 cadence has been the
> "mysterious" tritone interval.

**Significance (p. 17).** Tritone within 2-5 cadence as the pivot point.

## Chord-scale relationship rule

> Each chord represents a member chord sound of one of the modes
> of the scale it comes from.

**Significance (p. 17).** Foundational rule linking every chord to a parent scale mode.

## Ear preparation before playing scales

> Before playing each scale in this
> system, you should brainwash your ear with the
> tonality (F major in this case) with several cadences
> into the key.

**Significance (p. 18).** Concrete practice rule — prime ear with cadences before convergent scale.

## Last-note resolution rule (all 24 scales)

> The last note
>
> of all the scales in this system are only a whole
>
> or half step away from the Ist, 3rd, or 5th or root
>
> of the desired tonal destination.

**Significance (p. 22).** Universal voice-leading rule validating every scale in the system.
