# A System of Tonal Convergence (Ted Dunbar, 1975) — Statement of Outputs

## Overview

*A System of Tonal Convergence for Improvisors, Composers and Arrangers* is a harmonic method book by jazz guitarist and educator Ted Dunbar (1937–1998), self-published in 1975 (East Brunswick, NJ). The book constructs a complete framework for organizing the entire chromatic scale into directed, purposeful motion toward any tonal center. Its central claim — stated as a philosophical foundation in Chapter 1 and elaborated technically across Chapters 2–8 — is that every chromatic pitch bears a cadential relationship to any active key center, a principle the author names the **tonal gravity law**. From that single axiom, the book builds outward: a set of 24 scales organized by harmonic color (major, minor, augmented, dominant, diminished) that "bombard" a target tonality from all harmonic directions, visualized as a 360° compass with the key center at its hub.

The pedagogical arc is spiral rather than linear. Chapter 1 establishes the governing metaphor and vocabulary. Chapter 2 builds the technical core — the Scale Justification Sheets and universal voice-leading rule. Chapter 3 introduces key individual scales and the "no wrong notes" philosophy. Chapter 4 demonstrates convergence through mode application and tetrachord analysis. Chapters 5–7 present the method in pure notation (chord-only, scale-chord, chord-on-chord forms), and Chapter 8 returns to prose to treat the dominant seventh and minor chord as the system's culminating harmonic terrain, introducing the **Circle of Gravities** as a navigational map of tritone relationships.

The book distillation identifies two interlocking systems that jointly constitute the method: the **24-Scale Convergence Engine**, which is the technical apparatus, and the **Tonal Gravity Framework**, which is the perceptual and philosophical infrastructure that makes the engine operable.

## Systems

### The 24-Scale Convergence Engine

The 24-Scale Convergence Engine is the technical heart of the book. It comprises a fixed inventory of twenty-four scales, organized by harmonic color, that collectively surround any target tonal center and can approach it from any harmonic direction. The engine was conceived at the II–V cadence — the structural unit Dunbar identifies in Chapter 1 as the seed of the entire system — and assigns the dominant seventh chord, with its inherent tritone, the role of primary convergence vehicle. Practitioners do not select a single scale and apply it; instead, they rotate multiple scales in varying orders over a single target chord, especially the dominant seventh, deploying these convergent approaches in turnbacks, cadences, and cycles (Chapter 1 summary; Chapter 2 summary).

**Members:** Major Color Scales; Minor Color Scales; Dominant Color Scales; Diminished Color Scales (H-W Scale #4, W-H Scale #5); Augmented Color Scales; Scale #11 (Descending Tritone Scale); Scale #15 (Half-Diminished / Half-Whole-Tone); Scale #16 (Major with Raised 4th); Target Tonal Center; Dominant Seventh Chord.

**Traversal Rules:**

1. **Universal Last-Note Resolution Rule** `VoiceMotion` — every scale's last note is a whole or half step from the root, 3rd, or 5th of the target. *(Chapter 2 summary; ch02.md p. 22)*
2. **Free-Order Scale Rotation** `_pending:free-order-traversal` — all 24 scales may be connected in any order. *(Chapter 3 summary; ch03.md p. 30)*
3. **Tritone as Pivot Interval in II–V Traversal** `_pending:tritone-pivot` — the tritone is the central pivot of the 2-5 cadence. *(Chapter 2 summary; ch02.md p. 17)*
4. **Final Tetrachord as Convergence Bridge** `_pending:tetrachord-bridge` — the last tetrachord matches the target key scale. *(Chapter 4 summary; ch04.md p. 50)*

6. **Chord-on-Chord Tritone Approach** `_pending:tritone-sub-approach` — target chords are typically preceded by a V7 with a tritone. *(Chapter 8 summary; ch08.md p. 66)*

### Tonal Gravity Framework

The Tonal Gravity Framework is the perceptual and philosophical sub-system that establishes the conditions under which the 24-Scale Engine operates. Where the engine supplies the technical apparatus, the framework supplies the governing epistemology: that harmonic fluency is not rule-following but cultivated awareness of five governing factors — key center, Vantage Point, Interval Tonics, Resolution, and the artist's confidence — that together license unrestricted use of the chromatic scale. The framework encompasses the preparatory practices of ear-priming and sound projection and maps tritone relationships systematically via the **Circle of Gravities** introduced in Chapter 8 (Chapter 1 summary; Chapter 3 summary; Chapter 4 summary).

**Members:** Key Center; Vantage Point; Interval Tonics; Resolution; Artist's Confidence; Circle of Gravities.

**Traversal Rules:**

1. **Ear Priming via Cadences** `_pending:ear-priming` — prime the ear with cadences into the target tonality. *(Chapter 2 summary; ch02.md p. 18)*
2. **Sound Projection Technique** `_pending:mental-projection` — project sound-attention to the target tonality before arrival. *(Chapter 2 summary; ch02.md p. 20)*
3. **Tonal Gravity Law — Chromatic Cadential Pull** `_pending:tonal-gravity` — every chromatic pitch exerts cadential pull toward the active key. *(Chapter 1 summary; ch01.md p. 4)*

**Modification Rules:**

1. **No Wrong Notes — Full Chromatic Availability** `_pending:chromatic-license` — five governing factors unlock the entire chromatic scale. *(Chapter 3 summary; ch03.md p. 31)*
2. **Mistake as Generative Event** `_pending:generative-error` — errors are reframed as new harmonic fields. *(Chapter 4 summary; ch04.md p. 50)*
3. **Consciousness Over Prescription** `_pending:awareness-governance` — the system increases awareness rather than dictating procedure. *(Chapter 4 summary; ch04.md p. 50)*
