# Chapter 2 — Scale Justification Sheets (24)

_Distillation; backed by `chapters/ch02.md`._

Chapter 2 introduces the Scale Justification Sheets, a convergence system that bombards a target chord with 24 scales to establish harmonic "tonal significance." The foundational rule—that each chord represents a member chord sound of one of the modes of its parent scale—underpins the entire method. Key instructional moves include: priming the ear with cadences before playing each scale, understanding the tritone as the pivot interval within the 2-5 cadence, and extracting all possible chords from scales for resolution purposes. The system employs a universal voice-leading rule stating that the final note of every scale in the system sits only a whole or half step away from the root, third, or fifth of the target tonality, ensuring smooth resolution. Load-bearing terminology includes the "tonal significance" concept, the distinction between two diminished scale types (H-W and W-H interval structures), and the sound-projection technique (mentally projecting yourself sound-wise to the target tonality). The ultimate goal is chromatic tonal fluency—training the ear to execute a "plunging process" into any tonal destination from any chromatic point.
