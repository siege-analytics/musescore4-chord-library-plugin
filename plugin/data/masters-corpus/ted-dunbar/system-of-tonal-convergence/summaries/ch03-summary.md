# Chapter 3 — Scale of the System and some Constituent Chords and their Resolutions

_Distillation; backed by `chapters/ch03.md`._

This chapter establishes the harmonic foundation of the system by introducing Scale #11 (a descending tritone-based scale), Scale #15 (half-diminished / half-whole-tone with leading-tone function), and Scale #16 (major with raised 4th), along with the principle that all scales can be connected in any order for convergence into a tonal center. The author articulates a radical philosophy of "no wrong notes"—asserting that when factors like key center, Vantage Point, Interval Tonics, Resolution, and the artist's confidence are properly considered, the entire chromatic scale is at the artist's disposal. The system is organized as a 360° compass metaphor, with twenty-four scales and their constituent chords surrounding and "bombarding" a central key center in all directions. Load-bearing terminology introduced includes Scale #11, Scale #15 (and its half-diminished color and leading-tone function), Scale #16 (major with raised 4th), Vantage Point, Interval Tonics, and the geometric concept of tonal convergence.
