# Chapter 4 — Scale Examples of Convergence

_Distillation; backed by `chapters/ch04.md`._

Chapter 4 teaches convergence through scale examples, demonstrating how the final tetrachord functions as a structural bridge and how different modes of the same key (exemplified by Dorian mode and G Jazz melodic minor) can serve as convergence scales. The master's characteristic philosophy emerges in his rejection of prescriptive technique in favor of developing consciousness and awareness, emphasizing that mistakes are not failures but generative opportunities—"a new field, a new octave"—that allow musicians to spontaneously fill moments with prepared readiness. Load-bearing terminology includes "tetrachord" as the convergence mechanism, "mode" as a tool for harmonic flexibility, "enharmonic equivalence," and the central concept of "consciousness" as the governing principle that replaces formulaic instruction.
