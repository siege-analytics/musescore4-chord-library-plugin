# Chapter 8 — Convergence on the Seventh Chord, Minor Chord

_Distillation; backed by `chapters/ch08.md`._

Chapter 8 teaches the foundational principle of chord-on-chord convergence, where main chords can be embellished or approached through secondary chords, typically a dominant-seventh chord with a tritone that can precede the target chord. The master's characteristic move is the use of chromatic tones to resolve the tritone interval inherent in dominant seventh chords, establishing a core technique for applying scales over these chords. The chapter introduces the 'Circle of Gravities' as a systematic map of tritone relationships and establishes practical rules for handling scale-key conflicts, such as using a major-3rd scale to embellish the fifth of a minor chord despite the apparent tonal mismatch. This convergence framework becomes the harmonic scaffolding for understanding how chords relate to and transition between one another in tonal music.
