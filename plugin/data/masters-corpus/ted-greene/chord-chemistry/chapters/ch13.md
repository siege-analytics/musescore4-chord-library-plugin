## Treat chord notes separately

> One of the strongest ways to make chords connect smoothly is to treat the notes of each one
> SEPARATELY.

**Significance (p. 69).** Core voice-leading principle: chords as parallel lines.

## Voice and voice leading defined

> When thinking of the notes of the chords in this manner, each note is called a voice;
> the way that a voice moves from chord to chord will be called voice leading.

**Significance (p. 69).** Defines the chapter's key terminology.

## V7 to I centrality

> One important chord change that may use the principle of voice leading is V7 to I. Believe it or not,
> this one simple chord change is the basis for many, many sounds in music.

**Significance (p. 69).** Asserts V7-I as the fundamental voice-leading laboratory.

## Leading-tone resolution

> The 3rd of the V7 chord moves up to the root of the I chord in each example; this is common
> procedure for V7 to I.

**Significance (p. 69).** States the leading-tone resolution rule explicitly.
