## m(maj7) 7-tone voice leading rule

> when using m? type chords with m7 type chords of the same root, the 7 TONE SHOULD
> MOVE UP 1 FRET or DOWN 1 FRET INTO A ROOT OR 67 TONE.

**Significance (p. 74).** Voice-leading rule for the m(maj7) → m7 transition.

## Chord melody definition

> the term chord melody refers to the playing of the melody AND the chords of a song at
> the same time.

**Significance (p. 75).** Defines the chapter's central technique.

## Raise melody an octave rule

> It is almost always necessary to raise the melody one octave higher for chord melodies.

**Significance (p. 75).** Mechanical precondition: melody must be high enough to sit on top of chord.

## Melody-to-chord-tone correspondence

> The melody usually corresponds to one of the tones of the basic chord that is written above it, or
> to one of the tones of the extensions or altered chords with the same root as the basic chord.

**Significance (p. 75).** Foundational principle linking melody notes to playable chord forms.

## Melody on top

> the melody is always played ABOVE the chord, not in the middle or in
> the bass.

**Significance (p. 75).** Voicing rule positioning melody as soprano.

## Chord every few notes

> When harmonizing a melody (that is, playing a chord melody) in a simple manner, chords are
> generally played every few notes, not on every note.

**Significance (p. 76).** Pedagogical entry-point: sparse chord placement.
