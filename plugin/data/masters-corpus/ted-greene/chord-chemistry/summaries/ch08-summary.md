# Chapter 8 — Chord Reference Charts

_Distillation; backed by `chapters/ch08.md`._

Chapter 8 presents a comprehensive reference guide to chord synonyms and their practical application in substitution. The chapter establishes that chords with different names but identical notes can be used interchangeably, with the primary benefit being the discovery of substitute chords through synonym knowledge. Key rules introduced include the add9+ ≡ 7+ equivalence (eliminating add9+ from the charts), the 9-suspended derivation (9-sus as a 9-chord without the 3rd), and specific omission rules for altered dominants like 13b9. The dominant family receives disproportionate coverage due to containing more chord types than major or minor families. The chapter also addresses augmented chords, which exhibit symmetry and repeat every 4 frets, distinguishing between +11 and 9b5 dominant chords by tone content, and noting that 9b5 chords are preferred over 9#5b5 for their clearer sound and usability, with the exceptional 9#5b5 having only one playable inversion.
