# Chapter 10 — Moving Chords a 4th

_Distillation; backed by `chapters/ch10.md`._

Chapter 10 teaches chord substitution techniques centered on the move-by-4th principle and altered dominants. The master's characteristic moves include partial duration-splitting substitutions (playing a ii-V m7 type chord for part of a dominant's duration, then resolving to the dominant), tritone-sub interpolations that inject a dominant a half-step above before resolution to a chord a 4th higher, and the V-of-V dominant substitution where a dominant's root moves up a 5th. Key terminology includes altered dominant contexts (sounding #5th, b5th, #9th, or combinations), the 7#9 chord as a bluesy direct substitution, and the principle that the top note (soprano voice) is critical in altered voicings. The chapter emphasizes that these are guidelines, not rigid rules, and that altered I7 chords should not open a song (except 7#9); competent improvisers adapt to substitutions heard in real time. The closing directive encourages experimentation with the move-by-4th principle to develop intuition for the guitar's nature.
