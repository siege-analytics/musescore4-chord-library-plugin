# Chapter 13 — Moving Voices (Voice Leading) and Systematic Thinking

_Distillation; backed by `chapters/ch13.md`._

Chapter 13 teaches voice leading — the principle of treating each note of a chord separately as it moves to the next chord — as the fundamental technique for smooth harmonic progressions and modern chord usage. The chapter centers on V7 to I as the foundational voice-leading laboratory, establishing rules such as leading-tone resolution (the 3rd of V7 moving up to the root of I) and permitting structural liberties like 5th omission when voice leading is strong; it further demonstrates how voice leading justifies chromatic approach chords, anticipations, and the ii-V cliché (a dominant chord a 4th above a minor 7th). The master's characteristic move is systematic thinking — breaking down each chord into its tones and analyzing each voice separately — which the chapter argues is the reason every chord in the reference charts earns its place and why any chord becomes usable when properly voice-led. Key terminology introduced includes voice (each note of a chord), voice leading (how a voice moves chord to chord), and transformations like the major 7th rule (lowering one root in a two-root major chord), with special attention to m(maj7) as a chord type that necessitates voice-leading principles.
