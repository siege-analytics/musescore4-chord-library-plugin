# Chapter 14 — Chord Melodies

_Distillation; backed by `chapters/ch14.md`._

Chapter 14 introduces chord melody, the technique of playing a song's melody and chords simultaneously, with the melody always positioned above the chord voicing. The chapter establishes foundational rules: the melody must be raised an octave, should correspond to a tone within the underlying chord or its extensions, and chords are typically played on the first note of each measure rather than on every note. Key enhancements include using relative majors and minors (chords sharing the same key signature) by analyzing the top-note relationship between melody and chord, employing the Picardy-third technique (ending a minor song on a major chord), and back-cycling (preceding each chord with its V chord to create voice-leading movement). The m(maj7)–m7 voice-leading rule specifies that the 7 tone should move up or down one fret into a root or 6/7 tone when transitioning between these chord types.
