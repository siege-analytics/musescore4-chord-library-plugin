# Chapter 15 — Triads

_Distillation; backed by `chapters/ch15.md`._

Chapter 15 introduces triads — any chord containing three notes — and focuses on their close voicing, where tones are arranged as closely as possible on the guitar, with only 12 different forms of close-voiced major triads available for any key. The chapter establishes I-IV as the foundational triad sound requiring immediate memorization across the entire neck. Key techniques include reinterpreting ii-I progressions as ii-V-I for back-cycling opportunities, applying the back-cycling two-chord rule (moving back 2 chords yields the V and V of V), and using chromatic-up and tritone substitutions within back-cycles for harmonic variation. For fast melodies or alternate sounds, interval harmonization below the melody using 3rd, 6th, 10th, 13th, or 17th intervals offers a solution, and the chapter recommends studying piano arrangements for cross-instrument harmonic ideas applicable to guitar.
