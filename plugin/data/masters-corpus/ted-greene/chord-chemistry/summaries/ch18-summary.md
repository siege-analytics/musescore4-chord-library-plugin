# Chapter 18 — Blues Progressions

_Distillation; backed by `chapters/ch18.md`._

Chapter 18 teaches blues progressions as a fundamental form essential to modern music, defining blues within this book as any 12-bar progression anchored structurally by a I chord in bar 1 and a IV chord in bar 5. The master's characteristic move emphasizes the dominance of the dominant 7th family as the defining harmonic color of blues, with 9th chords providing additional bluesy character through extension richness. Core terminology includes the 12-bar form, dominant 7th, voice leading (referenced to section 13), and chord inversions, all selected with top-note attention to maintain smooth melodic lines. The chapter reinforces that right-hand technique matters — some progressions require fingers rather than pick alone — and anchors its approach in the understand-why principle: do what sounds good, but ground it in harmonic analysis and accepted blues practice.
