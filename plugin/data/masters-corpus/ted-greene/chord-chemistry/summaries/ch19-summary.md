Key reinterpretation moves include treating IVm as ii of a new dominant and substituting IIIm7/III7 in measure 7, while the turnaround technique can anchor introductory passages. The master's characteristic move is recognizing harmonic flexibility through substitution: IVm reinterpreted, scale-fragment harmonies deployed, and chromatic voice-leading employed (defined as stepwise motion without skipping). Load-bearing terminology includes turnaround, chromatic motion, scale-fragment substitution, and the distinction between major blues and minor blues forms. A critical rule emerges for fragment substitution: the starting chord matters less than the ending chord, which must align with the harmony being replaced.
