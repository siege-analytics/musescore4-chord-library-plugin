## Definition of chord homonyms

> Webster’s dictionary defines the word homonym as “a word with the same pronunciation as another but with a
> different meaning.” In other words, the two words sound the same but come from different places (like read and reed
> ot principal and principle). In playing through this book, you are going to encounter chord homonyms, that is, chords
> that sound the same but have different origins and names.

**Significance (p. 13).** This formally defines the central concept of the chapter by analogy to linguistic homonyms, establishing the terminological framework for the rest of the discussion.

## Homonym naming rule: context decides

> So, as you can see, the naming of homonyms will be determined by “what is easiest” or Just plain common sense.

**Significance (p. 13).** This states the governing rule for resolving homonym naming ambiguity: harmonic context and common sense dictate which name applies.
