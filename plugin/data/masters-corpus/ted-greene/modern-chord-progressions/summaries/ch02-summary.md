# Chapter 2 — How to Memorize Chords

_Distillation; backed by `chapters/ch02.md`._

Chapter 2 teaches foundational notation and memorization strategies for guitar chords, introducing the O notation (notes plucked after strummed x-notes), establishing chord symbol notation rules (slash for major, + for sharp 5th or 11th, sus for suspended), and explaining that chord naming with x's is intentionally flexible across different contexts. The master's characteristic move is the visual memorization method — relating new chord shapes to known chords — combined with organizing the fretboard into five neck areas as reference points for major and minor chord positions. Load-bearing terminology includes: O notation, x notation, major/sharp/sus chord symbols, and the five-position neck framework. A critical rule clarified here is that fingerings in diagrams apply only to the initial chord sound unless otherwise indicated, ensuring readers don't misapply fingerings to subsequent plucked notes.
