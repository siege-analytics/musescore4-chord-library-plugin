# Chapter 7 — ALTERED DOMINANT SCALES (TYPE 1, 2, and 3): Altered Dominant Chord Types; Chord Forms; Altered Dominant Arpeggios and Runs

_Distillation; backed by `chapters/ch07.md`._

Chapter 7 continues the altered dominant program from Chapter 5 into successive fingerboard areas (Areas 4A, 5, 5A, and a higher-register supplement), dominated by notated exercises with brief marginal prose directives. The substantive prose establishes structural rules specific to the guitar — notably "There won't be any Type 3 - 4A.. it's just the way the instrument works out," a structural boundary preventing students from expecting nonexistent material — and the #9 deployment rule allowing the altered #9 to substitute for or sit alongside the natural 9. Greene's characteristic move here is to mark explicit scope boundaries (a "this type of thing will be discussed in Vol. II" forward-reference) and a standing practice rule that every pattern should be tried an octave higher in the same position. The chapter closes with EXTRA ARPEGGIOS FOR HIGHER REGISTER, extending the altered dominant patterns upward.
