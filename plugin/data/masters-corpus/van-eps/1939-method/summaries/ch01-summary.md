# Chapter 1 — General Instructions

_Distillation; backed by `chapters/ch01.md`._

Chapter 1 establishes the foundational posture, hand mechanics, and practice principles that underpin all subsequent technique in the method. The core physical setup begins with a specific seated position and guitar angle that keeps the instrument stable and the hands free, followed by precise specifications for right-hand wrist position, pick grip (emphasizing oscillation rather than bending), and the signature 2:1 hand-to-elbow speed ratio that maintains consistent hand orientation across the neck. The left hand is governed by equally exacting rules: thumb placement one-third around the neck, arched fingers suspended half an inch above the strings, and a straight wrist maintained as an inviolable mechanical principle. The method introduces key load-bearing concepts including the Solfeggio system (treating each key's tonic as "do" to equalize all twelve keys), the dynamic-axis rule (wrist axis over the highest note for proper chord voicing), the attack-and-exit timing principle (simultaneous pick-strike and fretting-pressure), and the legato execution standard (full note values with pre-forming of the next position during hand motion). Throughout, the master's characteristic move is the reduction of guitar technique to explicit mechanical rules—each gesture stated as a near-absolute, with justifications drawn from biomechanics ("like machinery") and physics (oscillation, axis alignment)—and the definition of the string-set notation system and slow-practice tempo rule that structure how all exercises are to be learned.
