# Chapter 3 — With Minor Chords

_Distillation; backed by `chapters/ch03.md`._

Chapter 3 introduces harmonized minor scale studies in six fingering forms, establishing arpeggio picking and transposition to all keys as the standard treatment, alongside the constant goal of legato playing across all forms. The chapter's characteristic moves include precise left-hand rules—such as finger suspension above strings to prevent slides, the rocker-arm alternation for legato diminished triads, and deadening adjacent strings for open voicing—paired with specific right-hand prescriptions like the up-stroke arc around upper strings and alternate picking on second and fourth triads. Load-bearing terminology includes the harmonized minor scale, broken string sets, contrary motion, diminished symmetry (repeating every four frets), open voicing, rocker-arm technique, and the etude composition method (weekly construction with fixed fingering constraints). Throughout, the chapter preempts common mistakes by normalizing unconventional but correct fingerings and physical discomfort, while emphasizing that exercises yield benefit only when performed without stopping, no matter the tempo.
