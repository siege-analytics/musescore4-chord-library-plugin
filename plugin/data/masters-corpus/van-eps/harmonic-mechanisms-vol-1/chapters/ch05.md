## Upper line motion pattern

> 6ths with upper line motion = 1-5-3-1-—-S-1-3-5-
> Sixths opening up to tenths—

**Significance (p. 115).** Defines the numeric upper-voice pattern (1-5-3-1-5-1-3-5) and the sixth-to-tenth expansion that organize every exercise in the chapter.

## Triads inferred inside the station

> To begin with, there is reference to two of the
> basic triads in each station (bar) as is indicated in the model
> stations. A first inversion triad is inferred with the middle
> note deleted in the first half of each station, and a root
> position triad is present in arpeggio form in the middle to last
> half of each station.

**Significance (p. 115).** Explains the dual-triad construction inside each measure (inferred 1st-inversion + arpeggiated root-position) — the structural backbone of sixths-with-upper-line-motion.

## No barres — walking on fingers

> Occasionally double stops are used, but there are no small or
> grand barres employed for many reasons; the main one being:
> the barre system is not suitable for this notation because it is
> too harplike in nature. In this type of study the tones in each
> station must not be allowed to ring over each other, except
> when indicated by the ties.

**Significance (p. 115).** States the no-barre rule and its rationale: barres produce uncontrolled harp-like ring; this chapter demands clean individual-note articulation.

## Arched finger hammers

> A (walking on the fingers) mechanism is much
> more suitable, in which the fingers are arched like little
> hammers that can provide the so-important individual finger
> control that is so necessary.

**Significance (p. 115).** Names the arched-finger-hammers technique that replaces barring and enables the polyphonic clarity Van Eps demands here.

## Identify the related triad

> When working with sixths it is very important to identify the
> related first or second inversion triad. In all of these studies,
> identify the opening sixth with the basic triad, then the upper
> line arpeggio with the root position id. Also, be aware of
> the major and minor tenths involved.

**Significance (p. 115).** Encodes the mandatory analytic step before playing — every sixth must be mapped back to its parent triad and the tenths it implies.

## Ascending vs descending station numbering

> In the melodic form of these studies the ascending station
> numbers cannot match the descending numbers notationally
> because the scale descends in the relative major key. Note
> that station number one in the ascension becomes station
> number six in the relative major descension, and so it is with
> all of the other descending stations.

**Significance (p. 115).** Critical decoding rule for the melodic-minor exercises — station numbering rotates because descent uses the relative major.

## Reading is not studying

> Reading through exercises does not constitute study
> Studying is an analyzing process—a mental under
> standing and assimilation of a given material. Real
> practice does not begin until the hands and fingers know
> where they belong. Until that time, they are learning

**Significance (p. 115).** Drives a wedge between sight-reading and study, defining 'practice' as beginning only after physical memorization — a recurring Van Eps directive.

## Sustain half and whole notes for full value

> Always sustain the half and whole notes for their full value.

**Significance (p. 116).** Compresses the polyphonic-independence rule into a single sentence: anchor notes must hold their printed duration while other voices move.

## Finger marking convention

> When there are finger markings in one bar and none in the next bar or bars, it usually means that the fingering remains the same or that it is so
> obvious it need not be indicated. In many of these studies fingering will appear in the first bar only. That is done to show the type of finger
> mechanism to be employed. In other words, the fingers to be used may vary from bar to bar, but the “type” of mechanics does not change

**Significance (p. 116).** Decoding key for the notation: a single marked bar establishes a mechanism type, not literal fingering — necessary to read every subsequent exercise.

## Inverted pattern position of tenths

> When pattern is inverted 10ths are formed on the 1st and 4th beat-6ths with upper
> line motion=1-5-3-1-5-1-3-5

**Significance (p. 120).** States how inversion of the upper-line pattern relocates the tenths within the measure — the rule that lets the student transform the printed exercises.
