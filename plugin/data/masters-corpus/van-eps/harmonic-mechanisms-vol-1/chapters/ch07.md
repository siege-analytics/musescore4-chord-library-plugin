## Long reaches via relaxation

> The art of accomplishing long reaches is based on relaxed power control—not a large hand.

**Significance (p. 140).** States Van Eps's core thesis on extended fingerings: reach is a function of relaxed tendon control, not anatomy, which justifies prescribing wide-spread studies to all players.

## Develop reach gradually

> Reach must be developed gradually by patiently working with material that contains extended spreads. It must be practiced on the
> fingerboard under actual playing conditions—not forcing fingers apart with wedges etc.

**Significance (p. 140).** Rejects mechanical stretching aids in favor of in-context practice, defining the only sanctioned route to the extended spreads the method demands.

## Forcing shortens reach

> Forcing the fingers by tensing tightens the tendons and actually shortens the reach.

**Significance (p. 140).** Names the counter-productive failure mode (tensing) that students will instinctively use, framing relaxation as not just preferred but mechanically necessary.

## Chord symbol depends on use

> The G47°35 above is also an E diminished 9th-in other words the symbol for a chord depends on its
> use-its place in a progression etc.

**Significance (p. 141).** Establishes that Van Eps's chord labels are functional/contextual rather than absolute, which is essential for reading the substitution-heavy chromatic triad studies.

## Construct your own exercises

> Construct your own exercises by using portions of these forms, and all the other exercises in these
> books. use 1 note, 2 notes, 4 notes etc. Selected from the beginning, the middle or any place in the
> groups. Practise of this nature helps to increase reach and flexibility. If muscular discomfort is
> noticed, stop, rest the hand. Some discomfort is normal.

**Significance (p. 149).** Authorizes the student to fragment Van Eps's written exercises into custom drills, embedding generative practice as part of the method.

**Significance (p. 152).** Defines the per-measure practice unit and the sustain-the-pedal rule that together give every chromatic triad station its lap-piano character.
