## Sustaining voices through long reaches

> Two voices in motion while sustaining a third, requires a few long reaches. If at first some of the reaches are too great,
> it is permissible to treat the half note as though it were a quarter note—to let go of the half note after sounding the
> triad. However, endeavor to sustain the half note full value.

**Significance (p. 249).** Establishes Van Eps's core multi-voice technique rule: release the sustained voice only as a last resort, because full-value sustain is the load-bearing physical skill the chapter is training.

## Hearing voice motion beats physical execution

> Hearing and understanding the voice motion even though it is slightly disconnected is just as important as being able
> to physically sound all of the notes as written, because the harmonic knowledge is necessary.

**Significance (p. 249).** Defines the method's pedagogical hierarchy where ear/analytical understanding of voice motion is co-equal with the mechanical execution of sustained triads.

## Voice motion mastery as improvisation prerequisite

> The ability to recognize, analyze, and assimilate melodic and harmonic voice motion is very necessary to the
> art of improvisation.

**Significance (p. 249).** Frames the entire Super and Sub series as not merely a chord-drill but as the analytical foundation for improvisation, the book's ultimate target skill.

## Subtle differences across sets

> In all of these studies, it’s very necessary to note the subtle differences in shapes and notation when
> changing from one set to another - some shapes and fingerings will be identical, but the notation is
> not.

**Significance (p. 248).** Warns the student that identical fingerings carry different harmonic/notational meaning across string sets, a load-bearing observation for the 7-string string-set system that drives Van Eps's whole method.

## Transpose to all keys, both minors

> Place in all keys - Major and both Minors - sustain the middle voice - reverse the direction of voice
>
> motion = open to closed

**Significance (p. 253).** Encodes the standing instruction that every closed-to-open voicing study must be transposed across major and both minor forms and run in both directions, making the variation set non-optional.

## Repeating steps for finger control

> Repeating steps of any study over and over is very good for finger control

**Significance (p. 254).** States the practice rule that single steps from any Super/Sub study should be looped as finger-control drills, generalizing the chapter's examples into a practice methodology.

**Significance (p. 255).** Generalizes the finger-team articulation principle beyond harmonic minor, instructing the student to transfer the technique to every scale form the book treats.
