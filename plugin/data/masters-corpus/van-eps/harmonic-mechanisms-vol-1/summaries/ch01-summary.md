# Chapter 1 — Front Matter: Foreword, General Remarks, Chemistry, Selectivity, Voicing and String-Set Charts

_Distillation; backed by `chapters/ch01.md`._

This front-matter chapter is foundational rather than exercise-driven: it installs the four training objectives that engineer the entire seven-string method — independent finger control, multi-thought control, and independent harmonic chromatic notational selectivity (Independent finger and multi-thought control) — and declares the method polyphonic-only, with no single-voice studies because every example carries two or more voices (No single-voice studies). Van Eps codifies the past/present/logical-future/surprise framework the student must apply to every voice in every chord (Voice trajectory in composition) and warns that the book's near-duplicate exercises are deliberate studies of micro-differences rather than redundancy (Similar is not identical). The chapter then lays down the load-bearing physical rules that the rest of the volume depends on: classical thumb-behind-the-neck posture (Thumb placement on neck), the economy 'fatigue factor' principle of pressing only the strings that sound (Fatigue factor), four-finger walking-action alternation with the corollary 'don't repeat fingers' (Walking action / alternation principle; Don't repeat fingers), and 'standing on notes' — the arched-fingertip + barre combination that locks down sustained tones while other fingers move and which enables the lap-piano polyphonic independence Van Eps is famous for (Standing on notes). Finally it introduces the central organizing concepts of the method — 'selectivity' as chromatic fingerboard control with mind leading hands (Selectivity defined), the tonal-chemistry/timbre-density rationale that justifies heavy seven-string bass under light upper triads (Tonal chemistry / register density), the aphorism that arpeggios and chords are frozen/broken versions of each other (Arpeggios are frozen chords), and the spacing-for-audibility rule that inner voices need room to be heard (Inner voices need room).
