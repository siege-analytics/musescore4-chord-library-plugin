# Chapter 2 — The Mighty Triads: First/Second Inversion and Root-Position Triad Scales (Major, Harmonic Minor, Melodic Minor)

_Distillation; backed by `chapters/ch02.md`._

Chapter 2 launches the actual exercise program by enthroning the triad as the universal substrate of every study in the book and then running 1st-inversion, 2nd-inversion, and root-position triad scales chromatically through the major, harmonic-minor, and melodic-minor systems (Triad as central unit; Triad names defined). Van Eps ties this directly to selectivity by declaring chromatics 'the very life line of music' (Chromatics as life line) and routes all transposition through the descending cycle of fifths rather than chromatic key-shifts, both for fingering variety and because Western harmony is itself gravitationally pulled downward by fifths (Descending cycle of fifths preferred; Gravitational descending harmony). The chapter installs several load-bearing voicing doctrines that recur throughout the volume: the outer-voice-anchor / deletion rule — the major or minor sixth between top and bottom of a 1st-inversion triad is so strong that the middle can be deleted, compressing six-note compounded triads into four-note hand-grips (Compounding triads / deletion rule); the 'hint/suggestion of a tone' inferred-note device for implying notes the hand can't physically grip (Hint / suggestion of a tone); overtone-aware voicing that justifies heavy bass against light upper triads (Overtones in voicing); and 'delayed coupling,' the temporal-compounding trick where rapid successive partial chords are stitched together by the ear into a single larger harmony — the engine of Van Eps's lap-piano illusion (Delayed coupling). The chapter also codifies the satellite-key duplication pattern as a transposition shortcut (Satellite key relationships), warns that intentionally awkward fingerings are pedagogical features for agility rather than errors (Intentionally awkward fingerings), and concedes the structural limit that three-voice work cannot use full four-finger alternation — partial two-finger alternation is the workaround (Three-voice alternation limit).
