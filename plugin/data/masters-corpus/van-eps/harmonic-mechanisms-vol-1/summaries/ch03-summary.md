# Chapter 3 — Mixed Minors

_Distillation; backed by `chapters/ch03.md`._

This short chapter introduces the 'mixed minors' device — combining melodic-minor ascent in one voice against harmonic-minor descent in another, then reversing the roles so the harmonic form lives in the middle voice while the melodic form lives in the lower voice (Mixed minors definition). The chapter's exercise architecture is the matrix of all three triad inversions (1st, 2nd, root) crossed with melodic-up against harmonic-down (Mixed minors full inversion cycle), and Van Eps directs the student to extend every printed example across the full fingerboard rather than treat the page as the assignment (Extend full range). Two characteristic Van Eps mechanisms surface here: deliberate register-shifts when a voice-pair exhausts physical range (Register change device), and the discipline that substitute fingerings exist for very long second-interval spreads but the student should attempt the original wide spread first as a stretch builder (Substitute fingerings for wide spreads). The chapter closes by extending the chapter-1 fingerboard-mapping discipline downward into the inner voices: practical duplicate voicings must be hunted in the middle and lower voices, not just the upper line (Practical duplicates and inner-voice search).
