# Chapter 5 — Sixths with Upper-Line Motion (Full Range, All Three Modes)

_Distillation; backed by `chapters/ch05.md`._

Chapter 5 takes the sixths developed in chapter 4 and animates them with a fixed numeric upper-voice pattern (1-5-3-1-5-1-3-5) while the sixths themselves open out into tenths, generating sixths-with-upper-line-motion exercises across all three modes and the full range of the instrument (Upper line motion pattern; Inverted pattern position of tenths). Each measure ('station') is built as a dual-triad construction: an inferred first-inversion triad with the middle note deleted in the first half, followed by an arpeggiated root-position triad in the second half — and Van Eps demands the analytic step of mapping every sixth back to its parent 1st- or 2nd-inversion triad and the major/minor tenths it implies before playing (Triads inferred inside the station; Identify the related triad). The chapter installs the chapter-specific physical doctrine that there are no barres, small or grand: the barre is too harplike and lets tones ring uncontrollably, so individual notes must be sounded by 'arched-finger hammers' that walk on the fingers for clean polyphonic articulation (No barres — walking on fingers; Arched finger hammers), and anchor half- and whole-notes must always be sustained for their full printed value while the other voices move (Sustain half and whole notes for full value). Two notational decoding rules are made load-bearing for reading every subsequent exercise: a single marked bar establishes a mechanism type rather than literal fingering (Finger marking convention), and in the melodic-form studies the ascending station numbers do not match the descending ones because the descent uses the relative major — station 1 ascending becomes station 6 descending (Ascending vs descending station numbering). The chapter closes the loop on study discipline with Van Eps's wedge between sight-reading and study: 'reading through exercises does not constitute study,' and real practice does not begin until the hands have memorized where they belong (Reading is not studying).
