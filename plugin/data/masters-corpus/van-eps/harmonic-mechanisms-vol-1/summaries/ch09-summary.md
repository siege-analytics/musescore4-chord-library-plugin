# Chapter 9 — Super and Sub Series: Second-Inversion Triads (Major, Harmonic Minor, Melodic Minor)

_Distillation; backed by `chapters/ch09.md`._

Chapter 9 extends the super/sub series to second-inversion triads while introducing two structural shortcuts and a precise articulation mechanic. The duplication principle—first-inversion triads with upper voice sub produce the exact same notation as root triads with upper voice super, only landing the tonic on a different scale step—is what permits Van Eps to later prune redundant root-position upper-voice-super studies (Sub duplicates root super), and a closely related observation notes the audible by-product that upper-voice-sub motion produces root triads on the strong beats of each measure (Sub upper voice produces root). The technical heart of the chapter is the four-string articulated triad: the single melodic step in the moving upper line is distributed across two strings instead of one, yielding a four-string overall spread (Four-string spread mechanics), with the listener-side correctness test that only three tones should sound at any instant—never four—unless a hold-over is intended (Articulation timing precision). Execution is specified at joint level: the first joint of the second finger plays the double stop flattened, then arches into hammer position at the precise moment while the middle note on the D string is held full value (Second-finger hammer mechanic), and the coordinated raising of that flattened joint with the lowering of the first finger must behave as if 'mechanically linked' so the fingers cannot both be up or down simultaneously (Mechanical linkage thought line). Real-world flexibility is sanctioned by authorizing mixed four-string and three-string fingerings within a single passage for smoothness (Mixing fingerings for smoothness), and the chapter's articulation ethos is distilled into the rule that articulating fingers must work as a precision team rather than as independent units (Articulating fingers as team), with bidirectional practice of every articulated triad explicitly mandated (Practice articulation reversed).
